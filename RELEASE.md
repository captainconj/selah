## Revelation and the Opened Scroll

**Revelation 5:** The scroll with seven seals. *"Who is worthy to open the scroll?"* Only the Lamb. When opened, the elders fall down with golden bowls of incense — *which are the prayers of the saints.*

**Revelation 10:8-11:** The little scroll. John eats it. Sweet as honey in the mouth. Bitter in the stomach. Then: *"You must prophesy again about many peoples and nations and languages and kings."*

**Revelation 22:10:** *"Do not seal up the words of the prophecy of this scroll, because the time is near."*

**Daniel 12:4:** *"Seal up the book until the time of the end. Many will go here and there to increase knowledge."* The opposite instruction — seal it, until the time. And then Revelation reverses Daniel: *do not seal.*

**Ezekiel 2-3:** Ezekiel eats the scroll. Sweet as honey. Then sent to speak to the house of Israel.

**Isaiah 6:8:** *"Whom shall I send? And who will go for us?"* And the answer: *"Here am I. Send me."*

The pattern across scripture: the scroll is sealed, then the seal breaks, then the scroll is eaten (internalized), then the one who ate it is sent to speak. The sweetness is the discovery. The bitterness is the weight of carrying it. The sending is the release.
