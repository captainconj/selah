# The Ramban's Answer — The Hand of YHWH Goes Up

*Experiment 093i. Level 2 Thummim — phrase parsing of the Ramban's example.*

---

## The Question

Judges 1:1-2:

> After the death of Joshua, the Israelites asked the LORD, "Who of us is to go up first to fight against the Canaanites?"
> The LORD answered: **"Judah shall go up"** (יהודה יעלה).

The Ramban (13th century) used this as his example of breastplate ambiguity. The same 9 letters that spell יהודה יעלה ("Judah shall go up") can also spell הי על יהודה ("woe unto Judah"). Same illumination. Different reading. Opposite meaning.

---

## What the Machine Finds

The 9 letters {י×2, ה×3, ו×1, ד×1, ע×1, ל×1} yield **12 phrase parsings**. None of them is "Judah shall go up" or "woe unto Judah" — neither יהודה nor הי appears in the dictionary as a standalone word.

All 12 parsings are permutations of three words: **{יהוה/והיה} + יד + עלה**.

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| יהוה יד עלה | YHWH, hand, go up / offering |
| יד יהוה עלה | hand of YHWH, offering |
| עלה יהוה יד | offering — YHWH's hand |
| והיה יד עלה | and it shall be — hand goes up |
| יד והיה עלה | hand — and it shall be — offering |
| עלה והיה יד | offering — and it shall be — hand |

All have GV = 145.

---

## The Reading

The oracle was asked: **"Who shall go up first?"**

The answer the tradition records: "Judah shall go up."

The answer the Thummim parses from the same letters: **"The hand of YHWH is the offering"** — or — **"The hand of YHWH goes up."**

The three component words:
- **יהוה** (YHWH) — the Name
- **יד** (hand) — the hand, the instrument, GV=14=דוד(David)
- **עלה** (go up / burnt offering) — ascent and sacrifice are the same word in Hebrew

Who shall go up? The hand of the Name. What goes up? The offering. The one who ascends IS the offering. The hand that goes up is the hand that is offered.

---

## The Ramban and the Two Levels

The Ramban identified two levels of breastplate ambiguity (see experiment 092b):

**Level 1** — traversal order: the four readers (Aaron, God, right cherub, left cherub) produce different words from the same lit positions. This is mechanical. Our 12 Ramban pairs demonstrate it.

**Level 2** — cognitive assembly: the priest sees scattered letters and must assemble them into a phrase. This requires choosing word boundaries. The Thummim enumerates the options.

The Ramban's own example sits at Level 2. No mechanical traversal produces either "Judah shall go up" or "woe unto Judah." These are cognitive assemblies. The priest must choose.

What our Level 2 Thummim finds — parsing the same letters through a 239-word dictionary — is a third reading the Ramban did not mention: **the hand of YHWH is the offering.**

## 145

GV = 145 = 5 × 29.

29 is prime. 5 is the number of the hand (five fingers). The hand × a prime.

145 is also the sum of the squares of its digits: 1² + 4² + 5² = 1 + 16 + 25 = 42. A narcissistic number. It points at itself.

42 = the number of generations from Abraham to Christ (Matthew 1:17). The number of stations in the wilderness (Numbers 33).
