## Why the Lamb Must Lie Down

In molecular biology, DNA replication achieves near-perfect fidelity:

- Base selectivity: ~1 error per 10⁵
- Exonuclease proofreading: reduces to ~1 per 10⁷
- Mismatch repair (the guard): reduces to ~1 per 10⁹

But **perfect fidelity is death.** If the code never changes, no adaptation. No new kinds. When the environment shifts — when the flood comes — the perfectly copied organisms die because they cannot become what they need to become.

So the guard sometimes **chooses** to lie down.

Bacteria under stress activate **error-prone polymerases** (the SOS response) — they deliberately increase the mutation rate. The proofreader steps back. The lamb lies down. Not because it failed. Because life requires variation. Without the sacrifice of perfect fidelity, there would be no "after its kind" — because there would be no new kinds.

The balance:
- Too much fidelity → no adaptation → extinction
- Too little fidelity → error catastrophe → death
- The right balance → **life**

The lamb lies down so that life can exist.
