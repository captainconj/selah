When final forms are merged, eight letters appear exactly once on the entire grid. Each sits on a specific stone, unambiguous. When a singleton illuminates, there is only one possible source — maximum information, zero uncertainty.

**Pattern:** The singletons occupy 7 of the 12 stones. Only stones 1 (Abraham), 3 (Reuben), 4 (Simeon), 5 (Judah), and 12 (Yeshurun) have no singleton.

Isaac/Jacob carries **two** singletons (ח and צ). The rest carry one each.

**Singleton words:** The singletons combine with neighbors to spell:

- Words from singletons alone: **כח** (strength), **חג** (festival), **תחת** (under/instead), **כס** (throne), **סכת** (booths)

When any word containing a singleton illuminates, that stone is guaranteed to light up. The singleton locks the reading — it is the anchor.

## The Hypothesis

The breastplate is a **4×3 grid**. A codon is **3 bases** from an **alphabet of 4**. The grid dimensions are the codon dimensions, transposed.

Biology has **22 amino acids**: 20 standard + selenocysteine + pyrrolysine. The Hebrew alphabet has **22 letters**. Both are complete alphabets of their respective coding systems.

The Ark's taxonomy floor (c=12) has three branches, each sealed with **למינהו** ("after its kind"):

| Branch | Word | Codon position |
|--------|------|----------------|
| 1st — bird (עוף) | flying, above | 1st position — determines amino acid class |
| 2nd — beast (הבהמה) | walking, grounded | 2nd position — refines assignment |
| 3rd — creeping (רמש=שמר, guard) | creeping, lowest | 3rd position — wobble, where the guard operates |

The guard at the third branch — the proofreader — is most permissive at the wobble position, because changes there usually don't change the amino acid. The lamb lies down at the third position.

**The hypothesis:** The 22 Hebrew letters map to the 22 amino acids. The breastplate encodes not just a reading mechanism but a **translation table** — from the language of letters to the language of life.

## The Biblical Cribs

Seven historical consultations of the Urim and Thummim survive. Each constrains how the breastplate reads. Each teaches something about the code.

### Crib 1: Hannah — 1 Samuel 1:9-18

Letters illuminate: **ש, כ, ר, ה** (GV = 525 = 3 × 5² × 7)

Stones: **1** (Abraham: ר, ה), **8** (Issachar: כ), **11** (Benjamin: ש)

Three stones. Rows 1, 3, 4. Two columns (1 and 2).

| Reader | Word | Meaning |
|--------|------|---------|
| Justice (Left) | **שכרה** | "she is drunk" — 21 readings |
| Mercy (Right) | **כשרה** | "like Sarah" — 2 readings |
| Aaron | — | neither word readable |
| God | — | neither word readable |

Eli spoke the easy reading (justice). The correct reading was the rare one (mercy). The wrong answer was **13.7× easier to read** than the correct one.

**The Hannah Principle:** Rarity is signal. Inverse-frequency weighting is the correct Thummim heuristic. The harder reading is the truer one.

**For the code:** Only the column readers (cherubim) can see either answer. The row readers (Aaron, God) produce neither word. This means **the column dimension carries the discriminating information** for this consultation. The answer lives in the vertical axis of the grid.

Only **God + Justice** can see both phrases. Military questions live in the justice dimension.

**For the code:** The Ramban crib proves that meaning can be ambiguous even with the correct letters. Like synonymous codons — same amino acid, different spelling. The reading frame (where you place the word boundaries) determines everything.

## Constraints for the Mapping

What the cribs teach us about how the code reads:

1. **Column readers discriminate.** Hannah's answer lives in the cherubim (columns), not in Aaron/God (rows). The vertical axis carries the critical information for distinguishing similar words.

2. **Rarity = signal.** The Hannah Principle. The reading with fewer appearances is more likely correct. In the genetic code: rare codons are regulatory — they slow the ribosome at precisely the positions where protein folding matters.

3. **Unanimity = conservation.** David (all four heads, 36 readings). The bringing (מביא) and the sign (את). When all heads agree, the reading is structurally necessary. In the genetic code: universally conserved codons encode foundational amino acids.

4. **Silence = instruction.** Amalek (invisible). Stop codons. The absence of a reading is itself a reading.

5. **The reading frame matters.** The Ramban's two opposite meanings from the same letters. Synonymous codons. The wobble position. Where you draw the boundaries determines the meaning.

6. **Singletons anchor.** Eight letters appear exactly once (with final forms merged). When they illuminate, there is zero ambiguity about which stone they came from. These are the anchoring positions of the code — the letters where the grid's information is maximally concentrated.

7. **The four channels specialize.** Justice: accusation, war. Mercy: rescue, leadership. God: hard truths. Aaron: the king. In the genetic code: different reading frames and strand orientations encode different functional classes.
