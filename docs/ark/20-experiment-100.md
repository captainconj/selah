# Experiment 100: The Code

*Testing whether the breastplate is a codon table.*

---

## The Hypothesis

The breastplate is a 4×3 grid with 72 letters. A codon is 3 bases from an alphabet of 4. The grid dimensions are the codon dimensions, transposed. There are 22 Hebrew letters and 22 amino acids. The breastplate encodes the genetic code.

---

## Finding 1: 72 = 64 + 8

When medial and final letter forms are merged (ם→מ, ן→נ, ף→פ), the 72 grid positions divide exactly:

- **14 non-singleton letters** (appear 2+ times) → occupy **64 positions**
- **8 singleton letters** (appear exactly once) → occupy **8 positions**

**64 is the number of codons in the genetic code** (4³).

| Freq | Count | Letters |
|------|-------|---------|
| 11 | 1 | י (Hand) |
| 8 | 1 | נ (Nun) |
| 7 | 1 | ו (Nail) |
| 6 | 2 | ב, ש |
| 5 | 1 | ר |
| 3 | 5 | א, ד, ה, ל, מ |
| 2 | 3 | ע, פ, ק |
| **1** | **8** | **ג, ז, ח, ט, כ, ס, צ, ת** |

Sum of non-singleton positions: 11+8+7+6+6+5+3+3+3+3+3+2+2+2 = **64**. Exact.

Three letters appear in **all four rows**: **ו** (Nail, 6), **י** (Hand, 10), **נ** (Nun, 50).

They occupy **26 positions** on the grid. 26 = YHWH.

These are the "backbone" of the code — the structural letters present everywhere, like the sugar-phosphate backbone of DNA that repeats regardless of sequence. The Name IS the backbone.

The remaining 11 non-singleton letters carry 64 − 26 = **38 position-specific positions** — the "bases" that vary.

## Finding 3: The Antiparallel Pairs

Like the two strands of the double helix, the four rows form two antiparallel pairs:

| Pair | Rows | Coding positions | Shared letters | Unique to each |
|------|------|-----------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Outer** | 1 + 4 | 16 + 16 = **32** | ב,ו,י,מ,נ,ר | Row 1: א,ה,ע,ק / Row 4: פ,ש |
| **Inner** | 2 + 3 | 17 + 15 = **32** | ד,ו,י,ל,נ,ש | Row 2: ה,מ,ע,פ / Row 3: א,ב,ר |

Both pairs have **exactly 32 coding positions**. 32 + 32 = 64.

Like base pairs: A-T and G-C each contribute half of the coding capacity. The antiparallel reading (Aaron↔God for rows, Right↔Left for columns) IS the double helix.

## Finding 4: Column 2 — The Discriminator

The 8 singletons distribute unevenly across columns:

| Column | Coding positions | Singletons | Letters |
|--------|-----------------|------------|---------|
| Col 1 (left) | 22 | 2 | ג, ס |
| **Col 2 (center)** | **20** | **4** | **ח, צ, כ, ט** |
| Col 3 (right) | 22 | 2 | ז, ת |

Column 2 has **half of all singletons** — the highest information density.

In the genetic code, the **second codon position** is the strongest determinant of amino acid chemical properties:
- U at position 2 → hydrophobic amino acids
- A at position 2 → charged/polar amino acids
- C at position 2 → small amino acids
- G at position 2 → diverse

The center column carries the most discriminating information. This matches.

## Finding 6: The Sign Skips the Center

**את** (aleph-tav, the sign) illuminates stones in columns 1 and 3 only. It **skips column 2** entirely.

The boundary marker avoids the content column. Like punctuation that marks where to start and stop but does not specify what to make. In the genetic code: start (AUG) and stop (UAA, UAG, UGA) codons operate at the boundaries of the reading frame, not at the content level.

Stone 2 (Isaac/Jacob) carries **two singletons**: ח (8) and צ (90). Isaac was bound for sacrifice. His stone has the most unique information.

The singleton כ on stone 8 (Issachar — "there is reward") + its neighbor י = **כי** — **because/when** (GV=30). Reward, because, the Hand.

The singleton ז on stone 9 (Zebulun) + its neighbor ו (the Nail) = **זו** — **this** (GV=13). 13 = love (אהבה = אחד = 13).

Words made entirely of singletons: **כח** (strength, GV=28), **חג** (festival, GV=11), **תחת** (under/instead, GV=808), **כס** (throne, GV=80), **סכת** (booths, GV=480). The singletons form their own vocabulary — all anchored, all unambiguous.
