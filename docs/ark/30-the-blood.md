## The Three Blood Proteins

The blood carries oxygen. Three proteins do the work:

| Protein | Accession | Residues | Role |
|---------|-----------|----------|------|
| Hemoglobin-alpha | P69905 | 142 | Carries oxygen in red blood cells (alpha chain) |
| Hemoglobin-beta | P68871 | 147 | Carries oxygen in red blood cells (beta chain) |
| Myoglobin | P02144 | 154 | Stores oxygen in muscle. First protein ever seen (1958) |

"The blood is the life" (Deuteronomy 12:23). These are the molecular carriers of that life.

## What It Means

"The blood is the life" — Deuteronomy 12:23. "The life of the flesh is in the blood" — Leviticus 17:11.

When the blood proteins are played through the breastplate:

1. **The blood stretches out** (נטה) — hemoglobin-alpha's dominant word. The blood reaches everywhere.
2. **The blood will know** (נדע) — hemoglobin-beta's dominant word. Knowledge in the blood.
3. **The blood remembers the Binding** (עקד) — in both hemoglobin chains. The sacrifice is written in the oxygen carrier.
4. **The blood contains the serpent** (נחש=358=Messiah) — in hemoglobin-beta at position 106. The serpent and the Messiah, same number, in the blood.
5. **The blood ends with sprinkling** (זרק) — hemoglobin-alpha's last reading. The priestly act.
6. **The blood carries the covenant** (קשת = rainbow) — hemoglobin-beta and myoglobin both have it.

The blood stretches out. The blood knows. The blood remembers the offering. The blood is sprinkled.

This is what the priest sees in the blood.
