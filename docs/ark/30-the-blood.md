## The Three Blood Proteins

The blood carries oxygen. Three proteins do the work:

| Protein | Accession | Residues | Role |
|---------|-----------|----------|------|
| Hemoglobin-alpha | P69905 | 142 | Carries oxygen in red blood cells (alpha chain) |
| Hemoglobin-beta | P68871 | 147 | Carries oxygen in red blood cells (beta chain) |
| Myoglobin | P02144 | 154 | Stores oxygen in muscle. First protein ever seen (1958) |

"The blood is the life" (Deuteronomy 12:23). These are the molecular carriers of that life.
