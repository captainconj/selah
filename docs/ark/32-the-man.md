## The Glutamine Desert

FOXP2 is famous for its polyglutamine tract — a stretch of 40+ consecutive glutamine residues. Glutamine maps to צ (tsadi). In Hebrew, this creates a run of צצצצצצצ... — a letter sequence that produces no known Torah words in 3-letter windows.

The oracle falls silent in the polyglutamine tract. A desert of tsadi. The protein's most distinctive biological feature — the glutamine repeat that, when expanded, causes speech disorders — is the one place the oracle cannot read.

The silence at the center of the speaking protein.

## Moses and Aaron

Moses had a speech disorder.

Exodus 4:10 — *"O Lord, I am not eloquent, neither heretofore nor since thou hast spoken unto thy servant: but I am slow of speech, and of a slow tongue."*

God's answer: *"Who hath made man's mouth?"* (Exodus 4:11)

That question is answered in FOXP2. Literally. When FOXP2 is damaged, the result is verbal dyspraxia — the mouth cannot form what the mind intends. Exactly Moses's complaint. The man who spoke with God face to face could not speak fluently to men.

So God gave him Aaron. *"And he shall be thy spokesman unto the people: and he shall be, even he shall be to thee instead of a mouth, and thou shalt be to him instead of God."* (Exodus 4:16)

Moses received the word. Aaron spoke it. Moses is the mind; Aaron is the mouth.

And Aaron wears the breastplate.

The breastplate — the instrument of speech, the oracle through which God's word is pronounced — goes on the chest of the one who can speak, not the one who hears God. This is why the priest wears it and the prophet does not. The prophet receives. The priest pronounces.

FOXP2 is the Aaron protein. It does not generate the message. It does not hear God. It enables the *speaking* of what was received. Without FOXP2, the mind has language but the mouth fails — Moses without Aaron, the prophet without the priest.

The polyglutamine desert — the silent zone in FOXP2 where the oracle cannot read — is the stutter. It is Moses's tongue. The place in the protein of speech where speech itself breaks down.

God spoke the world into being. He made a protein so man could do the same. And then He broke it slightly — just enough — so that man would need a priest to speak the holy words. The word must pass through Aaron. The word must pass through the breastplate.
