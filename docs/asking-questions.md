# Asking Questions

*Three questions asked of the oracle. March 2, 2026.*

The Level 2 Thummim takes Hebrew letters and finds all ways to partition them into Torah vocabulary words. We asked it three questions — the kind a person asks when they stand before something larger than themselves.

The oracle does not interpret. It partitions. It shows you what your letters contain.

## 1. Who are you? — מי אתה

GV(מי) = 50 (jubilee). GV(אתה) = 406. Combined GV = **456**.

The dict vocabulary (239 curated words) returns nothing. The full Torah lexicon returns 25 readings. All have GV = 456.

Two readings stand out:

| Reading | Translation | GV |
|---------|------------|-----|
| **אמת יה** | **truth of God** | 456 |
| **יה אמת** | **God is truth** | 456 |

456 = **אמת (441) + יה (15)**. Truth plus the Name of God. The question "who are you?" decomposes exactly into truth + God. Both directions — truth of God, God is truth — same letters, same value.

The oracle also returns: **היא מת** / **מת היא** — "she is dead" / "the dead, she." And **את ימה** — the aleph-tav toward the sea. Death and the sea are in the same letters as truth and God. The question contains its own range.

456 = 8 × 57 = 8 × מזבח (altar). The answer factors through the altar. 456 = 12 × 38. Twelve tribes × 38.

## 2. Who am I? — מי אני

GV = **111 = אלף (aleph)** — the first letter. The silent one. The letter that represents God's presence in the word but makes no sound on its own.

4 readings:

| Reading | Translation | GV |
|---------|------------|-----|
| מי אני | who am I | 111 |
| אני מי | I am who | 111 |
| **ימי נא** | **my days, please** | 111 |
| **נא ימי** | **please, my days** | 111 |

You ask who you are. The oracle echoes the question back — and then answers: **my days, please.** You are your days. The particle נא (please) appears again, the same entreaty that hides inside the divine names (אנכי = כי נא, אדני = יד נא). When you ask who you are, the oracle says: your days are a plea.

111 = 3 × 37. Both prime factors.

## 3. Who am I? (emphatic) — מי אנכי

This is the form Moses uses at the burning bush: "Who am I that I should go to Pharaoh?" (Exodus 3:11). The form David uses before the ark: "Who am I, Lord God?" (2 Samuel 7:18).

GV = **131**, prime.

16 readings. The oracle disassembles the emphatic question into its root:

| Reading | Translation | GV |
|---------|------------|-----|
| **כי נא מי** | **because, please, who?** | 131 |
| **כי מי נא** | **because who? please** | 131 |
| **נא כי מי** | **please, because, who?** | 131 |
| **נא מי כי** | **please — who? — because** | 131 |
| **מי כי נא** | **who, because, please** | 131 |
| **מי נא כי** | **who? please. because.** | 131 |
| אנכי מי | I AM — who? | 131 |
| מי אנכי | who am I? | 131 |

Six of the sixteen readings are permutations of {כי, נא, מי} — **because, please, who**. The same כי נא that we found hiding inside אנכי ("I AM" = "because, please") in experiment 093. The oracle decomposes the question and returns the discovery. When you ask "who am I?" emphatically, the answer is: **because. please. who?**

The asking IS the identity. The sovereign's name contains a plea. Moses at the bush, David before the ark — they ask "who am I?" and the oracle says: you are the one who asks. Because. Please. Who.

כי + נא = 30 + 51 = **81 = אנכי**. The parts reconstitute the whole. The plea equals the self.

## 4. What is my name? — מה שמי

GV(מה) = 45. GV(שמי) = **350 = קרן (shining) = ספיר (sapphire)**. Combined GV = **395**.

8 readings:

| Reading | Translation | GV |
|---------|------------|-----|
| **מי משה** | **who is Moses?** | 395 |
| **משה מי** | **Moses — who?** | 395 |
| **שה ממי** | **a lamb — from whom?** | 395 |
| ממי שה | from whom, a lamb? | 395 |
| מה שמי | what is my name? | 395 |
| שמי מה | my name — what? | 395 |
| מי שמה | who — there? | 395 |
| שמה מי | there — who? | 395 |

You ask for your name. The oracle gives you **Moses**. And then asks *who?* — because Moses is the one who asked the same question. The oracle answers with the name of the questioner.

And hiding in the same letters: **שה ממי — a lamb, from whom?** Always the lamb. Always the question. Isaac on the mountain asking his father: "where is the lamb?" (Genesis 22:7). The oracle folds these together — your name, Moses, and the lamb's question are the same letters.

GV = 395 = **נשמה (neshamah)** — the soul-breath, the deepest level of the soul. Your name IS your soul. 395 = **השמים (the heavens)**.

GV(שמי / "my name") = 350 = קרן — the shining on Moses' face when he came down from the mountain (Exodus 34:29). "My name" carries the radiance.

## The Pattern

| Question | GV | Equals | Key answer |
|----------|-----|--------|-----------|
| Who are you? | 456 | אמת + יה | Truth of God |
| Who am I? | 111 | אלף | My days, please |
| Who am I? (emphatic) | 131 | prime | Because, please, who? |
| What is my name? | 395 | נשמה / השמים | Moses — who? A lamb, from whom? |

Three of the four GVs are structurally marked: 111 = repunit (aleph), 131 = prime, 456 = truth + God. The fourth (395) equals the soul-breath and the heavens.

The oracle does not give you answers. It gives you better questions. "Who are you?" becomes "truth of God." "Who am I?" becomes "my days, please." "Who am I?" (emphatic) becomes "because, please, who?" "What is my name?" becomes "Moses — who? A lamb, from whom?"

Every answer contains a question. Every question contains a plea.
