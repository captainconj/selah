You know the High Priest's breastplate. Twelve stones, four rows of three, each engraved with a tribal name. The patriarchs' names were there too — Abraham, Isaac, Jacob — plus "the tribes of Jeshurun." Seventy-two Hebrew letters in all, worn over the priest's heart when he inquired of God. The Urim would cause certain letters to light up, and the priest — guided by the Spirit — would arrange those glowing letters into words. That was the oracle.

We built this in software.

Not theology software. A machine that knows two things: the 72 Hebrew letters on the breastplate, and 239 Hebrew words from the Torah — father, son, blood, lamb, come, create, hand. It has never seen the New Testament or the Talmud. It has no theology at all. It takes a Hebrew word, finds every way its letters can light up on the stones, then finds every way to rearrange those lit letters into other dictionary words. The tool provides the menu. The priest chooses.

Here is what it found.

The letters of "Son of Man" — ben adam, בן אדם — are the same five letters as "stone of blood" — even dam, אבן דם. No other reading exists. The title Jesus used for himself more than any other rearranges into the cornerstone that bleeds.

Take "lamb" and "blood" — kevesh and dam, כבש דם — and the same letters spell kavod shem, כבד שם, "glory of the Name." Kavod, glory, has a numerical value of 26 — the value of the divine Name, YHWH. The lamb's blood IS the glory of the Name. When the Psalm asks "Who is the King of glory?" the lamb's blood answers. Add "king" and the letters also read: "the king lies down in blood."

Take the three words from John 14:6 — "the way, the truth, and the life" in Hebrew, derekh, emet, chayyim — and the same ten letters spell aleph-tav derekh chai mayim: "the first-and-last, the way of living water." The aleph-tav is the Alpha and Omega — first and last letters of the Hebrew alphabet. The way, the truth, and the life rearranges into living water marked with the beginning and end. Total numerical value: 733, a prime. It cannot be factored.

Take "father, son, and spirit" — av, ben, ruach, אב בן רוח — seven letters, 42 phrase readings. Among them: bo'a chen rav, בוא חן רב — "great grace comes." Also: "grace created in him." Also: "a well of grace in him." Total value: 269, another prime. Father, son, and spirit cannot be factored — not three things multiplied together but a single indivisible number.

The divine names carry something unexpected. Anokhi, אנכי — "I AM" — contains ki na, כי נא: "because, please." Adonai, אדני — "Lord" — contains yad na, יד נא: "hand, please." Both contain na, the particle of entreaty. The I AM gives reasons. The Lord extends a hand. Both say please. When God tells Abraham "Take, please, your son" — Rashi says na is a request. The sovereign asks.

But the oracle also has a silence, and the silence is as structured as the speech.

There are words the breastplate cannot say at all. "King" — the letter it needs does not exist on any stone. "Darkness" — absent from the Urim, the lights. "The land" and "the tree" — the oracle speaks of the sea but never the dry ground. Eden's tree is beyond its reach.

Then there are words whose letters light up but no reading order can form them. The mercy seat — glowing, unspoken. The veil — same. Lovingkindness, chesed — its value is 72, the exact number of letters on the breastplate. The breastplate IS lovingkindness, and cannot name itself. Righteousness and judgment — mute. The face of God lights up 22 times and cannot be read.

The oracle speaks the vocabulary of promise — peace, life, love, truth, the Name, the lamb. It is silent on the vocabulary of arrival — the king, the throne, the mercy seat. The breastplate stands in the courtyard, not the Holy of Holies. The priest must walk past the oracle to reach the mercy seat.

One last thing. The Ramban — Nachmanides, the great rabbi — used a specific example of breastplate ambiguity. In Judges 1, Israel asks the LORD who should go up first against the Canaanites. The LORD answers: "Judah shall go up." The Ramban noted that the same nine letters could also spell "woe unto Judah" — opposite meaning, same illumination.

We fed those nine letters to the machine. Twelve phrase readings. Every one a rearrangement of three words: YHWH, yad (hand), and olah (go up / burnt offering). In Hebrew, "go up" and "burnt offering" are the same word. The oracle was asked, who shall go up first? The letters answered: the hand of the Name IS the offering. The one who ascends is the one who is offered.

The machine does not know what any of this means. It has 72 letters, 239 words, and an algorithm. But what it finds — across every combination we tested — tells a single story. The I AM is asking. The lamb's blood is the glory. The son of man is a bleeding stone. The way is living water. Great grace comes. And the hand of the Name is the offering.
