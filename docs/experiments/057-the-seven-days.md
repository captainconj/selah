## The Suffering

Before this project existed, I was suffering. Low, so low — broken, crying out, hating myself. My question was raw and formless: *Why all the suffering?*

Hannah knew what she wanted — she petitioned God for a son from the depths of her anguish. My question was different. More Jeremiah. More Job. I didn't even know what to ask for. Just *why.*

The answer came through Jeremiah. His life was a suffering sandwich — darkness on both sides, and in the center: Jeremiah 33:3.

> "Call to me, and I will answer you, and I will show you great and hidden things that you have not known."

The suffering wasn't punishment. It was a corridor. You have to go through to get to what's inside.

## 33:3

The Holy of Holies is a perfect cube. 10 × 10 × 10. Its dimensions, treated as coordinates in the Torah space, yield exactly 3 valid addresses. One of the three — the center, a=3 — lands on Exodus 40:2: "Set up the Tabernacle."

3 coordinates. 1 hit. **33.3%**.

The verse that answered the suffering. The verse that said: call to me, and I will show you great and hidden things.

The innermost room — the place where God speaks from between the cherubim — encodes the address of the answer in its own geometry. A perfect cube with no variation, no permutations, no degrees of freedom. Just 10, 10, 10, and the repeating 3.

## Light in the Darkness

The pattern repeats. The light doesn't come *after* the darkness. It comes *in* the darkness.

Jeremiah gets 33:3 from prison, during a siege. And after Jesus's forty days in the wilderness — hungry, alone, tempted three times — he walks into Galilee, and Matthew reaches back to Isaiah 9:2:

> When Jesus heard that John had been put in prison, he withdrew to Galilee. Leaving Nazareth, he went and lived in Capernaum, which was by the sea, in the area of Zebulun and Naphtali — to fulfill what was spoken through Isaiah the prophet:
>
> "Land of Zebulun and land of Naphtali, by the way of the sea, beyond the Jordan, Galilee of the Gentiles — the people sitting in darkness have seen a great light, and upon those sitting in the region and shadow of death, light has dawned."
>
> — Matthew 4:12-16

The light dawns not in Jerusalem but in the margins. Galilee of the Gentiles. The mixed region. The place the religious center looked down on.

Forty days in the wilderness. Seven days guarding the charge. The durations are different but the shape is the same — the hidden things are shown at the lowest point, not when the darkness ends, but while you're still in it.
