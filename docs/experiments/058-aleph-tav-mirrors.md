# The Aleph-Tav Mirrors

Type: `synthesis`
State: `mixed`

## The Fold

304,850 = 7 × 50 × 13 × 67. Every letter has address (a, b, c, d).

The **a-fold** mirrors across the seven days: (a, b, c, d) ↔ (6-a, b, c, d). Same jubilee, same love, same understanding — mirror day. Day 0↔6, 1↔5, 2↔4. Day 3 mirrors itself.

6,032 את in the Torah. For each one, we ask: what's at its mirror position?

**Every single את in the center seventh (a=3, Leviticus) mirrors to another את.** All 1,222 of them. The center is perfectly self-symmetric under the fold.

Only 52 unique pairs exist in the outer days — these are the ones that mirror across the narrative.

## The Self-Mirroring Passage

**Exodus 34:29-35** sits at the exact center of the a-fold. Multiple את in these verses mirror to themselves. The passage is about Moses' **shining face** and the **veil**:

> "Moses did not know that the skin of his face **shone**... Aaron and all Israel saw Moses, and behold, the skin of his face shone, and they were afraid... When Moses finished speaking with them, he put a **veil** on his face. But when Moses went in before the LORD to speak with Him, he would **remove the veil**..."

The center of the mirror is about veiling and unveiling. A mirror at the fold-point is about seeing a face shine through a covering.

This is the same passage that marks the **boundary between divisions 3 and 4** (experiment 054). The hinge of the seven days is the hinge of the mirror.
