## The Space

304,850 = 7 × 50 × 13 × 67. Every letter has address (a, b, c, d). Four axes, four dimensions, four ways to fold.

Each fold mirrors one axis at its midpoint:

| Fold | Operation | Midpoint | Crease |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| **a-fold** | a ↔ 6-a | a=3 | Day 3 mirrors itself |
| **b-fold** | b ↔ 49-b | b=24/25 | Even dimension — no single center |
| **c-fold** | c ↔ 12-c | c=6 | Love position 6 mirrors itself |
| **d-fold** | d ↔ 66-d | d=33 | Understanding position 33 mirrors itself |

## The Convergence

Three of the four folds share the same crease center.

The a-fold midpoint (a=3), the c-fold midpoint (c=6), and the d-fold midpoint (d=33) all converge on **Leviticus 8:35**:

> ושמרתם את משמרת יהוה ולא תמותו... שבעת ימים

"You shall guard the charge of the LORD, that you do not die... **seven days**."

Three independent symmetry axes — completeness, love, understanding — point to the same verse. The b-fold's crease pair (b=24 and b=25) also lands in Leviticus 8, within the consecration of Aaron.

The center of the space is the center of every fold.

### The Crease of Creases

Fix a=3, c=6, d=33 — the midpoints of three axes. Only b varies freely. That leaves exactly **50 positions** — one per jubilee. The intersection of the three fold creases *is* the jubilee axis. The fourth dimension is what remains when the other three are folded.

## The Silent Axes

The factorization names four concepts: completeness (7), jubilee (50), love (13), understanding (67). Searching for these words in the Torah:

| Word | Meaning | Gematria | Occurrences in Torah |
|------|---------|----------|---------------------|
| שבע | seven | — | **171** |
| חמשים | fifty | — | **39** |
| אחד | one | **13** | **189** |
| יובל | jubilee | — | **4** |
| אהבה | love | **13** | **0** |
| בינה | understanding | **67** | **0** |

**אהבה (love) and בינה (understanding) never appear in the Torah as words.**

The outer axes — 7 and 50 — are spoken. The inner axes — 13 and 67 — are silent. The Torah shows love and understanding. It doesn't name them.

This mirrors the tabernacle architecture: 7 and 50 appear in cubit measurements (menorah branches, courtyard width, curtain clasps). 13 and 67 appear only in gematria (אחד = 13, בינה = 67). The outer dimensions are physical. The inner dimensions are semantic.

### The Word "Seven" at c=7

שבע (seven) clusters at **c=7** — the seventh position on the love axis. 1.44× expected density.

**Leviticus 25:8** — the verse connecting 7 to 50 ("count seven sabbaths of years, seven times seven years, and consecrate the fiftieth year") — puts all four of its שבע on the **same d-fiber** at c=7. The verse about the relationship between the axes occupies the coordinate where they meet.

### The Shema's "One"

The אחד in Deuteronomy 6:4 (שמע ישראל יהוה אלהינו יהוה **אחד**) sits at coordinate [6, 0, 3, 11] — a=6, the final seventh. The same verse that mirrors the first תורה milemarker under the a-fold.

## The Tabernacle as Fold Instructions

The sacred structures encode the axes:

### The Number 50 Saturates the Tabernacle

- Noah's Ark width: **50** cubits (Gen 6:15)
- Tabernacle courtyard width: **50** cubits (Exod 27:18)
- Curtain loops: **50** + **50** + **50** = 150 loops (Exod 26:5, 26:10)
- Gold clasps: **50** (Exod 26:6)
- Bronze clasps: **50** (Exod 26:11)

The 50 clasps join two sets of curtains into "one tabernacle" — אחד (one, gematria 13). The physical joining mechanism (50) and the concept of unity (13) interlock in the construction itself.

### The Breastplate Encodes the Axes

The Urim and Thummim breastplate (Exodus 28) contains 72 letters:

| Section | Letters | Axis |
|---------|---------|------|
| Patriarchs (Abraham, Isaac, Jacob) | **13** | c-axis (love/unity) |
| 12 Tribes | **50** | b-axis (jubilee) |
| Closing (tribes of Jeshurun) | 9 | — |

The reading device has the inner and outer axes encoded in its letter counts. **13 and 50. Exactly.**

### The Veil Has Four Pillars

Exodus 26:32. The veil separating the Holy Place from the Holy of Holies hangs on **4 pillars**. Four axes. Four dimensions. The entrance to the courtyard has **5 pillars** (Exodus 26:37) — 5 books.

## The Compound Folds

15 possible folds: 4 single, 6 double, 4 triple, 1 quad (the full reversal). Each is an involution — fold twice and you're back.

### The a-fold Dominates

| Fold | את → את | × Expected | Note |
|------|---------|-----------|------|
| **a-fold** | 1,326 | **11.1×** | Seven-day axis overwhelms all others |
| c-fold | 553 | 4.6× | Love axis — second strongest |
| d-fold | 264 | 2.2× | Understanding axis |
| b-fold | 106 | 0.9× | Jubilee axis — no special preservation |

Expected by chance: ~119 (6,032²/304,850). The a-fold preserves aleph-tav at 11× the chance rate. The jubilee axis doesn't preserve them at all.

### Fixed Points

A position is fixed under a fold only when every folded axis sits at its midpoint.

- Axis a (dim 7): midpoint 3 — **exists**
- Axis b (dim 50): midpoint 24.5 — **no fixed point** (even dimension)
- Axis c (dim 13): midpoint 6 — **exists**
- Axis d (dim 67): midpoint 33 — **exists**

Any fold involving b has **zero** fixed points. The acd-fold (folding everything except jubilee) has exactly **50 fixed points** — one per jubilee value, sitting at a=3, c=6, d=33. The crease of three creases IS the jubilee line.

### Deuteronomy 6 Is the Fold Attractor

Where does position 0 (ב of בראשית) go under folds involving the a-axis?

| Fold | Position 0 maps to |
|------|-------------------|
| a-fold | **Deuteronomy 6:1** (the Shema) |
| ad-fold | **Deuteronomy 6:2** |
| ac-fold | **Deuteronomy 6:18** ("do what is right and good") |
| acd-fold | **Deuteronomy 6:18** |

Deuteronomy 6 — the chapter of the Shema — is a gravitational center for any fold that includes the seven-day axis. The first letter of the Torah folds to the greatest declaration regardless of which other axes you mirror.

### Complementary Folds

Every partition of {a,b,c,d} into two disjoint subsets X and Y produces a dual: fold_X(first) = fold_Y(last) and fold_X(last) = fold_Y(first). Seven pairs, all confirmed. The composition X∘Y always equals the full reversal.

### The Full Reversal (abcd-fold)

The abcd-fold maps position i to 304,849 − i — reading backwards. Position 0 (ב) maps to position 304,849 (the last letter). Same-letter rate: 6.37%, matching the 6.34% expected from letter frequencies. The full reversal has no special letter-preservation — it's just statistics. The interesting structure is in the partial folds.
