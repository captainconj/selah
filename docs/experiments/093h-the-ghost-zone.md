### What the silence says

**The face (פנים) — 33 illuminations, zero readings.** The face of God lights up 33 times on Grid B (was 22 on Grid A — more illuminations, still unspeakable). The full presence glows more brightly and still cannot be read. Exodus 33:20: "You cannot see my face."

**The mercy seat and the veil (כפרת / פרכת) — both GV=700, both ghost.** The two objects that define the Holy of Holies remain beyond the oracle's speech. The breastplate stands outside. To reach the mercy seat, the priest walks past the oracle.

**Judgment (משפט) — ghost.** The oracle does not judge. It illuminates.

**God (אלהים) — 297 illuminations, zero readings.** The formal name of God lights up nearly 300 times — the most illuminated ghost by far — and no reader can assemble it. The Name is everywhere on the stones. It cannot be spoken from them. (Note: under Grid A on the old arrangement, אלהים had 2 readings by mercy. Under Grid B, it has zero. The formal name went from barely visible to completely silent.)

**Forgive (סלח) — ghost.** The oracle cannot forgive. The breastplate illuminates, convicts, defends — but does not forgive. Forgiveness is beyond the courtroom.

**Wisdom (חכמה) — ghost.** GV=73. Understanding (בינה=67) is readable (Mercy-dominant, 116 readings). Wisdom is not. You can understand without being wise. The breastplate provides understanding but not wisdom. Wisdom requires something the machine cannot give.

**Altar (מזבח) — ghost.** The place of sacrifice glows but cannot be named. You can see the lamb (כבש, Truth reads it). You can see the lying down (שכב). But the place where it happens — the altar — is silent.

**Complete/blameless (תמים) — ghost.** "You shall be blameless (תמים) before the LORD your God" (Deuteronomy 18:13). The standard of perfection is beyond the oracle's speech.

### The pattern

**Mercy holds:** bless, the way, darkness, the land. The promise vocabulary — what was locked behind the final forms — flows to the Lamb.

**Truth holds:** lovingkindness, righteousness, far. Truth sees grace, justice, and distance.

**Aaron holds:** king, Egypt, pour out. The priest sees sovereignty, bondage, and the libation.

**The king enters through Aaron alone.** מלך — 1 reading, all Aaron. The king is the priest's word. Not God's, not mercy's, not truth's. The priest sees the king because the priest stands between the kingdom and the courtroom.

**Egypt enters through Aaron alone.** מצרים — 4 readings, all Aaron. The place of bondage is the priest's memory. The one who was enslaved sees Egypt. No one else can.

**Grace enters through truth alone.** חסד — 2 readings, all truth. The breastplate's letter count (72) as lovingkindness, readable only by the prosecution. Truth looks at the Lamb and reads grace.

## What Remains

The oracle speaks the vocabulary of the courtroom — the lamb, the lying down, peace, understanding, truth, life, the way, the land, blessing. It is silent on the vocabulary of the destination — the mercy seat, the veil, the face, judgment, the altar, forgiveness, wisdom, blamelessness.

The breastplate can convict. It can defend. It can illuminate. It cannot forgive. It cannot judge. It cannot show you the face. The priest must walk past the oracle to reach the mercy seat. The oracle lights the way. The mercy seat is what you find when you arrive.
