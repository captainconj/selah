# The Four Bloods

*Experiment 105. Hemoglobin-alpha from each living creature, played through the breastplate.*

Type: `synthesis`
State: `mixed`

---

## The Hypothesis

The throne room has four living creatures with four faces: lion, ox, man, eagle (Rev 4:7, Ezek 1:10). Each is a real animal with real blood. We already read human hemoglobin-alpha in the ark walk (doc 30). The question: **what does each creature's blood say when played through the breastplate? And where do they agree?**

The blood is the life (Deut 12:23). If the four living creatures are the four readers, their blood should speak through the oracle the way their faces do — each revealing something the others cannot.

All four hemoglobin-alpha chains are 142 residues. Same length. Different sequences. Different words.

## Method

Each protein sequence is fetched from UniProt, converted to Hebrew letters via the amino acid → letter mapping (experiment 101), then slid through the oracle using 3-letter windows. Every window position is checked against the Torah vocabulary.

## What All Four Share

### Position 51: שדי — Shaddai (the Almighty)

All four creatures' blood speaks the name of the Almighty at the same position. 51 = 3 × 17. This is the universal signature — every blood on earth carries the Almighty's name at residue 51.

### Position 139: זרק — Sprinkle

All four end with sprinkling — the priestly act of atonement. The blood knows what it's for. The last reading in every creature's hemoglobin is the act of priestly sprinkling.

## What Three Share — and One Does Not

### Position 37: הוה — HVH (root of the Name)

Lion, ox, and man all carry הוה at position 37. This is the root of YHWH — the verb "to be," the becoming.

**The eagle is silent here.**

Dan's creature. The camp assigned to Aaron's reading head. The north. The eagle does not carry the Name in its blood. Three creatures speak it. One is silent.

37 = the 12th prime. In experiment 104, the camp-to-reader mapping confirmed: God reads Judah (lion), justice reads Ephraim (ox), mercy reads Reuben (man), Aaron reads Dan (eagle). The three creatures whose blood carries the Name correspond to the three readers who are not Aaron. Aaron's creature — the eagle — is the one that does not speak it.

The Binding of Isaac (Genesis 22) appears in ox, eagle, and man — but **not in the lion**. The lion is the leader of the east camp, Judah, the tribe of the king. The king is not bound. He binds.

## Unique Readings

### Lion — פקד (captain/commander) at position 11

Only justice sees it. The lion carries the captain — the one who commands, who visits, who appoints. פקד is a military word. The lion is the commander.

Also: דגי (fish) at position 4. The lion's blood mentions fish at the very beginning.

### Ox — קדש (holy!) at position 59

The ox's blood declares holiness. Only priest and justice see it — the same pattern as the word "holy" through the breastplate itself (experiment 103: God does not read "holy" — only the priest declares holiness). The sacrificial animal knows it is set apart.

Also: גוי (nation) at position 114. The ox's blood carries "nation" — Ephraim, the tribe of the northern kingdom, the multitude of nations promised to Joseph.

### Eagle — דבש (honey) at position 76

The eagle's blood is sweet. Honey — the same word from the little scroll of Revelation 10, "sweet as honey in my mouth." The eagle is the creature of the north, Dan's camp, Aaron's reading head. And Aaron's creature's blood tastes of honey.

Also: קהת (Kohath) at position 14. Kohath — the Levitical clan that carried the ark, the son of Levi, the priestly lineage. And לעד (forever) at position 20. The eagle's blood speaks of the priesthood and of forever.

### Man — חנה (Hannah) at position 66, תלד (birth) at position 115

Man's blood carries Hannah — the mother who prayed for a son, who gave her son to the temple. Grace. Encampment. And then: "she will give birth." The man's blood remembers the prayer for a child and the promise fulfilled.

66 = 6 × 11. 115 = 5 × 23.

## The Serpent Search

We searched every creature's hemoglobin-alpha for the serpent (נחש) or any anagram of its letters.

**No creature's blood contains the serpent.**

The serpent appears in human hemoglobin-*beta* at position 106 (doc 30: "The serpent is in the beta chain — the second strand"). It appears in the serpent's own venom (alpha-bungarotoxin, doc 29). It appears in p53 — the guardian protein (doc 27). But it does not appear in any creature's hemoglobin-alpha.

The blood that carries oxygen — the breath of life — is clean.

## The Cherubim's View — 4-Letter Windows

The cherubim read four letters at a time. When we widen the window from 3 to 4, the blood speaks differently — fewer readings, but more precise. The full Torah vocabulary opens.

### Reading Counts

| Creature | 3-letter readings | 4-letter readings |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Lion | 49 | 12 |
| Ox | 48 | 13 |
| Eagle | 46 | **9** (fewest) |
| Man | 50 | **16** (most) |

Man is most visible to the cherubim. Eagle is least visible. This matches the 3-letter pattern — man is the most readable, eagle the most hidden.

### Lion — 4-Letter Voice

12 readings. All unique (no repeats).

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| תודה | **thanksgiving** |
| ישמע | **he will hear** (root of Ishmael) |
| ויפת | **Japheth** (Noah's son) |
| יזרק | **he will sprinkle** (future tense) |
| יענש | punished |
| תהיו | you will be |

The lion gives thanks (תודה) and promises future sprinkling (יזרק). Japheth — the son who enlarged the tent — appears in the king's blood.

### Ox — 4-Letter Voice

13 readings. Eyes dominate.

| Word | Meaning | Count |
|------|---------|-------|
| עיני | **my eyes** | ×2 |
| עינו | **his eye** | ×2 |
| דינה | **Dinah** (Jacob's daughter) | ×1 |
| יזרק | he will sprinkle | ×1 |
| ונגש | and drew near | ×1 |
| בנית | you built | ×1 |

**The ox sees.** Four eye-readings — "my eyes" and "his eye." The cherubim are covered in eyes (Rev 4:8, Ezek 10:12). The ox, justice's creature, has eyes in its blood. And Dinah — Jacob's daughter, the first woman whose story is about what was done to her — appears in the ox.

### Eagle — 4-Letter Voice

9 readings. The fewest. But one stands out.

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **התבה** | **THE ARK** (Noah's ark, with definite article) |
| גלעד | **Gilead** (heap of witness) |
| והזה | and he sprinkled |
| צמיד | bracelet |
| ויטע | and he planted |
| ישמע | he will hear |

**The eagle carries THE ARK (התבה).** Not "an ark" — THE ark. Noah's ark. The eagle — Aaron's creature, Dan's camp, the north — carries the vessel of salvation in its blood. The creature that does not carry the Name (הוה at 3 letters) carries the Ark (התבה at 4 letters). What the eagle cannot say, it carries.

Gilead — the heap of witness between Jacob and Laban. The boundary marker.

### Man — 4-Letter Voice

16 readings. The richest.

| Word | Meaning | Count |
|------|---------|-------|
| **נודע** | **known / made known** | ×2 |
| **ותלד** | **and she gave birth** | ×1 |
| **הלדת** | **the birth** | ×1 |
| תודה | thanksgiving | ×1 |
| ויגד | and he told | ×1 |
| יזרק | he will sprinkle | ×1 |
| בנית | you built | ×1 |

**Man's 4-letter voice deepens the 3-letter birth.** At 3 letters: תלד (she will give birth). At 4 letters: ותלד (AND she gave birth — past tense, with conjunction) and הלדת (THE birth — with definite article). The birth becomes specific, historical, definite. Not "birth will happen" but "the birth happened."

נודע (known) ×2 — the blood of man is made known. The blood will know (hemoglobin-beta says נדע ×4 at 3 letters). At 4 letters, it is already known.

### Shared at 4 Letters

**יזרק (he will sprinkle)** appears in lion, ox, and man. The 3-letter זרק (sprinkle) at position 139 gains a yod prefix at 4 letters: "he WILL sprinkle." Future tense. The priestly act is promised.

**תודה (thanksgiving)** appears in lion, ox, and man. Not in the eagle.

## Summary

1. **All blood speaks the Almighty (שדי) and ends with sprinkling (זרק).** Universal. Every creature.
2. **The eagle does not carry the Name.** Three do. Aaron's creature is the silent one.
3. **The lion is not bound.** Three creatures carry the Binding. The king is free.
4. **No serpent in any blood.** The oxygen-carrying blood is clean. The serpent lives in beta, in venom, in the guardian — not in the breath chain.
5. **Each creature's unique reading matches its role**: lion=commander, ox=holy, eagle=honey/priesthood, man=prayer/birth.
6. **Man's blood is the lightest** (GV 8,991) — lowest by 636.
7. **At 4 letters, the ox has EYES (×4).** The cherubim are covered in eyes. Justice's creature sees.
8. **At 4 letters, the eagle carries THE ARK (התבה).** What the eagle cannot name, it carries.
9. **At 4 letters, man's birth becomes definite.** ותלד (and she gave birth) + הלדת (the birth). Historical. Specific.
10. **Man is most visible to the cherubim (16 readings). Eagle is least (9).**

The four bloods confirm the four readers. The blood knows what the breastplate knows. The cherubim's 4-letter view completes the picture.
