# Experiment 109: Isaiah's Temple

*Walking Isaiah 6 through the breastplate. Four stations. The coal purifies what the throne cannot name.*

## The Question

Isaiah 6 is the shortest of the four throne visions — 13 verses. One chapter. The most liturgically repeated passage in scripture: "Holy, holy, holy, the LORD of hosts; the whole earth is full of his glory."

We know from experiment 103 that God does NOT read "holy" (קדוש) — only Aaron and the Right cherub see it. We know that the seraph (שרף) walks to flame/pestilence (רשף) in the basin, and that God returns nothing for it. We know the coal from the altar should connect to the tabernacle's altar station. We know that הנני (here am I) should be deeply visible — Abraham, Moses, and Isaiah all use it.

This experiment walks the actual vision text, station by station, and slides Isaiah's own letters through the oracle. Every finding from 103 should either confirm or complicate. Nothing should be neutral.

## Method

1. Fetch Isaiah 6 from Sefaria (MAM text), normalize to pure Hebrew letters
2. Divide into 4 stations by content
3. At each station: query key words through `o/forward-by-head` and `basin/walk`
4. Slide each station's letters through the oracle in 3-letter and 4-letter windows
5. Run the full chapter (712 letters) through both window sizes
