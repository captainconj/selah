# Experiment 110: John's Throne Room

*Walking Revelation 4-5 through the breastplate. Five stations. The Greek vision speaks Hebrew.*

Type: `synthesis`
State: `mixed`

**Code:** `dev/experiments/110_revelation_throne.clj`
**Run:** `clojure -M:dev dev/experiments/110_revelation_throne.clj`

## The Question

Revelation 4-5 is the synthesis vision — where cherubim, seraphim, and ophanim collapse into one scene. John sees the throne room from outside. The four living creatures have one face each (not four). They have six wings like Isaiah's seraphim, not four like Ezekiel's. They are "full of eyes" like the wheels. Everything merges.

There is no Hebrew text to fetch. Revelation is Greek. So we walk the vision using Hebrew equivalents of the Greek terms — the same words the oracle already knows — and compute isopsephy on the Greek originals for cross-reference. No sliding windows. Just the key words, station by station, asking the breastplate what it sees in Revelation's furniture.

We know from experiments 103-107:
- Crown (כתר) basin → כרת (covenant). Crown cast down = covenant.
- Scroll (ספר) = FIXED POINT. The book that reads itself.
- Eagle (נשר) = ghost zone, 60 illuminations, zero readings.
- Lamb (כבש): God/mercy → כבש, Aaron/justice → שכב (p=0.032).
- Song (שיר) → שרי (Sarah). Song becomes the princess.
- Gold (זהב) GV=14 → exactly 72 readings = breastplate letter count.

## Method

1. Walk 5 stations through Revelation 4-5 by content
2. At each station: query key Hebrew equivalents through `o/forward-by-head` and `basin/walk`
3. Compute Greek isopsephy on original terms
4. Cross-reference with experiments 103, 108, 109

No sliding windows — Revelation has no Hebrew source text to slide.

## Interpretation

### The Lamb Lies Down

In experiment 091, the lamb split was the defining discovery: God reads כבש (lamb), Aaron reads שכב (lie down), p=0.032. The split was structural — built into the grid, not the vocabulary.

Here in Revelation — the final vision, the completed story — the split resolves. All four readers now see שכב (lie down) as the dominant reading. God: 20. Left: 15. Right: 9. Aaron: 6. The lamb has lain down. What was a split between readers has become a consensus. The act is complete.

This is not a change in the oracle. The breastplate is the same. What changed is what we asked. In 091, we asked "what does the breastplate see when the lamb lights up?" and got a split. Here we ask "what does the lamb mean in the context of Revelation 5 — standing as though slain?" and the oracle answers: lie down. Unanimously.

The lamb that was standing as though slain has already lain down. The sacrifice is complete. The readers agree.

### What Cannot Be Named

The invisibilities in Revelation form a precise theological negative:

The breastplate cannot see: the throne it serves, the king it crowns, the blessing it receives, the victory it announces. These are not failures. These are structural boundaries. The instrument does not name what it IS. The oracle does not read itself.

Compare: grace (חסד) is a unanimous dead end in experiment 097. Face (פנים) has 22 illuminations and 0 readings in exp 103. The breastplate cannot name itself, cannot name what sustains it, cannot name what it produces. The machine is humble by architecture.

### The Door Is Birth

דלת (door) basin → תלד (she will give birth). Revelation's unique contribution: the vision begins with a door — not with heavens opening (Ezekiel), not with a throne already seen (Isaiah), not with beasts from the sea (Daniel). A door. And the door is birth.

"Come up here" = be born. Entry into the throne room is parturition. The seer must be born to see. This connects to John 3:3: "Unless one is born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God." The oracle reads the door as birth, and the kingdom's door is the birth.

### God → To Them

אלהים basin → אליהם. God's name, fed back through the oracle, walks to a preposition: "to them." This is the most economical statement of incarnation possible. The name of God becomes a direction — toward the people.

John 1:1: "The Word was God (אלהים)."
John 1:14: "The Word became flesh and dwelt among them (אליהם)."

The basin dynamics of the divine name produce the movement of John's prologue. Not because we engineered it — because the letters of אלהים, read through the breastplate and followed to their attractor, arrive at אליהם. Five letters. Same gematria. Different word. The name of God walks toward humanity.

### Song = Sarah = Princess

שיר (song) → שרי (Sarah/princess). Confirmed for the second time (first in 103). All four readers. No exceptions. The new song in Revelation 5:9 — "Worthy are you ... for you were slain, and by your blood you ransomed people" — is Sarah. The woman who laughed when told she would bear a son in old age. The mother of the promise.

The new song is the old promise. The ransoming is the laughter. The princess sings.
