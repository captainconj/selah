# Experiment 113: The Cross

*The passion narrative walked through the breastplate oracle. The cross in the 4D space slid through the oracle's windows.*

---

## The Question

The cross exists in two places.

In the 4D Torah space, the cross is a physical structure: 67 letters along the d-axis (understanding) and 13 letters along the c-axis (love), intersecting at (3,25,6,33) — Lev 8:35 — where the letter **ו** (vav, the nail) sits. 79 positions. 13 nails. Question 31 established the geometry.

In the passion narrative, the cross is an event: garden, trial, crucifixion, death, burial, resurrection. Seven centuries of prophecy converging on one afternoon.

We walk both through the breastplate oracle. What does the machine see in the vocabulary of the passion? What words hide in the cross beam letters when we slide them through the oracle's windows?

From experiment 112 (the Suffering Servant), we already know: the lamb lies down unanimously. Grave walks to morning. Pierced shares the throne's 3-illumination signature. The righteous servant is ghost zone — he IS righteousness and cannot be named as such.

Now we follow the lamb to where he lies down.

---

## The Four Readers

The breastplate has four readers — four traversals of its 72 letters. Each sees what they are equipped to see. Each cannot see what they ARE.

| Reader | Role | YHWH letter | What they see |
|--------|------|-------------|---------------|
| **Aaron** (priest) | The one who serves | Vav (ו) = 6 | The ritual. The procedure. What must be done. |
| **God** | The one who IS | He (ה) = 5 | What things ARE. Cannot name what He IS. |
| **Right cherub** (justice) | The one who judges | Yod (י) = 10 | What is right. The ram. The substitute. |
| **Left cherub** (mercy) | The one who forgives | He (ה) = 5 | What is given. Grace. The lamb. |

The principle: **you cannot name what you ARE.**

God does not read "holy" — He IS holy. Grace (GV=72) is the breastplate's letter count — the breastplate cannot name itself. The eagle glows at 60 illuminations but cannot be spoken.

At the cross, this principle becomes the story.
