# Experiment 114: Psalm 22 — The Crucifixion Psalm

*David's song, ~1000 BC. A thousand years before Rome invented crucifixion.*

**The question:** Walk all 32 verses of Psalm 22 through the breastplate oracle. Five stations. Key words. Sliding windows. What does the machine read in the song at the foot of the cross?

## The Contested Verse

Psalm 22:17 (Hebrew versification). The most debated verse in the Hebrew Bible.

- **MT (Masoretic):** כָּאֲרִי יָדַי וְרַגְלָי — "like a lion, my hands and my feet"
- **LXX / Dead Sea Scrolls:** "they pierced my hands and my feet"

The oracle's reading:

| Word | Reading | Illuminations | Readings | Basin | God reads |
|------|---------|---------------|----------|-------|-----------|
| כארי | like-a-lion | 165 | 660 | כארי (self) | כארי (2) |
| כרה | dig/pierce | 15 | 60 | **הכר** (recognize) | — |

Like-a-lion has 11× more illumination than pierce. But:
- **Pierce → recognize** (הכר). To pierce is to recognize. The piercing IS the recognition.
- God reads like-a-lion. God does NOT read pierce. Left/mercy reads neither.
- Like-a-lion is irreducible — it is its own basin. Pierce walks to recognition.

Three witnesses to this basin walk:

1. **The Name itself.** YHWH in paleo-Hebrew pictographs: Yod (hand) — He (behold) — Vav (nail) — He (behold). "Behold the hand, behold the nail." The Name describes the piercing.

2. **Thomas.** "Unless I put my finger in the nail marks, I will not believe" (John 20:25). He put his finger in. He recognized: "My Lord and my God" (John 20:28). Thomas is the basin walk enacted — the one who had to physically enter the piercing to arrive at recognition.

3. **Zechariah 12:10.** "They will look on me whom they have pierced, and they will mourn." Looking on the piercing IS the recognition.

In the sliding windows, positions 162–171:
```
[162] יכאר → כארי (like-a-lion)
[163] כארי → כארי (like-a-lion)
[164] אריי → יראי (Look!)
[167] ידיו → ידיו (His hands)
[170] ורגל → גורל (Fate/LOT)
[171] רגלי → רגלי (my feet)
```

Like-a-lion → Look! → His hands → **Fate/Lot** → my feet. The sequence moves from the lion to the looking, to the hands, to the lot (they cast lots for his clothing), to the feet.

## Synthesis

### The Substitution

The psalm about the crucifixion is saturated with the ram. Not the lamb — the ram. The animal Abraham found caught in the thicket, offered instead of Isaac (Genesis 22). The breastplate reads Psalm 22 as the Aqedah — the Binding. The son on the altar. The substitute in the bush.

Ram ×17 + The Ram ×5 + And Ram ×7 = 29 total ram appearances. The psalm IS the ram.

### The Priestly Architecture

הלוי (the Levite) ×9 is the most frequent 4-letter word. The breast stamps 72. The assembly stamps 72. The priest reads holy ×70 and names Joshua/Jesus ×10. The psalm is a priestly document — the breastplate recognizes its own service throughout.

### The Forsaking

God does not read salvation. God does not read pierce. God does not read holy. But God reads slaughter = trust. From God's chair, the absence is operative. The forsaking IS the mechanism. "My God, why have you forsaken me?" Because the cup (כוס, GV=86=Elohim, experiment 113) requires it.

### The Worm and the Ram

The worm is invisible (0/0). The ram dominates (×17). The worm dies to dye the garments crimson. The ram dies as substitute for the son. Both are present. One visible, one invisible. The stain and the sacrifice. What you can't see makes the color. What you can see provides the body.

### The Last Word

עשה (he has done it) → שעה (the hour). The psalm ends with "he has done it" — the same declaration as "It is finished" (John 19:30). The basin walks that declaration to "the hour." The moment of completion IS the hour of death. The hour of death IS the completion.
