## Synthesis

### The Redemption Is Invisible

Eight things are invisible: angel, wood, blessing, star, walking, withholding, thicket, substitution. Every mechanism of salvation is dark to the oracle. The breastplate sees the father, the son, the fire, the knife, the lamb, the ram — but not the voice that stops the killing, not the wood on the son's back, not the bush that catches the substitute, not the substitution itself.

The breastplate sees the crisis. It does not see the rescue. What saves is hidden.

### The Ram Is the Priest's God

איל → אלי. The ram is "my God." Aaron reads this at 38:25 ratio — overwhelming. The priest who performs the sacrifice sees God in the substitute. The cry from the cross — אלי, אלי — is the priest's reading of the ram. The ram caught by its horns in the thicket IS the one the priest calls "my God."

### The Test Is the Revelation

נסה → סנה. The testing at Moriah and the burning bush at Sinai are the same event in the basin. The bush burned but was not consumed. The son was bound but was not killed. Both mountains show the same thing: fire that does not destroy.

### What Cannot Be Named

Three things are visible but walk to nothing: the only son (יחיד, 660 readings → null), the place (מקום, 112 readings → null), the slaughter (שחט, 24 readings → null). The uniqueness of the son, the location of the event, and the killing itself — all walk to emptiness. The Aqedah is made of things the oracle can point to but cannot name.

### The Bride While the Son Is Bound

While Isaac lies on the altar, the genealogy introduces Rebekah — 720 readings, 10 × 72, stamping the breastplate ten times over. Her name walks to הקרב (bringing near/sacrifice). She IS the offering brought near. The bride and the sacrifice are the same word. And in the genealogy's sliding windows: כלה (bride) ×3, מלכה (queen) ×4, הילד (the child) ×4. Birth and bride and queen — all present. All emerging from the moment of the son's binding.

### The Well Is the Chapter

הבאר ×26. The well — Beersheba, the well of the oath — dominates every station. It is the most frequent 4-letter word by a factor of nearly two. Abraham's life was about wells — digging them, naming them, fighting for them. The well is where the water is. The well is where the oath is sworn. The well IS the story.
