## Synthesis

### God Cannot Read His Own Name

I AM (אהיה) = 99 illuminations, 396 readings — and God reads zero of them. Holy (קדש) = 144 readings — God reads zero. Moses (משה) = 144 readings — Aaron reads zero. The breastplate enforces a principle: you do not read your own identity. God cannot say "I AM" from the breastplate because He IS it. The priest cannot read the prophet because they share the same office. The Name is spoken by everyone except the one who bears it.

### The Bush IS the Miracle IS the Test

סנה (bush) and נסה (test) share the same letters. The oracle reads both as הנס (the miracle). The bush, the test, and the miracle are one word in three arrangements. Moses turns aside to see — and the turning IS the test IS the miracle. There is no bush without the test. There is no test without the miracle.

### Fire Is the Breastplate, Burning Is Hunger

Fire (אש) stamps 72. It IS the breastplate. But burning (בער) walks to hunger (רעב from God's view). The fire that does not consume — the breastplate reads itself in the flame. The burning that the fire produces — God reads hunger. The bush burns for the hungry. The fire is the oracle. The burning is the suffering.

### Seven Things Hold

I AM, fire, holy, bush, shoe, Moses, milk. Seven fixed points, seven things that are what they are. The entire chapter pivots on irreducible realities. Everything else — Egypt, Horeb, deliverance, pain, the face — dissolves. The fixed points are the architecture of the encounter: who God is, what the ground is, who the man is, what he must remove.

### The Ram in the Bush

The ram (האיל) appears 35 times — more than any other 4-letter word, more than double the next. Abraham bound Isaac on this same mountain range (2 Chronicles 3:1 identifies Moriah with the Temple Mount; tradition links it to Horeb). The ram that replaced Isaac fills Moses' call. The substitute animal saturates the commission of the deliverer. The binding echoes forward into the exodus.

### Service IS Intercession

עבד → בעד. Every reader except Left agrees: service walks to "on behalf of." God reads ONLY "on behalf of" — never "servant." The mission is not servitude. The mission is standing between. Moses will stand between God and Pharaoh, between God and Israel. The oracle names the posture before the man accepts the call.
