## The Scan

Every readable Torah word (9,209 of 12,826) classified by which reader(s) can produce it. A word is "exclusive" to a reader if that reader sees it and no other does.

## Reader Domains (Grid B, 2026-03-27)

| **Mercy-exclusive** | **965** | **10.5%** | The largest exclusive vocabulary. Understanding, life, becoming. |

**Mercy sees the most that no one else sees.** 965 words exclusive to the Lamb — the defense sees more of its own than any other chair. This is the understanding axis: בינה (67) comes alive in Mercy's domain, opening a vocabulary the other readers cannot access.

**Truth sees the least that is uniquely its own.** 386 exclusive words. But the prosecution's small vocabulary contains the heaviest words.

## What Each Reader Holds Alone

### God-exclusive (551 words) — The Judge's vocabulary

Highlights:
- **אהיה** (I will be, GV=21) — Exodus 3:14, the Name at the burning bush. "I AM WHO I WILL BE." Only the Judge reads it.
- **אהובה** (beloved, GV=19) — The feminine beloved. God alone.
- **חלב** (milk/fat, GV=40) — The fat of the offering. The milk of the land.
- **יגאל** (he will redeem, GV=44) — Future tense redemption.
- **כלב** (Caleb/dog, GV=52) — The faithful spy. "He followed me fully" (Numbers 14:24).
- **שלום** (peace, GV=376) — Peace. Still God-only. Always.

God holds "I will be" and "he will redeem." The Judge's vocabulary is future-tense and faithful.

### Mercy-exclusive (965 words) — The Lamb's vocabulary

Highlights:
- **והביא** (and he brought, GV=24) — Bringing. Arrival. Incarnation.
- **האדם** (THE man, GV=50) — The definite man. Not אדם (man) but הָאָדָם (THE man).
- **האדמה** (the ground, GV=55) — The ground from which man was formed (Genesis 2:7).
- **אביהם** (their father, GV=58) — The father of them. Relational.
- **איביך** (your enemies, GV=43) — Your enemies. Mercy sees the accused's adversaries.
- **זבחיך** (your sacrifices, GV=47) — Your sacrifices. Mercy holds what you give up.
- **להביא** (to bring, GV=48) — The infinitive of bringing.

Mercy holds "THE man," "the ground," "their father," "your enemies," "your sacrifices," and every form of bringing. The Lamb's vocabulary is embodied, relational, possessive — it's about YOUR father, YOUR enemies, YOUR sacrifices. The defense sees everything in second person. It knows your life.

### Truth-exclusive (386 words) — The prosecution's vocabulary

Highlights:
- **חסד** (lovingkindness, GV=72) — Grace. The breastplate's letter count. Truth-only.
- **כבוד** (glory, GV=32) — Glory. כבוד = 32 = לב (heart).
- **הנביא** (THE prophet, GV=68) — The definite prophet.
- **כסא** (throne, GV=81) — The throne.
- **חטאה** (sin offering, GV=23) — The sin offering. Truth sees the mechanism of atonement.
- **הכהן** (THE priest, GV=80) — The definite priest. Truth sees the priest.

Truth holds grace, glory, the prophet, the throne, the sin offering, and THE priest. The prosecution's vocabulary describes the SYSTEM — the throne where judgment sits, the priest who mediates, the offering that atones, the glory that fills, the grace that covers. Truth sees the architecture. Mercy sees the people in it.

### Aaron-exclusive (533 words) — The accused's vocabulary

Highlights:
- **מצרים** (Egypt, GV=380) — Bondage. Aaron alone sees Egypt.
- **המלך** (THE king, GV=95) — The definite king. Visible only to the priest.
- **ביתך** (your house, GV=432) — Your house.
- **חנוך** (Enoch, GV=84) — The one who walked with God.
- **עבדך** (your servant, GV=96) — Your servant.

Aaron holds Egypt, the king, your house, Enoch, and your servant. The accused's vocabulary is about bondage and service and home. The one on trial remembers where he came from (Egypt), who rules (the king), where he lives (your house), and who walked before him (Enoch).

## The Pattern

| Reader | Sees | Orientation |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **God** | "I will be," beloved, redemption, Caleb | **Future-tense, faithful** |
| **Mercy** | THE man, the ground, your enemies, your sacrifices, bringing | **Embodied, relational, second-person** |
| **Truth** | Grace, glory, the prophet, the throne, the sin offering, THE priest | **Systemic, architectural** |
| **Aaron** | Egypt, the king, your house, Enoch, your servant | **Memorial, domestic, historical** |

The Judge looks forward. The Lamb knows your life. The prosecution sees the system. The accused remembers.
