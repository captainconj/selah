## Why 7D is impossible

304,850 = 2 × 5 × 5 × 7 × 13 × 67

This number has exactly **6 prime factors** (counting the repeated 5). To fill 7 or more axes without any axis being 1, you would need 7 or more factors. There aren't enough.

The only 7-tuple whose product equals 304,850 is:

**1 × 2 × 5 × 5 × 7 × 13 × 67** (sum = 100)

But this has a trivial axis (1). A 1-axis means one position along that dimension — no extent, no structure, no information. It's a degenerate dimension, an observer with no room to move.

## What the ceiling means

Seven is completeness. It is the first axis of the canonical space. It is the creation week, the menorah branches, the ordination days.

But seven is not the dimensionality. The Torah's letter count permits at most 6 independent axes. The seventh dimension would require a prime factor that does not exist in the number. Completeness is IN the structure (as an axis), not OVER the structure (as a dimension).

**You can walk seven steps along the a-axis. You cannot build seven axes to walk along.**

This mirrors the theology: seven days are lived inside creation. You do not step outside creation into a seventh dimension. The seven is a journey, not a container.

## The sum = 100 curiosity

The trivial 7D decomposition 1 × 2 × 5 × 5 × 7 × 13 × 67 sums to 100. The 6D floor sums to 99. Adding a degenerate dimension adds exactly 1 to the sum.

100 = the total number of sockets (אדנים) in the tabernacle foundation (Ex 38:27). 96 silver sockets for the boards + 4 for the veil pillars = 100. The foundation has room for the degenerate observer.

But this is counting a 1 as something. It isn't.
