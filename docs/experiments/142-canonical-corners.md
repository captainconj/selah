## The Even Axis Problem

304,850 = 7 × 50 × 13 × 67. Three axes are odd (7, 13, 67). One is even: **50.**

An odd axis has a true center: the middle element. 7 → center 3. 13 → center 6. 67 → center 33.

An even axis has no middle element. 50 → the center falls between positions 24 and 25. There are two candidates.

But all three — b=24, b=25, and the literal midpoint — land in **Leviticus 8.** The priestly investiture. The center of the canonical space is not a point. It is a passage:

The blood on the body. Ear, hand, foot — hearing, doing, walking. The consecration says: you will hear with this ear, work with this hand, walk with this foot. And the blood marks them.

The priest's portion. Moses takes the breast — the chest, where the breastplate sits. The exact middle letter of the Torah falls on the verse about the priest's chest.

### Leviticus 8:35 (b=25 center)

The instruction. Guard the charge. Seven days. Don't leave.
