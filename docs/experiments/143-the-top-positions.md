*The ten positions with the highest theological scores on the watermark diagonals are all at narrative turning points. The beginning. The doubt. The bread. The plague. Methuselah. The blessing. The famine. The veil. The last plague. Joseph's bones.*

Type: `control`
State: `clean`

## Method

Combined theological score across all four significant watermark diagonals (skips 804, 938, 870, 872). 7 steps forward from each starting position. 44 theologically significant words. Scanned every 500th position in the first half of the Torah (306 positions).

## What the Top Positions Are

The top 10 are all at narrative turning points:
- **Position 0:** The creation. The beginning of everything.
- **Exodus 4:1:** The doubt. Moses's first objection at the burning bush.
- **Exodus 16:4:** The manna. Bread from heaven. The provision.
- **Exodus 9:23:** The hail. Fire and ice from heaven. Judgment.
- **Genesis 5:25:** Methuselah born. "His death sends it." The flood clock starts.
- **Genesis 24:31:** Rebekah's family welcomes the servant. "Come in, blessed of YHWH."
- **Genesis 26:1:** The second famine. Isaac stays in the land.
- **Genesis 38:19:** Tamar removes her widow's garments. The veil. The lineage preserved.
- **Exodus 11:1:** The last plague announced. "One more plague."
- **Exodus 13:19:** Moses takes Joseph's bones. The separated son crosses the sea.

The creation, the doubt, the provision, the plague, the birth of the flood-clock, the welcome of the blessed, the famine endured, the veil removed, the final judgment, and the bones carried out. The watermarks mark the moments that matter.*
