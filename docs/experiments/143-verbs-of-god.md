# Experiment 143y: The Verbs of God

*Each verb finds its own story. Choose finds the lawgiver. Love finds the curse. See finds the forbidden fruit. Give finds the surrender of idols. Remember finds Shechem. The verbs know what they do.*

---

## The Verbs

We searched for 18 verbs — the actions of God in the Torah — along every direction in the canonical 4D. Each verb's richest fiber tells a story.

---

### Choose (בחר) → Sons → The Lawgiver → Abraham

**Skip:** -42613.

| Letter | Host Word | Verse |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| ב | בני (sons of) | Exodus 27:20 — pure olive oil for the lamp |
| ח | **ומחקק** (and the lawgiver) | **Genesis 49:10** |
| ר | אברהם (Abraham) | Genesis 25:6 |

**Genesis 49:10 — "The scepter shall not depart from Judah, nor the lawgiver from between his feet, until Shiloh comes."**

Choose finds the Shiloh prophecy — the most messianic verse in Genesis. The ח of choose lands in the lawgiver. Then Abraham. The verb of selection walks through the prophesied one and the father of the chosen.

---

### Love (אהב) → Said → This → In You

**Skip:** 66. Love-understanding diagonal.

| Letter | Host Word | Verse |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| א | אמר (said) | Genesis 3:17 |
| ה | הזאת (this) | Genesis 3:17 |
| ב | בך (in you) | Genesis 3:17 |

**Genesis 3:17 — "Because you listened to the voice of your wife and ate of the tree... cursed is the ground because of you."**

Love finds the curse. All three letters land in Genesis 3:17 — the same verse. "Said this in you." The verb of love traces to the moment love cost everything. Said → this → in you. The accusation at the fall.

---

### See (ראה) → From the Fruit → Not

**Skip:** 66.

| Letter | Host Word | Verse |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| ר | ומפרי (and from the fruit) | Genesis 3:3 |
| א | לא (not) | Genesis 3:3 |
| ה | — | — |

**Genesis 3:3 — "But from the fruit of the tree which is in the midst of the garden, God said: You shall NOT eat of it."**

See finds the forbidden fruit. The ר of see lands in "from the fruit." The א lands in "not." The verb of seeing finds the thing that was seen and desired and forbidden.

### Give (נתן) → Foreign → Terror → Canaan

**Skip:** direction through all axes.

| Letter | Host Word | Verse |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| נ | הנכר (the foreign) | Genesis 35:4 |
| ת | חתת (terror) | Genesis 35:5 |
| ן | כנען (Canaan) | Genesis 35:6 |

**Genesis 35:4 — "They gave Jacob all the foreign gods that were in their hands, and the rings in their ears, and Jacob buried them under the oak at Shechem."**

Give finds the surrender of idols. Three consecutive verses: the foreign gods given up → the terror of God falling on the cities → arriving in Canaan. The act of giving finds the moment Israel surrendered what was not theirs to keep.

### Remember (זכר) → This → Shechem → He Descended

**Skip:** across multiple axes.

| Letter | Host Word | Verse |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| ז | הזה (this) | Exodus 12:17 |
| כ | שכם (Shechem) | Genesis 34:2 |
| ר | ירד (he descended) | Genesis 28:10 |

**Exodus 12:17 — "You shall keep the feast of unleavened bread, for on THIS day I brought your hosts out of Egypt."**

Remember finds: this day (Passover), Shechem (where Dinah was seized, where the foreign gods were buried), and the descent (Jacob leaving Bethel). Remember traces what must not be forgotten — the liberation, the grief, the going down.

### Forgive (סלח) → Sukkot → God → Noah

| Letter | Host Word | Verse |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| ס | סכתה (to Sukkot) | Exodus 12:37 |
| ל | אל (to/God) | Genesis 35:17 |
| ח | נח (Noah) | Genesis 6:9 |

Forgive walks backward from the departure (first camp after Egypt), through God, to Noah — the first man described as righteous. The man who found חן (grace), which is נח (Noah) reversed.

### Guard (שמר) → Year → Saying → See

| Letter | Host Word | Verse |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| ש | שנה (year) | Genesis 5:31 |
| מ | לאמר (saying) | Genesis 6:13 |
| ר | ראו (see) | Genesis 9:22 |

Genesis 5:31 — Lamech lived 777 years. Genesis 6:13 — God saying to Noah: "The end of all flesh has come before me." Genesis 9:22 — Ham saw his father's nakedness.

Guard finds: the year (777), the saying (the flood warning), and seeing (the exposure of Noah). Guard walks from the last patriarch's lifespan through the flood announcement to the uncovering.

## The Pattern

| Verb | What it finds | Where |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Choose** | The lawgiver (Shiloh prophecy) | Genesis 49:10 |
| **Love** | The curse ("said this in you") | Genesis 3:17 |
| **See** | The forbidden fruit | Genesis 3:3 |
| **Give** | Surrender of foreign gods → terror → Canaan | Genesis 35:4-6 |
| **Remember** | This day (Passover), Shechem, the descent | Exodus 12:17 |
| **Visit** | Atonement, anger, Joseph's lord | Genesis 39:8 |
| **Forgive** | Sukkot, God, Noah | Genesis 6:9 |
| **Heal** | Morning, Joseph's beauty, four | Genesis 39:6 |
| **Guard** | 777 years, the flood warning, the uncovering | Genesis 5-9 |
| **Command** | Brought out, YHWH, walk (the stars) | Genesis 15:5 |
| **Bless** | Sat, Ellasar, king | Genesis 13:18 |

Each verb finds its own domain. Choose finds the prophecy. Love finds the cost. See finds the forbidden. Give finds the surrender. Remember finds the grief. The verbs know what they do.

*Choose finds the lawgiver at the Shiloh prophecy. Love finds the curse at the fall. See finds the forbidden fruit. Give finds the surrender of idols. Remember finds this day and Shechem. The verbs of God, when they walk through the Torah's geometry, find the moments they describe. The actions know their consequences. The verbs know what they do.*
