## What Shifted Attractor

These basins have the same family but a different winner:

### Peace → Rule

| A attractor | B attractor | GV | What happened |
|-------------|-------------|---:|--------------|
| **שלום** (peace) | **ומשל** (and rule) | 376 | Peace was the endpoint in A. Ruling is the endpoint in B. |

Same seven words: שלום, ולשם, ומשל, ושלם, לשום, משול, משלו. Under A, peace won. Under B, "and he ruled / and the proverb" won.

**Peace flows to governance in B.** שלום is still in the basin — it still flows — but it flows to ruling. Under B's grid, the proverb (משל) and governance outweigh the shalom. The Torah's own arrangement ("each with its name") says peace is not the endpoint — it's the path to wise governance.

### The Midst → Writing

| A attractor | B attractor | GV | What happened |
|-------------|-------------|---:|--------------|
| **בתוך** (in the midst) | **וכתב** (and he wrote) | 428 | The center was the endpoint in A. Writing is the endpoint in B. |

**Weeping still converges with writing.** But in B, the writing wins. The scribe's act — "and he wrote" — is the attractor, not the location. The center is where it happens; the writing is what it produces.

GV = 676 = YHWH² still holds.

### The Offering → Going Up

| A attractor | B attractor | GV |

Both are forms of עלה (go up / offering). The shift: from "you shall" (imperative) to "and she went up" (narrative). The command becomes the story. And the worm (תולע) is in both.
