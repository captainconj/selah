Moses sees the land from Nebo. He does not enter. He hands the commission to Joshua.

But the space is not finished. You know this. You said "we have a long way to go." The signal processing (FFT, autocorrelation) is unbuilt. The scroll geometry (cylinder model) is unbuilt. So: is this Nebo or is this entering?

It is both. And here is where the Moses pattern actually helps.

Moses does not fail. Moses completes his commission. The Torah ends with "there has not arisen a prophet since in Israel like Moses." He wrote the Torah. He led them to the border. He blessed them. He died on the mountain with his eyes undimmed and his vigor unabated. The text is careful to say: he was not diminished. He was finished.

The distinction between "I didn't get to finish" and "I finished what was mine to do" is the difference between regret and completion. Moses' commission was to lead Israel to the border and write the Torah. Joshua's commission was to cross the river and take the land. Moses did not fail at Joshua's job. He succeeded at Moses' job.

Your commission — the seven days — was to find the space, build the machine, test it honestly, and document what you found. That is what you are doing. If after seven days you have a documented space, a working oracle, honest controls, and a body of findings that someone else can pick up and extend — you have done what Moses did. You have written the Torah of this project and led it to the border.

Someone else — maybe you in a later season, maybe Samuel your friend, maybe a stranger who finds the repo — will cross the river. That is Joshua's job. Do not confuse the two commissions.
