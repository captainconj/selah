# Catalog of Every Measured Structure in the Bible

*For experiment 095 expansion: dimensions as coordinates in the 7x50x13x67 Torah space*

The 4D Torah space has axes: a in 0..6, b in 0..49, c in 0..12, d in 0..66.
304,850 = 2 x 5^2 x 7 x 13 x 67.

Notation: dims = physical dimensions in cubits (or specified unit).
Factors shown for each number. **Bold** when a factor is 7, 13, 50, or 67.
"Fits" = all dimensions within axis ranges (a<=6, b<=49, c<=12, d<=66).

## IV. Ezekiel's Temple (Ezekiel 40-48)

The most measured structure in the Bible. Uses the "long cubit" = cubit + handbreadth.
One rod = 6 long cubits. I list dimensions in long cubits unless noted.

### 34. Outer Gates (East, North, South — identical)
- **Ref**: Ezekiel 40:5-27
- **Overall**: **50 cubits long, 25 cubits wide**
- **Threshold**: 1 rod = 6 cubits deep
- **Guardrooms**: 6 x 6 cubits each (3 per side)
- **Between guardrooms**: 5 cubits
- **Vestibule**: 8 cubits deep, pilasters 2 cubits
- **Gateway opening**: 10 cubits wide
- **Gateway width**: **13 cubits** (Ezek 40:11)
- **Gateposts**: 60 cubits high
- **Steps**: **7** steps up (Ezek 40:22,26)
- **Factors of key numbers**:
  - **50**=2x5^2 (=b-axis)
  - 25=5^2
  - **13**=**13** (=c-axis!)
  - **7**=**7** (=a-axis!)
  - 6=2x3
  - 60=2^2x3x5
- **Fits**: 50 NO (one beyond b-axis). **13** = c-axis maximum+1 (c in 0..12; 13 out by 1). **7** = a-axis maximum+1. 6 fits.
- **CRITICAL**: The gate width is exactly **13** and the steps number exactly **7**. Two of four axis values appear as structural numbers in the gate.

## VIII. The Pattern

Three observations emerge from this catalog:

**1. The jubilee axis (50) is the architectural constant.** It appears more than any other axis value — in courtyard widths, curtain clasps, gate lengths, building widths, and dimension sums. The tabernacle IS 50 (sum of its dimensions). The courtyard IS 50 (width). Every major complex includes a 50-measurement.

**2. Seven and thirteen appear at thresholds.** The a-axis (7) appears as steps, gate entries, and story widths — places of transition. The c-axis (13) appears as gate widths and Goliath's height (both boundary/entrance measurements). These axis values mark the places where you cross from one space into another.

**3. Sixty-seven is absent from architecture.** Understanding (binah) is not built. It appears only through time: 33 days and 66 days of purification in Leviticus 12. The d-axis is temporal, not spatial. The builders can construct 7 (completeness), engineer 50 (jubilee), and frame 13 (love) — but they cannot build 67 (understanding). It must be experienced through time.

**4. The ten-stand coincidence.** The ten bronze stands (4x4x3 cubits) have the same dimension sum (11) as the Ark of the Covenant in half-cubits (5+3+3). There are ten of them. 10 x 11 = 110. The construction chapters contain 110 verses (see 095 Phase 2: Ark region has 110 construction verses).
