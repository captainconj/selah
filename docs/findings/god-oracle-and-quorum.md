- **Finding**: אמן (GV=91=7x13) as God's solo deserves special attention -- the human word of affirmation visible only from across the mercy seat. God affirms; nobody else does.
- **Finding**: בראשית (the first word of Torah) is a God solo. Buried at line 4104 of raw output.
- **Finding**: The unanimous core reveals real structure: verbs of communication are unanimous, content words are perspective-dependent.
- **Finding**: Under independence model, expected unanimous = 233, observed = 642 = 2.76x expected. Heads agree far more than chance (structurally explained by grid constraints).
- **Finding**: The Aaron deficit has geometric explanation: Aaron reads R-to-L within rows (natural Hebrew direction), producing more valid Hebrew words as alternatives, creating more competition for self-weight. Most "Hebrew-compatible" traversal generates fewest eigenwords (2,649 vs ~3,800).

### What Doesn't Hold Up
- **Finding**: "Eigenword" label is misleading -- these are diagonal-maximum entries under inverse-frequency weighting, not eigenvectors of the matrix. The Hannah weighting inverts natural frequency.
- **Finding**: The four heads are not independent. Single-stone illuminations produce identical output for all four. Aaron/God are 180-degree rotations. Right/Left are 180-degree rotations. "Four independent attention heads" framing overstates independence.
- **Finding**: Theological labels ("mercy," "justice," "priestly," "divine") are post-hoc, fit to data not predicted in advance.
- **Finding**: Solo vocabulary presentations are cherry-picked: each head has ~370-990 solo eigenwords, synthesis highlights <1% of actual list. Vast majority are conjugated verbs, prefixed nouns, morphological variants with no obvious theological weight.
- **Finding**: Hebrew morphology inflates apparent coherence: root ח-ט-א (sin) alone generates 13+ mercy solo entries through conjugation. The "vocabulary of redemption" is really a handful of redemptive roots among ~150 distinct concepts.
- **Finding**: The justice cluster is the weakest. בוא (come/enter) is one of most common Hebrew verbs -- semantically neutral.
- **Finding**: God/mercy lamb weight ratio shifted from 1.376 (dictionary) to 1.621 (full) -- 18% change. Qualitative pattern holds but quantitative claim of "identical" is wrong.
- **Finding**: The 87.6% eigenword rate is actually slightly below the independence prediction of 88.3%. Not remarkable -- it's sparsity, not signal.

### The Missing Experiment
- **Finding**: All four reviews converge on the same gap: the grid permutation test. The same control that killed the tensor decomposition (090) had not been applied to head separation. [Note: this was subsequently run as experiment 092.]

### Five Additional Controls Recommended
- **Finding**: Illumination-count correlation (are solos just words with few illumination patterns?). Singleton-letter control (is lamb asymmetry specific to lamb or to kaf being singleton?). Pre-registered predictions. Non-Torah Hebrew text. Density comparison (is density of mercy words in mercy list higher than in other lists?).

## query-engine.md

### Design
- **Finding**: The query engine works backward: given an answer (Hebrew word), find the pre-image -- which stones light up, which reader sees it, how many paths lead to this reading.
- **Finding**: The breastplate model: 12 stones with 6 letters each = 72 positions. Certain positions light up (Urim). Reading order depends on reader (Thummim). One letter per stone lights up per query.
- **Finding**: For 4-letter words: 495 possible stone combinations x 3 reading orders. For 3-letter words: 220 combinations. Computationally tractable.
- **Finding**: Stone 2 is critical: the only source for ח, ע, צ, and ק. Any word using those letters must include stone 2.
- **Finding**: 5 letters appear on exactly one stone each: ג (stone 7), ז (stone 9), ח (stone 2), ט (stone 11), כ (stone 8). These constrain all words containing them.
- **Finding**: The שכרה/כשרה test case: כ only on stone 8. These four letters cannot be drawn from any single column. The mechanism operates at the individual-letter level across the full 72-letter surface.
- **Finding**: The model converges on: letters glow wherever they sit across the grid, and the reader's scan order determines the sequence. Different reading paths through same lit positions produce different words.

## query-process.md

### The Process
- **Finding**: The oracle takes Hebrew letters and finds every way to partition them into real Torah words. No interpretation, no weighting, no ranking. The oracle provides the full menu. The priest chooses.
- **Finding**: The vocabulary is the Torah itself -- word forms that actually appear in the five books of Moses. The partitions are constrained by what the Torah contains.
- **Finding**: Order of input letters does not matter. What matters is the letter multiset (bag of letters and counts).
- **Finding**: The algorithm: build frequency map, search vocabulary for sub-multiset matches, subtract chosen word's letters, recurse, backtrack. All complete partitions are readings.
- **Finding**: Every reading of a given input has the same gematria value (provably necessary -- same letters always sum same total). The GV of a question IS the GV of every answer.
- **Finding**: Three vocabulary levels: :torah (~7,300 full lexicon, default), :voice (~2,050 at knee of limiting distribution), :dict (239 curated). Findings vocabulary-invariant.
- **Finding**: Level 1 (mechanical reading) = breastplate traversals. Level 2 (cognitive assembly) = what the Ramban described. `parse-letters` implements Level 2.
- **Finding**: The oracle is not an interpreter, not random, not weighted, not a cipher. Same input always produces same readings. Rare and common readings have equal standing. The vocabulary is public -- it's the Torah.
