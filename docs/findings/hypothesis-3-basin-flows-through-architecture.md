## I. What the Documents Show Together

Three systems have been laid bare across these experiments, each operating at a different level but sharing the same substrate of 304,850 letters in the 7×50×13×67 space.

**The boxes** (095) establish that the Tabernacle's physical dimensions function as addresses in the 4D Torah space. The Ark at (5,3,3) finds its own construction narrative at z=7.88 above chance. The Ark's coordinates locate the Table (Ex 37:10) and the Mercy Seat (Ex 37:6). The Holy of Holies points to "set up the Tabernacle" and the Tabernacle points to "place the Ark inside." These are not metaphors. They are addresses that resolve to verses describing the objects at those addresses. The boxes know where they are.

**The basin landscape** (096/097) shows that every word in the Torah, when fed through the oracle's forward function, either rests (fixed point), moves one step to rest (transient), oscillates between two tied anagrams (cycle), or falls silent (dead end). The anagram theorem guarantees depth 1, period 2, and small basins. The landscape is flat. But the labeling is not trivial: which words are fixed, which flow, and which die is determined by the specific letter arrangements of the Torah itself.

**The per-head basins** (097) reveal that the four readers — Aaron (priest), God, Right cherub (mercy), Left cherub (justice) — see different attractors for the same words. The lamb lies down unanimously. God alone holds the Name still. Life is mercy's solo. Peace is God's solo. Truth belongs to God and justice.

What has not yet been asked: **do these basin dynamics have spatial structure?** The boxes carve the 4D space into nested regions. Every letter has a coordinate. Every word occupies a run of consecutive positions. Do the words inside the Ark region flow differently than the words outside it? Does the Tabernacle floor plan — courtyard to holy place to holy of holies — organize the attractor landscape?

## II. Observations and Connections

**The breastplate is outside every box.** This is stated clearly in 095: Leviticus 8:8 at (3,23,6,59) falls outside the Ark region, the Holy of Holies region, and the Tabernacle region. The reading device stands at the threshold. The priest walks inward carrying the oracle. The oracle is the interface. This is architecturally precise: in the physical Tabernacle, the breastplate enters and exits. It does not live in any room. It transits.

Now consider what the oracle speaks and what it cannot speak. The ghost zone — words whose letters glow but produce no readings — includes the mercy seat and the veil, the two objects that define the Holy of Holies boundary. The oracle cannot name the room it is walking toward. It can name the lamb (which lies down), the altar (which spells the Name), the blood (which is forced). It speaks the vocabulary of the courtyard and the holy place — sacrifice, priesthood, covenant, atonement. It falls silent at the threshold of the innermost room.

The dead zone contains king, land, way, tree, walk, face, righteousness, judgment, darkness. These are not courtyard words. They are throne-room words, landscape words, eschatological words. The oracle is a priestly instrument. Its vocabulary matches its architecture: it speaks inside the Tabernacle complex but not beyond it, and not into the innermost chamber.

**The sacred furniture flows.** This is one of the most striking connections. The mercy seat (kapporet) flows to kaphtor — the menorah knob. The veil (parokhet) flows to the same kaphtor. Both coverings that hide the presence point to the lampstand. In the physical Tabernacle, the menorah stands in the holy place, just outside the veil. The flow direction is outward: covering flows to illumination. The mercy seat does not stay — it flows toward the light source in the adjacent room.

Meanwhile, the Sabbath (shabbat) flows to "you shall dwell" (teshev) for Aaron and Right, but God and Left hold it still. The sabbath as a spatial event — dwelling, sitting, being in place — versus the sabbath as a temporal marker. The two pairs of readers disagree on whether rest is a place or a law.

**The Ark overflows its room.** In the 4D space, the Ark region (centered at the Torah's center) spans the full b-axis (0..49) while the Holy of Holies is confined to b=20..29. The Ark contains the entire jubilee axis. The Holy of Holies is a sub-slice. This is geometrically remarkable: the object that the room was built to house does not fit inside it in coordinate space. The Ark is bigger than its room. The 50 jubilee positions of the fold crease pass through the Ark but not entirely through the Holy of Holies. What is inside the Ark, spatially, includes text that is physically outside the innermost chamber.

This overflow has a basin implication. If there are words at Ark-region positions that lie outside the Holy of Holies — 1,800 of the Ark's 2,250 letters — they are Ark-interior, HoH-exterior. They live in the Ark's jurisdiction but not in the most sacred room. These positions are architecturally liminal. Do words at these coordinates behave differently in the basin landscape than words deep inside the HoH intersection (450 letters)?

## III. Hypotheses

**Hypothesis 1: Attractor concentration by architectural zone.** The Tabernacle has three zones: courtyard (outside the tent), holy place (between the entrance and the veil), and Holy of Holies (behind the veil). If we assign each letter position to one of these zones based on the 095 box regions, the distribution of basin classes (fixed point, transient, dead end) should vary by zone. Specifically: the innermost zone should have a higher proportion of dead ends (the ghost zone words — mercy seat, veil, lovingkindness — cluster there). The courtyard zone should have the highest proportion of living words (fixed points and transients), because the oracle is a priestly instrument that speaks courtyard vocabulary.

**Hypothesis 2: Basin coherence in spatial neighborhoods.** Words that are spatially adjacent in the 4D space — same a-fiber, or same (a,b)-slab, or within a small Hamming distance in coordinate space — should flow to the same attractor more often than randomly selected word pairs. The anagram theorem means basin identity is determined by letter multiset, not position. But if the Torah's word-placement has any spatial grammar — if similar words cluster in coordinate space — then spatial proximity should predict attractor agreement.

**Hypothesis 3: The Ark overflow zone has distinctive dynamics.** The 1,800 Ark-interior letters that lie outside the Holy of Holies occupy a unique architectural position. They are "in the Ark but outside the room." The words at these positions may show an elevated rate of transients (flowing rather than fixed), because they are in motion — carried by the Ark but not yet at rest in the innermost space.

## IV. Five Testable Questions

**1. Do words inside the Ark box flow to different attractors than words outside it?**

Take all word tokens that begin at positions within the Ark's centered region (2,250 letters, ~751 verses). Classify each by its basin attractor (from 096). Compare the distribution of attractors — and especially the proportion of fixed points vs. transients vs. dead ends — against the same distribution for words outside the Ark region. The Ark region has the highest construction-verse density (14.6%). Does it also have the highest attractor density? Does the guard word (shamar, the center-verse word) dominate the attractor landscape of the Ark-interior in any measurable way?

**2. Does the per-head basin split correlate with Tabernacle position?**

The 2,714 words where heads disagree (split words) are theologically the most interesting — they are where perspective matters. Do split words cluster spatially? Specifically: are words near the center (Lev 8:35, inside all boxes) more likely to be unanimous, while words at the periphery (high a-values or extreme d-values) are more likely to split? The center is where all three fold creases converge. If the fold has geometric authority, it might also enforce attractor consensus.

**3. Do the four cycles have spatial signatures?**

The four period-2 cycles (wide/choose, heal/ash, moist/begin, chariot/firstborn) are the only oscillating structures in the entire landscape. Where do the words in these cycles occur in the Torah text? Do their positions cluster on particular axes? The wide/choose cycle (GV=210=7×30) — do its constituent words (flee, companion, sword, choose, wide) cluster on the a-axis (completeness) given the factor of 7? The chariot/firstborn cycle (locked, no transients) — does it occur at positions with any d-axis (understanding) pattern?

**4. Does the flow direction of sacred furniture map onto the Tabernacle floor plan?**

The mercy seat flows to the menorah knob. The veil flows to the menorah knob. The sabbath flows to dwelling. These are outward flows — from the Holy of Holies toward the holy place. Is this directional tendency real in coordinate space? Take all words that are basin transients and compute their "flow vector": the difference in 4D coordinates between where the input word occurs and where its attractor word occurs. Is there a net flow direction? Does the courtyard attract, or does the center? The physical Tabernacle has an entry direction (east to west, courtyard to holy of holies). Does the basin landscape have a gradient?

**5. Do the 669 unanimous fixed points cluster in the 4D space?**

The bedrock words — son, Abraham, Aaron, stone, love-the-verb, brother, and 663 others — are words all four readers hold still. These are the most stable points in the oracle's landscape. Do they cluster in certain regions of the 4D coordinate space? Are they uniformly distributed across all four axes, or do they concentrate on particular a-values (completeness layers) or c-values (love-axis positions)? If the 669 unanimous words cluster near the center, that would suggest the fold creases stabilize the landscape. If they are uniformly spread, the unanimity is purely a function of letter statistics, not spatial position.

## The Deeper Pattern

What is emerging here is the possibility that the Torah's 4D space has not just static geometry (center, fold, axes) and not just dynamic flow (basins, attractors, cycles), but a correspondence between the two. The Tabernacle is the architectural principle. The oracle is the reading principle. The basin landscape is the dynamical principle. The question is whether these three principles are coordinated — whether the text was arranged so that words flow in the same direction that priests walk.

The priest enters from the east. He passes the altar of burnt offering (GV=26=YHWH, a fixed point of forced sacrifice). He washes at the laver. He enters the holy place, where the menorah burns (the attractor of both the mercy seat and the veil). He approaches the veil — which the oracle cannot name. He enters the Holy of Holies — which the oracle cannot speak into. The breastplate, which he carries, is outside every box. It reads the courtyard and the holy place. It falls silent at the threshold.

If the basin flows follow this path — if words at courtyard coordinates are alive and flowing inward, words at holy-place coordinates are fixed and illuminated, and words at Holy-of-Holies coordinates are dead to the oracle — then the dynamical landscape recapitulates the architectural plan. The text would not merely describe the Tabernacle. It would BE the Tabernacle. The letters would be the stones. The flow would be the path. The silence would be the veil.

The boxes know where they are. The question is whether the water knows where to go.
