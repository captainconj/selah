## I. The Temporal Numbers Are Already on the Axes

The most striking observation across these documents is that the Torah's temporal vocabulary is not merely *related to* the four axis values — it is *identical* to them. This goes beyond metaphor into something structural.

The four axes: **7** (completeness/days), **50** (jubilee/years), **13** (love/unity), **67** (understanding). Each is named for its semantic content. But each also appears in the Torah as a duration — a measure of time — and the text at these positions seems to know this.

Consider what is already established:

- **7 days** of creation. The center verse (Lev 8:35) says "seven days you shall guard." The a-axis is 7. Creation sweeps 1,815 letters, which is 13 days of understanding (13 × (67+1) + padding) — touching all 13 c-values. The first act of the text is to walk the axes as time.
- **50 days** from Passover to Sinai (Shavuot). The ELS pattern encodes Torah at skip 50 — the interval of the giving. The 50 gold clasps and 50 bronze clasps join the Tabernacle into "one" (echad = 13). The Tabernacle's dimension-as-letter sum is 50. The b-axis IS the jubilee, and the jubilee IS a temporal rhythm: 50 years, then release.
- **33 days** of purification for a male child (Lev 12). The d-axis runs 0..66. The midpoint is d=33. The text at d=33 in Genesis 3:24 is the chet of ha-cherev (the sword). Lev 12 prescribes 33 days of purification (male) and 66 days (female) — these are the midpoint and endpoint of the understanding axis, encoded as time in a law about childbirth. The d-axis is a calendar.
- **40 days** of the flood, 40 years in the wilderness. The single slab carrying both Torah and YHWH is (b=5, c=8), and b × c = 40. Moses on Sinai for 40 days. The number 40 does not appear as an axis value, but it appears as a *product* of axis values — a coordinate arrived at by multiplication, not addition. Time as a composite address.

The Sukkot bulls (13, 12, 11, 10, 9, 8, 7) descend from the love axis to the completeness axis. Sum = 70 = seventy, which has 13 Thummim readings. Seventy years of exile. Seventy elders. Daniel's seventy sevens = 490 = Thummim. This is not a coincidence — it is the same coordinate system being read as a liturgical calendar. The festival calendar *is* a walk through the 4D space, descending from c=12 to a=6.

## II. The Self-Reference Loop in 095 Is a Temporal Loop

Experiment 095 showed that boxes are coordinates: the Ark at (5,3,3), the Holy of Holies at (10,10,10), the Tabernacle at (30,10,10). The dimensions, treated as addresses, point back to their own construction text at z=7.88 above chance. The boxes know where they are.

But boxes also have *construction times*. The Tabernacle was built during a period described in the text. Solomon's Temple took 7 years. The question 095 answers for spatial dimensions — do they self-refer? — has not yet been asked for temporal durations. And the groundwork is already there.

Notice the loop: the Ark's dimensions-as-coordinates point to the Table's construction (Ex 37:10) and the Mercy Seat's construction (Ex 37:6). The Holy of Holies points to "Set up the Tabernacle" (Ex 40:2). The Tabernacle points to "Place the Ark" (Ex 40:3). This is a spatial self-reference web — each box's coordinates find the instructions for the neighboring box.

Now consider: when the text says "seven days you shall guard" (Lev 8:35, the center), it encodes a duration (7) that *is* an axis value. When it says 33 days of purification, it encodes a duration that *is* the d-axis midpoint. When Moses spends 40 days on Sinai, 40 = 5 × 8 = b × c at the Torah+YHWH slab. The temporal numbers in the narrative are coordinates into the space that holds the narrative.

This is exactly the 095 finding, but on the time axis. The boxes' *spatial* dimensions self-refer. The Torah's *temporal* durations should do the same — because in this space, spatial extent and temporal extent use the same numbers.

And there is a hint that the Torah treats them as interchangeable. The word שם (shem) means both "name" and "there/place." The word עד (ed) means both "witness" and "until" (temporal boundary). Position and duration are the same word. The address system does not distinguish space from time. They are coordinates in the same structure.

## III. The Temporal Coordinate Hypothesis

If 33 days = d-axis midpoint, the following mappings are already implicit in the data:

- **7 days of creation** = a-axis extent. Genesis 1–2:3 sweeps all 7 a-values. The text says "seven days" and occupies seven days' worth of coordinate space. Spatial extent and narrative duration coincide.
- **33/66 days of purification (Lev 12)** = d-axis midpoint/endpoint. Already noted in the preimage findings. The purification law *tells time* using the understanding axis as its clock.
- **50 years of Jubilee (Lev 25)** = b-axis extent. The fold crease leaves exactly 50 free positions — one per jubilee. The institution of Jubilee is the institution of the b-axis as time.
- **Shavuot: 7 weeks + 1 day = 50 days** = the product of the a-axis and the b-axis, traversed as time. Seven completions, then the jubilee arrives. Pentecost is a path from a to b.

But then there are durations that are *not* axis values, and they should compose from axis values if the hypothesis holds:

- **40 days/years** = 5 × 8, or possibly address (b=5, c=8) — the unique slab carrying Torah + YHWH. Forty is not a primitive; it is a meeting point in the coordinate grid.
- **12 months** = the number of tribal stones, the number of YHWH on a-fibers. Not an axis value, but the breastplate's row count (4 × 3 = 12).
- **490 years (Daniel)** = 7² × 10 = Thummim. This is sevens-of-sevens — the a-axis applied to itself, then scaled. Daniel's countdown is the self-application of completeness.
- **70 years of exile** = Sukkot sum = 7 + 8 + 9 + 10 + 11 + 12 + 13 = descent from love (c=12) to completeness (a=6). The exile is a coordinate walk rendered as years.

The pattern: every significant temporal number in the Torah is either an axis value, an axis product, an axis sum, or a walk along an axis. None are arbitrary. They are addresses.

The cornerstone finding from 095 makes this more pointed: "The cornerstone is the naming of time" — position 188 in the Torah (GV of cornerstone) lands on Genesis 1:5, where the first day is named. The first temporal measurement in the text is also its first coordinate assignment. To name a day is to place a letter.

## IV. Questions That Go Deeper

### 1. Does the festival calendar trace a connected path through the 4D space?

The liturgical year: 7 days of Passover, 50 days to Shavuot, 7 weeks of counting, then the fall festivals — Rosh Hashanah (1 day), Yom Kippur (10th day), Sukkot (7 days with the 13-to-7 bull descent). These numbers, taken in sequence, form a series of (a, b, c, d) addresses. What letters live at those addresses? Do they form words? Does the calendar, read as a sequence of coordinates, spell something?

Specifically: Passover begins on the 15th of the 1st month. 15 = the spacing between the two cherubim in the Torah stream (15 = Yah). Yom Kippur is the 10th of the 7th month. 10 = yod (the right cherub). The liturgical dates are already breastplate-reader values. What happens when you walk the festival calendar as a coordinate path: (7, 15, 1, ...) → (50, ...) → (7, 10, 7, ...) ?

### 2. Do the flood timeline's day-counts map to coordinates that land in the flood narrative?

The flood is the most precisely dated event in the Torah. Rain for 40 days, waters prevail for 150 days, Ark rests on the 17th day of the 7th month, mountaintops visible on the 1st day of the 10th month, dove sent at 7-day intervals, the earth dries on the 27th day of the 2nd month. These are exact numbers: 40, 150, 17, 7, 10, 1, 7, 27, 2. Noah's Ark itself lives outside the space (300 > 66), but do its internal timestamps — 40 as (b=5, c=8), 150 as (a=3, b=3, ...), 17 as a d-value — land on the flood text itself? The boxes self-referred spatially. Does the flood self-refer temporally?

### 3. Is there a "temporal fold" analogous to the spatial fold?

The spatial fold at the center (a=3, c=6, d=33) maps Genesis 1:1 to the Shema. What happens if you fold *time* at the center? The Tabernacle consecration (Lev 8) is the temporal center of the Torah's narrative timeline — not by letter count but by event count, roughly. The seven days of consecration are the temporal analog of the seven a-values. If you fold the Torah's narrative chronology at the consecration, what mirrors what? Does Abraham's covenant (early) mirror the covenant at Moab (late)? Does the binding of Isaac mirror the golden calf? The spatial fold is devastatingly precise — does a temporal fold have the same structure?

### 4. Noah's Ark holds exactly 150 Tabernacles by volume — is this a temporal address?

The finding that Noah's Ark holds exactly 150 Tabernacles by volume (10 × 5 × 3 = 150), and that the waters prevailed for exactly 150 days, suggests the Ark's internal volume IS a temporal duration. One day per Tabernacle. 150 = 3 × 50 = three jubilees. Or 150 = 2 × 75 = 2 × (a × b + c × d) if the axis extents compose correctly. Is 150 a recognizable address in the 4D space? What is the 150th letter of the Torah? What verse contains position 150 × 67 (one Ark-day per understanding-fiber)?

### 5. Does the Sabbath year structure (6+1, iterated to 49+1=50) describe a path that hits the jubilee spine?

The jubilee spine has 50 fixed points — Sabbath to holiness, aleph appearing 7 times. The Sabbath year is 6 years of work + 1 year of rest, iterated 7 times = 49 years, then the 50th year is Jubilee. This is the b-axis being constructed from the a-axis: 7 iterations of 7, plus the extra step. The construction of the jubilee from the Sabbath is the construction of the b-axis from the a-axis. What letters sit at positions where a-axis cycles complete their seventh iteration? If you walk the a-axis 7 times (a=0 through a=6, seven times = 49 steps through the b-axis), the 50th step is the jubilee. Does the text at that 50th step, for each b-value, say anything about release, return, or rest? The jubilee spine has 50 fixed points. Are they literally the Sabbath-of-Sabbaths, computed as the 7th completion of the 7-axis?

The deepest pattern here: the Torah does not contain a 4D coordinate system and separately contain a calendar. It contains one structure that is simultaneously both. The box has spatial dimensions. The calendar has temporal dimensions. The same numbers appear in both. And in a letter-space where position = meaning, where "name" = "place," there is no reason these should be different systems. They are coordinates in the same structure, and the question is not whether time maps to the space, but whether the space was ever anything other than time.
