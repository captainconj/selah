## II. The Proportions of the Cross

The cross has four arms of unequal length. Let us compute the ratios systematically.

**Cardinal totals:**
- East (E) = 186,400
- West (W) = 108,100
- North (N) = 157,600
- South (S) = 151,450

**Axis sums and ratios:**
- East-West axis: E + W = 294,500
- North-South axis: N + S = 309,050
- NS/EW = 309,050 / 294,500 = 1.04940...
- E/W = 186,400 / 108,100 = 1.72340... (compare: sqrt(3) = 1.73205...)
- N/S = 157,600 / 151,450 = 1.04060...

The EW asymmetry is dramatic: East is 1.724x West. The NS asymmetry is mild: North is only 1.041x South.

**The near-miss with sqrt(3).** E/W = 1.7234 vs sqrt(3) = 1.7321. The error is 0.50%. This is close enough to notice but not close enough to assert. If the Torah intended sqrt(3), the counts would need to be adjusted by ~940 men on one side. That said: sqrt(3) is the ratio of height to half-base in an equilateral triangle. It appears in hexagonal close-packing — the geometry of the honeycomb, the breastplate stones. Worth noting. Not conclusive.

**The golden ratio test.** phi = 1.6180. E/W = 1.7234. Not close. The cross proportions are NOT the golden section.

**What IS 186,400 / 108,100?** Simplify: both are divisible by 100, giving 1,864 / 1,081. GCD(1864, 1081) = 1. These are coprime. The ratio is irreducible. No clean fraction. But: 1,864 = 8 x 233 and 1,081 = 23 x 47. The factor 233 is the 13th Fibonacci number. And 1,081 = T(46) is the 46th triangular number. These are not obviously related, but 233 being F(13) and the c-axis being 13 is notable.

**Camp totals modulo axis values:**

Mod 7 (completeness):
- 186,400 mod 7 = 4 — East
- 151,450 mod 7 = 5 — South
- 108,100 mod 7 = 6 — West
- 157,600 mod 7 = 2 — North

No camp divides by 7. But the remainders form a striking pattern: East=4, South=5, West=6, North=2. The three camps that march in order (E, S, W) produce the consecutive sequence 4, 5, 6 — three adjacent values on the a-axis. North (Dan, the rear guard) breaks the pattern at 2. The marching camps count upward on the completeness axis; the guard stands apart.

Mod 13 (love):
- 186,400 mod 13 = 6 — East (the center of the c-axis!)
- 151,450 mod 13 = 0 (!) — South divides exactly by love
- 108,100 mod 13 = 5 — West
- 157,600 mod 13 = 1 — North

**South divides by 13.** Reuben's camp (Reuben, Simeon, Gad) aligns perfectly with the love axis. The firstborn's camp sits at c=0 modulo love. And East sits at c=6 — the center of love, the axis midpoint.

The diagonal pairs are symmetric in love: E+S mod 13 = 6+0 = 6 (the love center), W+N mod 13 = 5+1 = 6 (also the love center). The camps that touch in the corners of the cross — the ones joined by the march sequence (East flows to South, West flows to North) — each sum to the midpoint of love. The cross-opposed pairs (E+W = 11, N+S = 1) do not balance.

Mod 67 (understanding):
- 186,400 mod 67 = 6 — East
- 151,450 mod 67 = 30 — South
- 108,100 mod 67 = 29 — West
- 157,600 mod 67 = 16 — North

No camp divides by 67. But South (30) and West (29) are adjacent: the firstborn and Rachel's sons sit side by side on the understanding axis. And East mod 67 = 6 — the same as its mod 13 residue. Judah's camp occupies position 6 in both love and understanding simultaneously.

- 186,400 mod 50 = 0 (!) — East divides by jubilee
- 108,100 mod 50 = 0 (!) — West divides by jubilee
- 157,600 mod 50 = 0 (!) — North divides by jubilee
- 151,450 mod 50 = 0 (!) — South divides by jubilee

ALL four camps divide by 50. Every camp count is a multiple of the jubilee axis. This is not surprising given every tribal count is divisible by 50 (since each is divisible by 100). But it is structurally important: the entire cross is quantized in jubilee units.

## III. The Cross as a 2D Projection

| a x c | 7 x 13 | 1.86 | completeness x love |
| a x d | 7 x 67 | 9.57 | completeness x understanding |
| b x c | 50 x 13 | 3.85 | jubilee x love |
| b x d | 50 x 67 | 1.34 | jubilee x understanding |
| c x d | 13 x 67 | 5.15 | love x understanding |

The cross is not a rectangle — it is a cross: four arms radiating from a center. But the cross has an aspect ratio defined by its extent in each direction:

Cross NS/EW extent ratio = 309,050 / 294,500 = 1.0494

Now: 50/67 = 0.746, and 67/50 = 1.34. The b x d projection has aspect ratio 1.34. The c x d projection gives 13/67 = 0.194. None match 1.05 directly.

But consider this differently. The cross is not the projection of the hyperrectangle's edges. The cross is the projection of its *mass*. The tribal counts are weights, not lengths. A better question: if you assign each camp to a pair of axes, do the camp masses correspond to areas (products of axis ranges)?

  - But: **186,400 = 7 x 26,628.57** — no. **186,400 / 7 = 26,628.57** — not integral.

Wait: 186,400 = 2⁵ x 5² x 233. And 26,628 is not clean. Let me try differently.

**The mass is not the area. The mass is the population.** Perhaps the ratio of populations encodes axis ratios in a different way. Consider:

E / W = 186,400 / 108,100 = 1.7234

However: **(13 - 7 + 50) / 50 = 56/50 = 1.12.** Not right either.

Let me try composite ratios. The cross has 4 arms. The 4D space has 4 axes with values 7, 50, 13, 67. What if each arm corresponds to an axis?

- East = Judah = first to march, strongest. Assign axis b (50, jubilee)? Count 186,400.

Normalize to the smallest: 108,100 (West) and 7 (smallest axis):
- 186,400/108,100 = 1.724 vs 50/7 = 7.143 — no.

That assignment does not produce proportional counts. The camp populations are NOT directly proportional to axis values. This rules out the simplest projection model.

## IV. The Levites at the Center: 22,000 and the Alphabet

The Levites number 22,000 in the first census (Numbers 3:39). They camp around the Tabernacle at the center of the cross.

22,000 = 2⁴ x 5³ x 11

But the decomposition that matters: **22 x 1,000**. The Hebrew alphabet has **22 letters**. The Torah is written in 22 letters. The Tabernacle IS a text — the word תבה (ark/word) says so. The Levites who guard the text number one-thousand per letter of the alphabet.

Is there a coordinate connection? The center of the 4D space is (3, 25, 6, 33) = Lev 8:35. The strides are [43,550; 871; 67; 1]. The center's linear position is:

3 x 43,550 + 25 x 871 + 6 x 67 + 33 = 130,650 + 21,775 + 402 + 33 = 152,860

The "center region" — the letters closest to the center — extends in each direction. How many letters are within 11,000 of the center in each direction? 22,000 letters = 11,000 on each side of center = a slab of 22,000 consecutive letters centered on position 152,860.

22,000 / 67 = 328.358... d-slabs. Not clean.
22,000 / 871 = 25.258... c-fibers. Not clean.
But: **22,000 / (13 x 67) = 22,000 / 871 = 25.26** — close to 25, the center b-value.

And: 22 x 871 = 19,162. Not 22,000. But: **22 x 1,000 = 22,000**, and 1,000 is the cube root of the total fighting men's order of magnitude.

The deeper connection is structural, not arithmetic. The center of the 4D space is guarded by the center verse (Lev 8:35). The center of the cross is guarded by the Levites. Both guard systems use the same verb: שמר. Both occupy the innermost position. The Levites are the physical instantiation of what the center verse describes: "you shall remain at the entrance of the Tent of Meeting day and night for seven days, and guard the charge of the LORD."

**22 letters. 22,000 guards.** Each letter has a thousand men. The Torah has 304,850 letters. 304,850 / 22,000 = 13.857... which rounds to approximately 13.86. Not exactly 13, but tantalizingly close to the c-axis value. If you round: the Torah has about 13 to 14 letter-lengths per Levite-unit.

More precisely: 304,850 / 22 = 13,856.8 letters per alphabet-letter. And 13,856.8 / 1,000 = 13.857. The Torah holds approximately 13,857 copies of each letter (on average). The c-axis (love) is 13. Each Levite guards approximately 13.86 letters. Love is the guard density.

## V. The Breastplate as the Folded Cross

The breastplate has 12 stones arranged in a 4 x 3 grid (Exodus 28:17-20). Each stone carries a tribal name. The cross has 4 camps of 3 tribes each. The bijection:

| Breastplate Row | Cross Camp | Tribes |
|----------------|------------|--------|
| Row 1 (Exodus 28:17) | East | Judah, Issachar, Zebulun |
| Row 2 (Exodus 28:18) | South | Reuben, Simeon, Gad |
| Row 3 (Exodus 28:19) | West | Ephraim, Manasseh, Benjamin |
| Row 4 (Exodus 28:20) | North | Dan, Asher, Naphtali |

The breastplate maps rows to compass directions. But the breastplate hangs vertically on the priest's chest. It has a physical top and bottom (rows 1-4) and a physical left and right (columns 1-3). The priest faces east. So the top row = east (what he faces), the bottom = north (Dan at the rear). When the priest stands at the Tabernacle entrance facing east, his breastplate IS the cross — not as a spatial map but as a vertical compression of the horizontal layout.

Now the coordinate system: the breastplate encodes two axes of the 4D space (experiment 068):
- **13 patriarchal letters** (Abraham, Isaac, Jacob) map to the **c-axis** (love)
- **50 tribal letters** map to the **b-axis** (jubilee)

Selecting one patriarchal letter and one tribal letter gives a (b, c) pair, which determines a 7 x 67 = 469-letter slab — one completeness-span of understanding. The breastplate is, literally, a 2D address selector for the 4D Torah space. It projects from 4D to 2D by fixing b and c, then reads the remaining a x d slab.

The cross does the same thing from the other direction. The cross arranges 12 tribes (= 12 b-axis ranges of the breastplate) into 4 compass directions. Each direction collapses three b-ranges into one cardinal orientation. The cross is a further projection: from the breastplate's 2D (b, c) space down to 1D per axis (just the cardinal direction). The cross loses information — you can no longer tell which of the three eastern tribes is speaking. The breastplate preserves it (each stone is distinct).

**The folding:** Imagine the cross with its four arms flat on the desert floor. Now fold the four arms upward and press them together into a 4 x 3 grid. The east arm (3 tribes) becomes row 1. The south arm becomes row 2. And so on. The cross folds into the breastplate. The breastplate unfolds into the cross.

This means the 4D coordinate system has THREE physical manifestations in the Torah:
1. **The letter stream itself** — 304,850 letters in 7 x 50 x 13 x 67
2. **The breastplate** — 72 letters on a 4 x 3 grid, selecting (b, c) slabs
3. **The tribal encampment** — 12 tribes in a cross, the breastplate unfolded onto the desert

The breastplate is the interface between the Torah-as-text and the tribes-as-body. It reads the 4D space. It is worn on the body. It maps to the encampment. It is the projection operator.

## VIII. Toward Experiment: Testing the Projection Hypothesis

For all four camps: compute the coordinate (count mod 7, count mod 50, count mod 13, count mod 67) and look up what text sits there. If the camps find their own arrangement narrative (Numbers 2), the cross is self-referencing just as the boxes were.

## IX. Synthesis

The tribal cross is not the 4D space in any simple projective sense. The camp populations are not proportional to axis values. The cross ratios do not match axis ratios directly. The naive model — that the cross IS a shadow of the hyperrectangle — fails at the first test.

But something subtler is true. The cross interacts with the 4D space through modular arithmetic:
- The South camp (Reuben) is 0 mod 13 — the firstborn aligns perfectly with the love axis.
- The East camp (Judah) is 6 mod 13 AND 6 mod 67 — Judah sits at the love-center in both love and understanding.
- The marching camps (E, S, W) have mod-7 residues 4, 5, 6 — three consecutive values, counting upward.
- All four camps divide by 50 (the jubilee quantizes everything).
- The total is 0 mod 50 but hits bedrock at 12,071 (prime).
- The change between censuses is 0 mod (7 x 13) — the shift is quantized by love-completeness.
- The march's cumulative sums mod 7 descend (4, 2, 1) then the rear guard pulls to center (3).
- The march's cumulative sums mod 13 dwell at the love-center (6, 6) then sweep outward (11, 12).

The cross and the 4D space share a modular skeleton. They are not the same structure projected, but the same structure *quantized differently*. The 4D space quantizes 304,850 letters into 7 x 50 x 13 x 67 positions. The cross quantizes 603,550 men into 4 camps. Both quantizations respect the same prime factors (7, 13) and the same jubilee unit (50). The census shift respects both. The march halves its way down the completeness axis (4 -> 2 -> 1) before the rear guard pulls it to center (3). On the love axis, it dwells at the midpoint (6, 6) before sweeping outward (11, 12). The assembled army stands at a=3 (center of completeness), c=12 (edge of love). The army is centered in completeness but stands at the boundary of love — love extended to its full range.

The breastplate is the Rosetta Stone between the two: it is simultaneously a 2D selector in the 4D letter-space (choosing b and c to read an a x d slab) and a physical representation of the 12 tribes (one stone per tribe). When the priest wears the breastplate and stands at the Tabernacle entrance, he holds the coordinate system on his chest. The tribes arranged around him in the desert reproduce the same 12-fold structure at human scale. The letters inside the Torah reproduce it at symbolic scale.

Three scales. One structure. The letter, the stone, the camp.

The most striking finding is the second census convergence. The first census cross is lopsided: E/W = 1.724, N/S = 1.041. The generation that left Egypt carries an asymmetric burden — Judah vastly outweighs Ephraim, while north and south nearly balance. The generation that enters the land shifts: Simeon collapses (the violence of Genesis 34 echoing forward), Manasseh surges, and the cross settles into near-isotropic asymmetry: E/W = 1.539, N/S = 1.533, both converging on (7+13)/13 = 20/13. The wilderness generation's cross evolves toward a ratio built from the two axes that name the census shift itself. The cross doesn't just sit in the coordinate system — it *learns* the coordinate system over the 38 years of wandering.
