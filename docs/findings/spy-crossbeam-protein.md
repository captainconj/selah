## Honesty Section: What a Skeptic Would Say

A skeptic would correctly note:

1. **22 triplets from a 67-letter sequence is a choice.** We chose to read 67 as 22x3+1. We could equally read it as 16x4+3 (16 quadruplets) or 33x2+1 (33 pairs). The triplet reading is motivated by the genetic code analogy, not by the data itself.

2. **The rank mapping is arbitrary.** Sorting 22 GVs and 20 amino acids by their respective scales and then mapping rank-to-rank is always possible. It doesn't prove correspondence.

3. **The 506 bridge could be coincidence.** With 11 mirror-pairs, the probability of any 3 consecutive pairs sharing a sum depends on the distribution. A permutation test would be needed to assess significance.

4. **Helix-turn-helix is one of the most common protein motifs.** Finding a vague HTH signature in a 22-residue sequence is not remarkable.

5. **The G-C = את result is cute but relies on a mapping that was chosen to make the adenine case work.** If we mapped differently, the sums would change.

These are fair critiques. The findings are suggestive and structurally rich, but they do not constitute proof. They constitute **observations that reward further investigation**.

The 506 bridge and the 676 = 26^2 flanking are the most testable claims. A permutation test shuffling the 22 GV values and checking how often three consecutive mirror-pairs share a sum would give a p-value. That test should be run.
