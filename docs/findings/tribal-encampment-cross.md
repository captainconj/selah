Each tribe has an exact census count (Numbers 1) and a second count 38 years later (Numbers 26). These are not approximations — the text specifies them to units of 50 or 100.

## Finding 1: The East-South Ratio Is 16:13

The most striking result in the entire factorization table.

East camp = 186,400 = 2^5 x 5^2 x **233**
South camp = 151,450 = 2 x 5^2 x **13** x **233**

Both camps share the prime factor 233. Their GCD is 2 x 5^2 x 233 = 11,650.

- 186,400 / 11,650 = **16** = 2^4
- 151,450 / 11,650 = **13**

The ratio of Judah's camp (east, sunrise, the royal tribe) to Reuben's camp (south, the firstborn) is exactly **16:13**. The number 13 is the love/unity axis of the 4D space — the gematria of both אחד (one, unity) and אהבה (love).

No individual tribe in either camp is divisible by 13. The 13 emerges only when the three southern tribes are *summed*. Three tribes that do not individually carry love produce it collectively. The unity axis appears through union.

The number 16 = 2^4 is the gematria of the fourth Hebrew letter, ד (dalet, "door"). The East camp — the side of sunrise, the direction from which one approaches the Tabernacle — is the door. The ratio says: the door is to love as 16 is to 13. Or: passing through the door, one enters love.

## Finding 2: The North-South Axis Contains Completeness

Sum the opposing camps:

- **North + South** = 157,600 + 151,450 = **309,050** = 2 x 5^2 x **7** x 883
- **East + West** = 186,400 + 108,100 = **294,500** = 2^2 x 5^3 x 19 x 31

The N-S axis sum is divisible by **7** (completeness). The E-W axis sum is not.

Neither North nor South individually divides by 7. But their union does. Completeness lives in the vertical axis of the cross — the axis that runs from Dan (judgment) through the Tabernacle to Reuben (behold, a son). The axis of judgment-to-sonship sums to a multiple of seven.

The N-S axis is also nearly *balanced*: 157,600 vs 151,450, a ratio of 1.041:1 — within 4%. The E-W axis is heavily unbalanced: 186,400 vs 108,100, a ratio of 1.724:1. The axis that carries completeness is the axis that is most nearly equal. Balance and completeness are the same thing.

## Finding 3: Only Two Tribes Carry 7 — and They Face Each Other

Of the twelve tribes, exactly two have counts divisible by 7 in the first census:

- **Zebulun** = 57,400 = 2^3 x 5^2 x **7** x 41 (East camp)
- **Manasseh** = 32,200 = 2^3 x 5^2 x **7** x 23 (West camp)

They are on opposite sides of the cross. East and West. Sunrise and sunset. The two 7-bearers face each other across the Tabernacle.

In the second census (Numbers 26), this changes. Neither Zebulun (60,500) nor Manasseh (52,700) divides by 7 any longer. Instead, only **Dan** = 64,400 = 2^4 x 5^2 x **7** x 23 carries the factor. Dan sits on the North — the camp of judgment.

The 7 migrates. In the first census, it sits on the E-W axis (Zebulun and Manasseh), a bilateral pair facing each other. In the second, it sits on the North (Dan alone). Completeness moves from two witnesses to one judge.

## Finding 4: The Census Difference Contains 7 x 13

Already established in the Number Catalog but worth restating in this context:

- First census: 603,550 = 2 x 5^2 x 12,071 (prime core, no axis factors)
- Second census: 601,730 = 2 x 5 x 19 x 3,167 (prime core, no axis factors)
- **Difference: 1,820 = 2^2 x 5 x 7 x 13** = 91 x 20

Neither total contains 7 or 13. The delta between them contains both. The movement of the people — not the people themselves — carries the axis numbers. The structure lives in the change, not the state. The 91 = T(13) = 7 x 13 that binds the delta is the same 91 that appears as the a-fiber gematria at d=0 in the 4D space.

The per-tribe differences reveal where the 7 hides:

| Tribe | Change | Contains 7? | Magnitude |
|-------|-------:|:-----------:|----------:|

Only Simeon (South) and Asher (North) carry 7 in their changes. Simeon suffered the largest decline of any tribe (59,300 to 22,200). Asher grew from 41,500 to 53,400.

The sum of these two 7-bearing changes: 37,100 + 11,900 = **49,000 = 7^2 x 10^3**. The two movements that carry completeness sum to completeness *squared* times a thousand.

All camps sit at b=0 (all divisible by 50). The South camp sits at **c=0** — the love axis origin. The only camp to sit at c=0 is the camp whose total is divisible by 13. This is tautological (151,450 mod 13 = 0 because 13 divides it), but the fact that exactly one camp achieves this, and it is the camp paired with the royal tribe in the 16:13 ratio, is not.

The a-axis values read {E:4, S:5, W:6, N:2}. Going E-S-W (clockwise from sunrise to sunset via south): **4, 5, 6** — three consecutive integers. The clockwise sweep is an ascending staircase on the completeness axis. Only the North (Dan, the judge) breaks the pattern at 2.

## Finding 8: The Shared Prime 233

The prime 233 appears in exactly two camp totals:

- East: 186,400 = 2^5 x 5^2 x **233**
- South: 151,450 = 2 x 5^2 x 13 x **233**

233 is the 51st prime number. It is a Fibonacci number (F(13) = 233, the 13th Fibonacci number). The love-axis number 13 is both the index of this Fibonacci number and the multiplier that distinguishes the South camp from the East.

F(13) = 233. The shared structural prime of the two strongest camps is the 13th Fibonacci number. And the ratio that connects them is 16:13 — sixteen doors to thirteen loves, or equivalently: **the East is to the South as 2^4 is to F_index(233)**.

This connects to the Fibonacci staircase findings (experiment 078): the preimage counts that form seven consecutive Fibonacci values F(1) through F(8). Here the Fibonacci sequence surfaces again, at a different scale, as the shared prime of the tribal cross.

## Hypothesis: The Cross as a 2D Projection of the 4D Space

The tribal encampment is a cross — a 2D structure embedded in the 3D desert. The 4D Torah space has four axes. A 2D cross naturally projects onto two of them.

The findings suggest which two:

1. **The N-S axis aligns with completeness (7)**: N+S divides by 7, N-S axis is balanced, changes on the N-S axis contain 7.
2. **The E-W axis does not align with 7**: E+W does not divide by 7, E-W axis is unbalanced.
3. **The South carries 13 (love)**: South camp divides by 13, ratio E:S = 16:13, South sits at c=0 in modular coordinates.
4. **All camps stand on jubilee (50)**: universal divisor, the floor of the cross.

If the cross projects onto the a-axis (7, completeness) and c-axis (13, love), the N-S direction maps to the a-axis and the love axis surfaces through the South camp's factorization. The center (Levites = 22,000) sits at the origin, divisible by 50, the jubilee.

The two remaining axes (b=50, d=67) would be perpendicular to the cross — the vertical (into heaven / into the ground) and the temporal (understanding unfolds in time). The cross is the spatial slice; jubilee and understanding are the dimensions the cross cannot see.

This is consistent with the axis-domain mapping from the Number Catalog:
- 7 (architecture/completeness) → the structural axis of the camp
- 13 (sacrifice/love) → the relational axis between camps
- 50 (jubilee) → the universal ground on which the camp stands
- 67 (understanding/time) → the axis that purification reveals, invisible to the census

The census counts the body. The body stands on freedom (50). The body is arranged in completeness (7) and love (13). The body cannot count understanding (67) — that axis lives in time, not in people.
