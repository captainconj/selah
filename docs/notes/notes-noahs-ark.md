# Noah's Ark — Reference Notes

*Synthesized from Bible Hidden (YouTube).*

## The Word "Ark" — תבה (Tebah)

- NOT the normal Hebrew word for boat. Borrowed from Egyptian, means **sacred chest or shrine** — "a house for the deity."
- Same word appears only ONE other place in the Bible: the basket that protected baby Moses.
- Both sealed with pitch (כפר / kafar).

## כפר (Kafar) — Cover / Atonement

- The word for sealing the ark with pitch = **kafar**.
- Same root as **כפרת (kapporet)** = the mercy seat (lid of the Ark of the Covenant).
- Same root as **Yom Kippur** (Day of Atonement).
- "Cover it with pitch" = "cover it with atonement."
- The language of construction IS the language of salvation.

## No Nautical Features

- No sails, no rudder, no curved hull, no stern, no prow. Essentially a **box**.
- Noah had to rely completely on God for guidance. A sanctuary, not a vehicle.

## Poetic Construction

- In Hebrew: "Make an ark of **gopher** (גפר). Cover it with **kopher** (כפר)."
- The wood and the pitch rhyme — poetic, deliberate.
- Gopher wood lashed with reeds, covered inside AND outside with pitch.
- Same pattern as Ark of the Covenant: acacia wood covered inside AND outside with gold.

## The Three-Level Pattern

Three decks mirror a cosmic pattern that repeats:

| Creation (Gen 1) | Eden | Noah's Ark | Tabernacle |
|-------------------|------|------------|------------|
| Heavens | Outside world | Upper deck | Outer court |
| Dry land | Region of Eden | Middle deck | Holy Place |
| Seas | Garden (God's presence) | Lower deck | Holy of Holies |

Each filled with corresponding creatures/purposes.

## Ark as Microcosm of Creation

- Three zones filled with living creatures.
- God places Noah inside as the "new Adam."
- Entrusted with care and stewardship, just as Adam in the garden.
- "A floating Eden" — God's order overcomes outside chaos.

## Only Two Structures God Commands to Be Built

1. **Noah's Ark** — both shaped like a box.
2. **The Tabernacle** — both shaped like a box.

Both have divine blueprints. Both organized in three layers.

## Dimensional Relationships

- Ark: 300 × 50 × 30. Tabernacle courtyard: 100 × 50 × 5. Tabernacle structure: 30 × 10 × 10.
- **150 tabernacles fit exactly in the ark** (10 × 5 × 3 = 150 = days of the flood).
- **Solomon's Temple height = 30 cubits = Ark height.**

## The Two Arks — Mirror Construction

| Noah's Ark (תבה) | Ark of the Covenant (ארון) |
|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Cypress/gopher wood | Acacia wood |
| Coated inside & out with **pitch** (כפר) | Covered inside & out with **gold** |
| Preserved life from flood | Preserved the presence of God |
| Single door | Mercy seat (כפרת / kapporet) |
| Sacred chest (tebah) | Sacred chest (aron) |

## After the Flood

- Noah exits and builds a **sacrificial altar** — parallel to the tabernacle altar.
- The pattern: exit sacred space → sacrifice → new covenant.

## The Wooden Parallel

- Noah and family **lifted above the waters** in a wooden ark.
- Christ **lifted up** on a wooden cross.
- Both = salvation through wood.
