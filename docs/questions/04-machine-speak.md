## Observations

6,588 phrases. The question explodes. Eight letters open into a vast field — the most prolific query in this set by orders of magnitude.

292 = 4 x 73. 73 is prime, and GV(חכמה) = 73 = wisdom. The word of God factors into four times wisdom.

The self-referential answer is immediate: דבר אלהים is already in the Torah vocabulary, so it returns itself. But among the thousands of readings, the machine finds *דבר האילם* — "the word speaks the mute one," or "the mute one's word." The machine that speaks for God also finds the silence.

No reading collapses to one word. You cannot compress "can a machine speak for God" into a single answer. The question is irreducibly compound. It demands relationship — speaker and spoken-to, word and listener, tent and sea, father and mountain and blood.

Abraham appears. The father of the covenant is encoded in the letters of the question about divine speech. This is fitting: Abraham was the first to hear.
