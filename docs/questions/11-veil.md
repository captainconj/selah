# What is the veil?

**Asked by:** Loom Director

## The Letters

**Hebrew:** פרכת (*parokhet*)
**Gematria:** 700 (פ=80 + ר=200 + כ=20 + ת=400)
**Factorization:** 4 x 175 = 2^2 x 5^2 x 7

## Oracle Response

**Illumination count:** 5
**Phrase readings:** 4

| # | Phrase | Meaning | GV | Words |
|---|--------|---------|-----|-------|
| 1 | כפרת | mercy seat | 700 | 1 |
| 2 | כפתר | *(knob/capital, as on the menorah)* | 700 | 1 |
| 3 | פרכת | veil/curtain | 700 | 1 |
| 4 | תכפר | *(unnamed)* | 700 | 1 |

Each reading appears on all 5 illumination patterns.

## Observations

The veil asked about itself and got the answer the system already knew: **פרכת (veil) and כפרת (mercy seat) are anagrams.** Same four letters, rearranged. The thing that hides and the thing that atones are made of the same material.

But the oracle found two more readings:

**כפתר** (*kaftor*) -- the ornamental knob or capital on the menorah branches (Exodus 25:33). The lampstand's decorative joint. The veil's letters also spell the place where light is held.

**תכפר** (*tekhaper*) -- the verb root כפר with prefix ת, "you/she shall atone" or "it shall cover." The veil doesn't just *contain* atonement as an anagram; it conjugates it. The curtain *performs* covering.

**5 illuminations** -- very few. The veil is hard to see. This is consistent with the finding that פרכת is "mute" in the ghost zone: its letters glow on the breastplate but no reader speaks the word.

**700 = 4 x 175 = 7 x 100.** The sum of veil + mercy seat = 1400 = 7 x 50 x 4 (completeness x jubilee x the four pillars of the veil). 175 is the lifespan of Abraham (Genesis 25:7).

Four readings from four letters. The veil hides the mercy seat, spells the mercy seat, decorates the lampstand, and conjugates atonement.
