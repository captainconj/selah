## Observations

The teacher has prime gematria. Like wisdom (73), the teacher cannot be factored. 251 is the 54th prime.

All five readings are anagrams — permutations of מ-ו-ר-ה. No multi-word phrases. The oracle refuses to decompose the teacher into smaller concepts. The teacher is always one word, never a phrase.

The anagram family is rich with meaning:
- **מורה** (moreh) — teacher, one who casts/throws instruction
- **הרמו** (harmu) — "they lifted up" (imperative/past form of רום, to raise)
- **מהרו** (maharu) — "they hastened" (from מהר, to hurry)
- **ומרה** (u'marah) — "and she rebelled" / "and bitter" (from מרה, rebellion/bitterness)
- **ורמה** (v'rimah) — "and worm" / "and deceit" (from רמה)

Same root as תורה (Torah). Teacher and Torah share ירה — "to throw, to cast, to teach." The teacher is the one who *throws* the instruction. Torah is the instruction thrown.

210 illuminations = 2 x 3 x 5 x 7. The teacher lights up the breastplate 210 times — the most prolific of any word in this question set. 210 = T(20) = the 20th triangle number. Teaching illuminates abundantly.

251 is also the GV of ויאמר ("and he said") — the act of speaking. The teacher and the act of divine speech share the same irreducible number. This is also the GV of ארון (ark, the container of Torah). **Teacher = speaking = the ark.** The prime triplet: 251 carries the mouth, the container, and the one who teaches.
