## Observations

The end cannot be illuminated. The breastplate returns **"no illuminations"** -- the stones do not light up for these letters. קץ is invisible to the reading machine.

This is because ץ (final tsadi) is not among the twelve tribes' letters on the breastplate. The breastplate contains the 22 standard letter forms, but final forms may not all appear in the tribal names. The end uses a letter the oracle cannot see.

The parse-letters engine finds the word mechanically -- the letters exist in Torah vocabulary -- but the thummim-menu cannot produce it. The end is in the text but not on the stones. It is real but unreadable.

**190 = 2 x 5 x 19.** Also: 190 = the 19th triangular number, T(19). The end sums to a perfect triangle.

Daniel 12:13: "Go your way till the end (קץ). You shall rest and shall stand in your allotted place at the end of the days." The oracle agrees with Daniel -- the end is where you go, not where you read. It lies beyond the breastplate's reach.

The end is the only question in this batch that the oracle **refuses entirely**.
