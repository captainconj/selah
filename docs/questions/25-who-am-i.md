# Question 25: Who am I? — מי אני

Letters: **מ י א נ י** | GV = **111**

660 illuminations. Level 1: all four heads read the 5 letters as a single string — no Torah word matches. The breastplate glows but the heads are silent. Level 2 (Thummim phrase assembly): 4 phrases. All GV=111.

| Phrase | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| **מי אני** | Who am I? |
| **אני מי** | I am who |
| **ימי נא** | My days, please |
| **נא ימי** | Please, my days |

The oracle echoes the question back — "who am I?" becomes "I am who." The only other reading: **ימי נא** — *my days, please*. The answer to "who am I?" is: you are your days. Your identity is your time. And the נא — the particle of entreaty, the same "please" hiding inside אנכי (I AM) and אדני (Lord). Who am I = my days, please.

GV = 111 = אלף = **aleph**. The name of the first letter. The silent one.
