## Notable 5D Spaces

**#11 — The canonical with jubilee split: 5 × 7 × 10 × 13 × 67**
Sum = 102. This IS the canonical 4D space (7 × 50 × 13 × 67) with 50 = 5 × 10 split into He and Yod. Five named axes. The minimum axis sum of any 5D decomposition. The jubilee becomes its two YHWH letters.

**#7 — The witness-completeness-love-understanding space: 2 × 7 × 13 × 25 × 67**
Sum = 114. Four of the canonical axes plus witness. Jubilee halved to 25 (center of jubilee). All five axes carry theological meaning.

**#10 — David's five: 5 × 5 × 13 × 14 × 67**
Sum = 104. He, He, love, David, understanding. David (14 = יד = hand) gets his own dimension alongside the two He's (grace, grace) and the silent axes.

**#9 — The Name in five: 5 × 5 × 7 × 26 × 67**
Sum = 110. He, He, completeness, YHWH, understanding. The tetragrammaton (26) gets its own axis.

**#3 — Witness to the angel: 2 × 5 × 5 × 67 × 91**
Sum = 170. Understanding (67) and angel (91 = מלאך = triangle of 13) each get their own axis.

## The Narrowing

At 5D, we can see the constraint tightening. With 6 prime factors (2, 5, 5, 7, 13, 67), filling 5 non-trivial slots means at most one composite axis. Only 11 arrangements work. The space of possible coordinate systems is shrinking toward the prime factorization.
