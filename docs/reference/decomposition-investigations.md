## Investigation Categories

### 1. Centers

Every decomposition has a geometric center. In 4D canonical, center = (3,25,6,33) = Lev 8:35 ("seven days, guard the charge of the LORD").

- Compute center verse for every non-trivial decomposition (2D through 6D)
- Do any centers converge on the same verse or passage?
- The 5D canonical (5×7×10×13×67) center: (2,3,5,6,33) — still Leviticus 8?
- The 6D prime factorization center: (1,2,2,3,6,33) — where?
- Map which book/chapter each center lands in
- Look for clusters — multiple spaces centering on the same region

### 2. Folds

The Torah folded in half at letter 152,425. In 4D, three fold creases converge on the breastplate passage. The fold produced statistically significant ELS and aleph-tav alignments.

- The half-fold point is constant (letter 152,425) but its coordinates change per space
- Compute fold-point coordinates in every decomposition
- In 2D: fold is a line. In 3D: fold is a plane. In 4D: fold is a hyperplane.
- **The fold test**: run the same statistical tests (ELS alignment, aleph-tav clustering) across all non-trivial spaces. If the fold only works in 4D canonical — that's selection. If it works in many — that's deeper.
- Do fold creases converge on the same region across spaces?

### 3. Aleph-Tavs (את)

Aleph-tav positions are fixed in the linear text. Their coordinates change per decomposition.

- In 4D canonical, they cluster at division boundaries. Do they cluster at boundaries in other spaces?
- Test aleph-tav boundary alignment for each axis of each decomposition
- Which decompositions show significant aleph-tav structure?
- The fold-aligned aleph-tavs — do they still align in non-canonical spaces?

### 4. ELS (Equidistant Letter Sequences)

A skip-k ELS walks along specific axes depending on the decomposition. Skip-7 walks the d-axis in canonical, but the a-axis in 7×67×650.

- Do ELS at skip distances matching axis values become more meaningful when the axis matches?
- 7-skip, 13-skip, 50-skip, 67-skip: test in each space where that value IS an axis vs where it isn't
- 871-skip (=13×67, love×understanding fused): in 7×50×871 this walks the c-axis
- 469-skip (=7×67): in spaces with 469 as an axis
- The key ELS findings from the fold — do they persist across decompositions?

### 5. Biblical Structures as Coordinates

Measurements from biblical structures map to coordinates. Different decompositions give different addresses.

- **Noah's ark**: 300×50×30. Natural fit for spaces with a 50-axis. What about 3D spaces?
- **Tabernacle**: 30×10×10 (holy place), 10×10×10 (holy of holies)
- **Altar of burnt offering**: 5×5×3 cubits
- **Ark of the covenant**: 2.5×1.5×1.5 cubits (5×3×3 half-cubits)
- **Table of showbread**: 2×1×1.5 cubits
- **Mercy seat**: 2.5×1.5 cubits
- **Brazen laver**: dimensions not given (significant?)
- Do biblical measurements prefer certain decompositions? If the tabernacle is 30×10×10 and there's a 10×...×... decomposition, does it fit naturally?
- Walk the tabernacle stations in each space — do the same cross-references emerge?
- Walk the ark layers in each space

### 6. Division Boundaries

In 4D canonical, the 7-axis gives 7 divisions. These divisions have theological meaning (creation days, etc.).

- In spaces with a 7-axis (e.g., 7×67×650 in 3D), are the 7 divisions the same?
- In 13-axis spaces: 13 divisions. Creation already sweeps all 13 in canonical.
- The 6D prime space gives ALL prime divisions simultaneously: 2, 5, 7, 13, 67
- Do division boundaries in different spaces overlap? Reinforce each other?
- Are there verses that sit on boundaries in EVERY decomposition? (Universal boundaries)

### 7. The Oracle Across Dimensions

The breastplate oracle is inherently 2D (4×3 grid). But the input words are addressed in Torah coordinate space.

- For a given word, its coordinate changes per decomposition. Does the coordinate carry meaning in non-canonical spaces?
- Basin dynamics: do basins change if we re-derive the oracle in a different coordinate space?
- Can we define a "reader" for 3D? For 2D? The four readers correspond to four traversal orders of a 2D grid — what traversal orders exist for higher/lower dimensional grids?
- Reader count: 2D grid has 4 natural traversals (rows/cols × 2 directions). 3D box? 6 face traversals × 2 directions = 12? Is there a 12-reader oracle?

### 8. Spectral Analysis

### 9. Silent Axes

In 4D canonical, the c-axis (13=love) and d-axis (67=understanding) are "silent" — אהבה and בינה never appear as Torah words on the breastplate.

### 11. Verified Number-Theoretic Properties

Discovered and verified computationally:

- **phi(304,850) = 95,040 = 12!/7! = |M_12|** — Euler's totient equals the order of the Mathieu group M_12, a sporadic simple group that acts on 12 points. The breastplate has 12 stones. 95,040 = the number of ordered 5-selections from 12 objects.
- **13^2 + 67^2 = 4,658 = 2 x 17 x 137** — the squared face diagonal of the two silent axes (love, understanding) factors to contain the axis sum 137.
- **Graph diameter = 133 = 137 - 4 = 7 x 19** — the Manhattan distance corner-to-corner equals the fine structure constant minus the dimensionality. Also equals the axis sum of the #2 ranked decomposition (5x26x35x67, the Name-of-God space).
- **Uniqueness**: no integer in [304,840..304,860] besides 304,850 has a non-trivial 4-tuple decomposition (all factors > 1) summing to 137.
- **mu(304,850) = 0** — the Mobius function vanishes because 5^2 divides it. The breath (He) is the only repeated prime.
