# Isaiah–Bible Map

*66 chapters. 66 books. The Bible in miniature.*

Type: `reference`
State: `clean`

Isaiah has 66 chapters. The Bible has 66 books. Isaiah splits 39/27 (judgment/comfort). The Bible splits 39/27 (Old Testament/New Testament). The parallel is ancient — noticed by many readers across centuries.

This table maps each Isaiah chapter to its corresponding Bible book, with letter counts and verse counts for the Isaiah side.

## Patterns in the Oracle Profile

### Serpent + Lamb Together
- **Ch 5** (Deuteronomy): 1 serpent, 2 lamb — the vineyard song, six woes
- **Ch 14** (II Chronicles): 2 serpent, 2 lamb — fall of Lucifer. The only chapter with both ≥2.

### Servant Peaks
- **Ch 14** (II Chronicles): 7 — fall of the morning star
- **Ch 28** (Hosea): 4 — the cornerstone in Zion
- **Ch 37** (Haggai): 7 — Hezekiah's prayer
- **Ch 44** (Acts): 8 — "I will pour water on the thirsty land." Cyrus named.
- **Ch 45** (Romans): **9** — highest. "I am the LORD, and there is no other."
- **Ch 49** (Ephesians): 6 — second servant song
- **Ch 51** (Colossians): 7 — "Awake, awake, put on strength."
- **Ch 65** (Jude): 8 — new heavens and new earth

### Curse Peaks
- **Ch 6** (Joshua): 7 — throne room. "Holy holy holy." Holiness registers as curse words.
- **Ch 14** (II Chronicles): **11** — fall of Lucifer. Highest curse count.
- **Ch 27** (Daniel): 9 — Leviathan slain
- **Ch 30** (Amos): 9 — trust in Egypt rebuked
- **Ch 38** (Zechariah): 7 — Hezekiah's illness
- **Ch 53** (II Thessalonians): **10** curses, **0 blessings** — the suffering servant absorbs all curses
- **Ch 59** (James): 8 — sin separates

### Blessing Peaks
- **Ch 5** (Deuteronomy): 10 — vineyard song
- **Ch 11** (I Kings): 10 — branch from Jesse, wolf and lamb
- **Ch 30** (Amos): **13** — highest in judgment. Trust in Egypt rebuked. 13 = love.
- **Ch 44** (Acts): 10 — water on the thirsty land
- **Ch 52** (I Thessalonians): 10 — "How beautiful the feet"
- **Ch 63** (II John): **17** — highest of all. The treader of the winepress. 17 = the 7th prime.
- **Ch 65** (Jude): 10 — new heavens and new earth

### Ch 53 (The Suffering Servant)
**10 curses, 0 blessings.** The only chapter with zero blessings. Top word: נוה (dwelling/pasture). The servant absorbs every curse. The oracle sees home.

### The Lamb Path (Ch 57 = Philemon)
Ch 57 has **4 lamb windows** — the most of any chapter. "The righteous perish." Maps to Philemon. The most lamb-dense chapter in Isaiah maps to the most personal letter of Paul — a slave returned as a brother.

### Key Divisors
- **÷ 7**: Ch 7, 12, 15, 17, 25, 28, 30, 42, 63 — completeness across both halves
- **÷ 13**: Ch 3, 23, 33 — love. Ch 33 is the center chapter (GV ÷ 13 × 26)
- **÷ 53**: Ch 14 — garden. Fall of Lucifer.
- **÷ 67**: Ch 3, 50 — understanding. Leviticus mirror and third servant song.
- **÷ 137**: Ch 16, 54 — axis sum. Nehemiah and I Timothy. Wall-building and church-building.

### Ch 53 (The Suffering Servant)
Zero blessings. One curse. Three servants. Top word: נוה (dwelling/pasture) — not היו like most chapters. The servant chapter has its own voice.
