# The Scattered Names

*One history. One Babel. Many names for the same figures.*

Reference catalogue. The divine figures, adversaries, messiahs, floods, and fallen councils — traced across every tradition that remembers them.

### Patterns

- Almost every tradition has a supreme god who is remote, who delegates, who is above the active pantheon.
- The sky-father etymology (*dyaus/zeus/jupiter/tiu*) connects Indo-European traditions to a single Proto-Indo-European source.
- The "god who withdraws" pattern (Anu, El, Dyaus, Nyame, Shangdi, Baiame, Amenominakanushi) — the supreme god steps back and active gods/spirits take over. This may reflect the assignment: God keeps Israel, the overseers get the nations.
- Creator-from-water appears in Hebrew (Genesis 1:2), Egyptian (Nu/Atum), Sumerian (Abzu/Enki), and Polynesian (Tangaroa).
- Creator-by-speech appears in Hebrew (Genesis 1), Polynesian (Tagaloa), Egyptian (Atum/Hu), and Vedic (Vāc).
- No-image/no-idol monotheism: Hebrew (YHWH), Chinese (Shangdi), and possibly Maori (Io). The oldest layers of Chinese religion and Hebrew religion share this trait independently.

The prosecutor, the serpent, the departed one, the tester — in his various phases.

### The Chinese flood in detail

The Chinese traditions deserve special attention because multiple layers of the flood memory survive:

**Fuxi and Nüwa:** The survival narrative. Two survivors (brother and sister, like Noah's family). They consult heaven about repopulating — permission is sought and granted. Fuxi is sometimes depicted with a serpent/fish lower body (the Apkallu/Oannes form). He brings writing, fishing, the calendar. He is simultaneously the flood survivor and the knowledge-bringer.

**Yao, Gun, and Yu:** The engineering narrative. Gun steals self-expanding soil (xirang 息壤) from heaven without authorization to dam the flood — unauthorized disclosure, the Prometheus/Azazel pattern. He is executed by the supreme god. His son Yu succeeds by working with the water, not against it, and founds the first dynasty. The father who steals is punished. The son who serves is rewarded.

**The Chinese character theory (C.H. Kang and Ethel Nelson, *The Discovery of Genesis*):**

| Character | Components | Proposed reading |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| 船 (ship) | 舟 (boat) + 八 (eight) + 口 (mouths/people) | Eight people on a boat. Noah's family. |
| 禁 (forbidden) | 示 (God/altar) + 林 (two trees) | God and two trees. Eden. |
| 婪 (covet) | 林 (two trees) + 女 (woman) | Woman at the trees. Eve. |
| 洪 (flood) | 氵(water) + 共 (together) + 八 (eight) | Water + together + eight. The flood. |
| 沿 (follow along) | 氵(water) + 八 (eight) + 口 (mouth) | Following the water, eight mouths. |

**Status:** Controversial. Most sinologists reject the theory — characters evolved over millennia for phonetic and practical reasons. The component analysis doesn't always hold for the oldest oracle bone forms (Shang dynasty, ~1200 BCE). But: the oracle bone forms ARE the oldest layer. If the memory is from before Babel, it would have been encoded at the very beginning of Chinese writing. The debate is unresolved.

**Nu'u and Noah:** The Hawaiian flood hero is named **Nu'u**. He survived on a large vessel, landed on Mauna Kea, made offerings, and the rainbow appeared. The phonetic similarity Nu'u / Noah, combined with the vessel + offerings + rainbow pattern, is either remarkable coincidence or very old memory. Polynesian migrations into the Pacific began ~3000-1500 BCE from Southeast Asia, which would place the departure of these oral traditions well before Western contact.

### The Earth Diver

A distinctive flood motif found across North America, Central Asia, and India:

An animal dives into floodwaters and retrieves a handful of mud/earth from the bottom. From this mud, the new world is formed.

| Tradition | Diver | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Ojibwe | Muskrat | Nanabozho's raft. Others fail. Muskrat succeeds and dies from the effort. |
| Cherokee | Water beetle (Dayunisi) | Earth is an island on water, suspended by cords from the sky vault. |
| Cree | Muskrat | Wisakedjak's flood. |
| Iroquois | Various animals | Sky Woman falls. Animals dive. Turtle offers its back. Turtle Island. |
| Arapaho, Cheyenne | Various | Multiple Plains traditions. |
| Hindu | **Varaha** (boar avatar of Vishnu) | Dives into cosmic waters to retrieve the earth on his tusks. |
| Slavic/Romanian | A bird sent by God | God sends a bird to retrieve earth from under the waters. |
| Siberian/Central Asian | Various | Multiple traditions across Eurasia. |

The geographic spread — North America, South Asia, Central Asia, Eastern Europe — suggests a very old, pre-migration memory. Possibly the oldest flood motif in the world.

### Convergences

- **Warning from a divine source** — almost universal. The hero is told in advance.
- **One family survives** — universal.
- **A vessel** — nearly universal. Boat, chest, reed, tree.
- **Pitch/sealing** — Hebrew (כפר, same root as atonement), Babylonian (bitumen). The vessel is sealed.
- **Animals preserved** — Hebrew, Babylonian, Hindu.
- **Post-flood sacrifice** — Hebrew (Noah), Greek (Deucalion), Babylonian (Utnapishtim), Hindu (Manu).
- **Divine regret or covenant** — Hebrew (rainbow), Babylonian (gods regret), Greek (Zeus relents).
- **The hero receives special status** — Noah (covenant), Utnapishtim (immortality), Ziusudra (eternal life), Manu (father of humanity).

### The Babylonian parallel in detail

The closest parallel to the Genesis account. Both preserved in Mesopotamia.

| Element | Genesis | Gilgamesh XI / Atrahasis |
|---------|---------|-------------------------|
| Reason | Wickedness + Nephilim | Noise / overpopulation |
| Who decides | God (YHWH) | Enlil (in council) |
| Who warns | God directly | Enki/Ea through a reed wall (circumventing Enlil's decree) |
| Vessel dimensions | 300×50×30 cubits (rectangular) | A cube (equal dimensions) — Gilgamesh. Rectangular in Atrahasis. |
| Sealed with | כפר (pitch/atonement) | Bitumen |
| Duration | 40 days rain + 150 days water + gradual recession | 7 days of storm |
| Birds sent | Raven, then dove (3 times) | Dove, swallow, raven |
| Landing | Mountains of Ararat | Mount Nisir (Pir Omar Gudrun) |
| Sacrifice after | Noah builds an altar, offers clean animals | Utnapishtim offers incense, gods "gather like flies" |
| God's response | "Never again" — the rainbow covenant | Enlil regrets, grants Utnapishtim immortality |
| Resolution | God establishes seasons, human lifespan set | Gods establish population control mechanisms |

**The reed wall:** In the Babylonian version, the supreme god (Enlil) decrees destruction. The wise god (Enki/Ea) cannot directly disobey but finds a loophole — he speaks to a reed wall, knowing the hero is listening on the other side. This is God circumventing his own decree through a technicality. In the Hebrew version, there is no conflict among gods — YHWH both decrees and warns. One God, no loopholes needed.

## VI. The Titans and the Watchers

The most direct structural parallel between Greek and Enochic tradition.

### The Watchers (1 Enoch 6-16, Genesis 6:1-4)

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Who | 200 בני אלהים (sons of God) |
| Leader | Semyaza (organized the oath), Azazel (chief teacher) |
| Location | Mount Hermon (חרמון) — "they swore an oath and bound one another with a curse (חרם)" |
| Sin | Desire: took human wives. Disclosure: taught forbidden knowledge. |
| Offspring | **Nephilim** (נפילים, "fallen ones") — giants, 3,000 ells tall. Consumed everything. Turned on humanity. |
| Forbidden knowledge | Azazel: weapons, cosmetics, metalworking, dyes. Others: astrology, herbalism, enchantments, writing. |
| Judgment | Watchers bound. Azazel cast into Dudael (desert pit, sharp rocks, darkness). Semyaza and others bound in valleys. Their offspring (the giants) destroyed — their spirits become demons (1 Enoch 15:8-12). |
| The flood | Comes to cleanse the earth of the corruption. |

### The Titans (Hesiod's Theogony, ~700 BCE)

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Who | 12 Titans — children of Ouranos (Sky) and Gaia (Earth). The older generation of gods. |
| Leader | **Kronos** (overthrew his father Ouranos). Ruler of the Golden Age. |
| Location | Originally ruled from Mount Othrys. |
| Sin | Kronos: swallowed his children to prevent being overthrown. The Titans sided with Kronos against Zeus. |
| Offspring | The Olympian gods (Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, etc.) — born from Titans. Also: various monsters from Titan unions. |
| Key Titans | **Atlas** — forced to hold up the sky (cf. Semyaza, the organizer). **Prometheus** — gave fire to humanity, bound (cf. Azazel, the teacher, bound). **Kronos** — swallows children, castrated his father. **Iapetus** — father of Prometheus and Atlas. |
| Judgment | **Titanomachy** — ten-year war. Zeus and the Olympians defeat the Titans. Titans imprisoned in **Tartarus** — the deepest pit of the underworld, as far below Hades as earth is below heaven. |
| Post-judgment | Some Titans pardoned (Prometheus eventually freed by Heracles). Most remain in Tartarus. |

### The Structural Mapping

| Watchers | Titans | Correspondence |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| בני אלהים (sons of God) — divine council members | Titans — children of Sky and Earth, the older gods | A class of divine beings above humanity but below the supreme authority |
| 200 descend on Mount Hermon | Titans rule from Mount Othrys | A mountain as the place of departure/authority |
| Bound by oath (חרם on Hermon) | — | Hermon = חרם (curse/oath). The place name IS the act. |
| Semyaza organizes | Kronos leads, Atlas organizes | The ringleader/organizer as a distinct figure from the teacher |
| Azazel teaches forbidden knowledge | Prometheus gives fire to humanity | The disclosure figure. Forbidden technology. Both bound. |
| Azazel bound in Dudael (desert, darkness, sharp rocks) | Prometheus bound on a mountain (eagle eats liver) | Binding in a desolate place. Ongoing torment. |
| Watchers bound in valleys of the earth | Titans imprisoned in Tartarus | Underground imprisonment. Awaiting judgment. |
| Nephilim (giants, offspring of the union) | Various monstrous offspring (Typhon, the Hundred-Handers, etc.) | The hybrid offspring — too powerful, destructive, neither fully divine nor human |
| Spirits of dead Nephilim become demons | — | The origin of demons as disembodied spirits of destroyed hybrids |
| The flood cleanses the earth | Deucalion's flood (later, but post-Titan war) | A cataclysm follows the corruption |
| New order established (post-flood) | Olympians rule (post-Titanomachy) | A new divine order replaces the corrupted one |

### Key parallel: Iapetus and Japheth

**Iapetus** (Ἰαπετός) — a Titan, father of Prometheus, Atlas, Menoetius, and Epimetheus.
**Japheth** (יפת) — son of Noah, father of the Greek peoples (Javan = Ionians).

The names are phonetically similar: Iapetos / Yapheth. Many scholars have noted this. If the Greeks descend from Japheth (Genesis 10:2-5), then the Greek memory of Iapetus as a Titan — a primordial figure, father of the one who gave fire — may be a scattered memory of the pre-flood patriarch. The father of nations remembered as the father of gods.

### Key parallel: Hermon and Othrys

Mount Hermon (חרמון): where the Watchers swore their oath and descended.
Mount Othrys: where the Titans ruled before the war.

Both: a mountain where the older divine order held power before judgment.

Hermon is in the northern Levant (modern Lebanon/Syria border). Othrys is in central Greece. After Babel, the same mountain — or the memory of the same event at a mountain — was placed in each tradition's local geography.

### Key parallel: Tartarus and Dudael

**Tartarus** (Greek): the deepest pit. As far below Hades as earth is below heaven. Where the Titans are imprisoned.
**Dudael** (Enochic): the desert pit where Azazel is bound. Sharp rocks, darkness.

**2 Peter 2:4** bridges them explicitly: "God did not spare the angels when they sinned, but cast them into **Tartarus** (ταρταρώσας), putting them in chains of darkness to be held for judgment." Peter uses the Greek word Tartarus — not Gehenna, not Hades — specifically for the imprisoned angels. He is identifying the Watcher tradition with the Titan tradition in a single word choice.

## VIII. The Americas, the Pacific, Africa, Asia — The Furthest Scatterings

These traditions are the hardest test of the hypothesis. If the same patterns appear in populations separated by oceans and millennia, either the memory is very old (pre-Babel, pre-migration) or there is a common human template. Or both.

### A. Mesoamerica (Aztec, Maya, Olmec, Toltec)

**The Supreme God:**
- **Ometeotl** (Aztec) — "Dual God." Both male and female. The source of all. Remote, above the active pantheon. Cf. the supreme god who withdraws.
- **Hunab Ku** (Maya, post-Classic) — "One God." The supreme creator. Debated whether pre-contact or missionary-influenced.
- **Itzamná** (Maya) — Creator god, lord of the heavens, inventor of writing. The old god.

**The Knowledge-Bringer / Feathered Serpent:**
- **Quetzalcoatl** (Aztec) / **Kukulkan** (Maya) / **Gucumatz** (K'iche Maya)
  - The feathered serpent. A SERPENT with WINGS.
  - Described in some traditions as bearded, light-skinned, tall.
  - Brought civilization: calendar, agriculture, law, writing, maize.
  - Departed across the sea. Promised to return.
  - The seraphim (שרפים) in Isaiah 6 are literally "fiery flying serpents." A winged serpent at the throne. Same image.

**The Adversary / Trickster:**
- **Tezcatlipoca** — "Smoking Mirror." The dark counterpart to Quetzalcoatl. Night, sorcery, jaguars, the north.
  - Drove Quetzalcoatl out through trickery — made him drunk, caused him to sin with his own sister.
  - The reframing: not a direct attack, but a manipulation that causes the good one to fall. Same method as the garden. Same ערום.
  - After the fall, Quetzalcoatl departs in shame. Tezcatlipoca takes his place.
  - This IS the departure narrative. The knowledge-bringer tricked, shamed, driven out by the deceiver who takes his seat.

**The Giants:**
- **Quinametzin** — Giants who lived in a previous world age. Too large, too violent. Destroyed. They built the great pyramids at Teotihuacan (according to Aztec legend).
  - Direct parallel to the Nephilim: giants, offspring of an earlier age, violent, destroyed before the current era.

**The Flood:**
- The Fourth Sun (Nahui-Atl) ended in a great flood. The current world is the Fifth Sun.
- **Nata and Nene** survived in a hollowed cypress tree (told by Tezcatlipoca to save them).
- The Popol Vuh (K'iche Maya): the wooden people destroyed by flood. The gods' failed creation before the current humanity.

**The Descent to the Underworld:**
- **Quetzalcoatl** descends to Mictlan (the underworld), retrieves the bones of the dead, bleeds on them to create new humanity. The redeemer who descends, bleeds, and gives life.
- The **Hero Twins** (Popol Vuh) — Hunahpu and Xbalanque descend to Xibalba (underworld), defeat the lords of death through cleverness and sacrifice, and rise as the sun and moon.

**The Sacred Tree:**
- The Maya **World Tree** (Wacah Chan) — axis mundi connecting underworld, earth, and heavens. Depicted on stelae with the ruler at its base and a celestial bird at its top. A serpent at its roots.
- The bucket/handbag motif at La Venta and Tula.

**The Dying-and-Rising God:**
- Quetzalcoatl: departs, burns himself on a pyre, his heart rises as the morning star (Venus). He becomes הילל — the light-bearer. The morning star. The same astronomical identification as Helel ben Shachar.

### B. South America (Inca, Mapuche, Amazonian)

**The Supreme God / Knowledge-Bringer:**
- **Viracocha** (Inca) — The creator. Emerged from Lake Titicaca. Created the sun, moon, stars, and humanity. Made humans from stone at Tiwanaku.
  - Described as tall, bearded, light-skinned, wearing a long robe.
  - Departed across the Pacific, walking on water.
  - Promised to return.
  - The departure pattern again — the knowledge-bringer who leaves.

**The Flood:**
- Inca: Viracocha destroyed his first creation with a flood, then re-created humanity.
- **Mapuche** (Chile): Trentren Vilu (earth serpent) battles Caicai Vilu (sea serpent). Their war causes the flood. People flee to mountains raised by Trentren Vilu.
  - Two serpents fighting. The primordial conflict in serpent form.
  - The earth serpent SAVES by raising the land. Cf. the bronze serpent — the serpent form used for rescue.

**The Underworld:**
- **Supay** (Inca) — Lord of the underworld. Later syncretized with the Christian devil by missionaries, but originally the ruler of the dead, not a figure of evil.

### C. North America

Hundreds of nations, enormous diversity. The patterns that recur:

**The Trickster / Fire-Bringer:**

| Tradition | Figure | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Southwest, Plains | **Coyote** | Steals fire for humanity. Creates, tricks, transforms. Both hero and fool. |
| Pacific Northwest | **Raven** | Steals the sun (light) and gives it to humanity. Creator-trickster. |
| Ojibwe | **Nanabozho** | Half-divine trickster. Transforms the world. |
| Lakota | **Iktomi** | Spider trickster. Cunning. Teaches through mistakes. |
| Cree | **Wisakedjak** | Trickster who causes and survives the flood. |

**The pattern:** The trickster steals fire/light/knowledge from the gods and gives it to humanity. He is Prometheus. He is Azazel. He is the disclosure figure. But in many Native traditions, he is not condemned for it — he is celebrated. The theft was necessary. Humanity needed the fire.

This is a crucial difference. In Enoch, Azazel is bound. In Greece, Prometheus is bound. In Native American traditions, Coyote gets away with it. Different judgment on the same act.

**The Flood:**

| Tradition | Narrative | Survivor | Key element |
|-----------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Hopi** | Three previous worlds destroyed (fire, ice, water). Emergence through a reed into the fourth world. | The faithful who listened | Emergence through a hollow reed / sipapu. Cf. Babylonian reed wall. |
| **Ojibwe** | Nanabozho (or the Great Spirit) sends a flood. | Nanabozho on a raft with animals | **Earth Diver**: muskrat dives to the bottom, brings up a handful of mud to rebuild the world. |
| **Lakota** | Unktehi (water spirit) floods the world. | Survivors on a hilltop | Thunderbird battles the water serpent. |
| **Cherokee** | Dayunisi (water beetle) retrieves mud from under the waters. | — | Earth Diver again. Earth is an island on water. |
| **Cree** | Wisakedjak causes a flood through trickery. | Animals on a raft | Earth Diver: muskrat retrieves earth. |
| **Navajo** | Flood between the third and fourth worlds. Caused by Coyote stealing Water Monster's children. | Ascent to the next world through a reed | Ascent through a hollow reed — same as Hopi. |

**The Earth Diver motif** is extraordinary. An animal dives into floodwaters and retrieves a bit of earth from which the new world is formed. This appears across:
- Ojibwe, Cree, Cherokee, Iroquois, Arapaho, Cheyenne (North America)
- Hindu: Varaha (boar avatar of Vishnu) dives to retrieve the earth from cosmic waters
- Slavic/Finno-Ugric: God sends a bird to dive for earth
- Romanian, Siberian, Central Asian variants

The geographic spread of this motif suggests a very old, pre-migration memory.

**Giants:**
- **Starnake / Stone Giants** (Cherokee, Haudenosaunee) — ancient giant beings made of stone. Hostile.
- **Tsul'kalu** (Cherokee) — "Slanting Eyes," a giant who lived in the mountains.
- Many traditions remember a race of giants who preceded current humanity.

**Star Beings:**
- Multiple traditions speak of ancestors or teachers who came from the stars — the Pleiades are particularly significant across many Native American nations.
- **Lakota**: The people originally came from the stars.
- **Cherokee**: The Pleiades (Ani'tsutsa, "the Boys") — seven boys who danced into the sky.

### D. Australia

Possibly the oldest continuous cultural traditions on earth (40,000-65,000 years).

**The Rainbow Serpent:**
- The most widespread figure in Aboriginal Australian religion. Known by different names across language groups: **Yurlungur**, **Ngalyod**, **Borlung**, **Almudj**, **Wagyl**, etc.
- A massive serpent associated with water, rain, rivers, fertility, and creation.
- Both creator and destroyer. When angered, causes floods.
- Associated with the rainbow — the sign across the sky after rain.
- The rainbow as a sign after the flood: Genesis 9:13 — "I have set my rainbow in the clouds, and it will be the sign of the covenant between me and the earth."
- A serpent and a rainbow. A flood and a covenant sign. The oldest continuous tradition on earth remembers both.

**The Dreaming / Dreamtime (Tjukurpa):**
- Not "the past" — an eternal present underlying reality. The time when ancestor beings shaped the world.
- The ancestor beings are still present in the landscape — hills, rivers, rocks ARE the ancestors.
- Creation by walking and singing — the Songlines. The world was sung into existence.
- Cf. "In the beginning was the Word." Creation by utterance. The Logos as song.

**Flood narratives:**
- Present in many Aboriginal traditions, often linked to the Rainbow Serpent.
- Some traditions describe a time when the sea was lower and land bridges existed (consistent with Ice Age geography — genuine ancient memory of sea level rise).
- The Gunditjmara people of Victoria have an oral tradition about volcanic eruptions that geologists date to ~37,000 years ago. If oral tradition can preserve geological events for 37,000 years, it can preserve a flood.

### E. Polynesia

**The Trickster / Demigod:**
- **Maui** — Half-divine. Trickster hero across Polynesian traditions.
  - Fished up islands from the sea (creator).
  - Snared the sun to make it move slower (controlling cosmic order).
  - **Stole fire from the underworld** (from Mahuika, the fire goddess). Prometheus.
  - Died trying to win immortality by entering the body of Hine-nui-te-pō (goddess of death). The quest for immortality that fails.
  - The parallel: he steals fire (Prometheus/Azazel), controls the sun (Joshua 10:13?), seeks eternal life (Gilgamesh), and dies in the attempt.

**The Supreme God:**
- **Tangaroa / Tagaloa** — Creator, god of the sea. In some traditions, creates by speaking. In Samoa, Tagaloa speaks the world into existence from the void.
- **Io** (Maori, some traditions) — The supreme, hidden god. Above and before all other gods. Debated whether pre-contact.

**The Serpent/Dragon:**
- **Mo'o** (Hawaiian) — Giant lizard/dragon spirits. Water guardians.
- **Taniwha** (Maori) — Serpentine water creatures. Guardians or monsters depending on the story.

**The Flood:**
- Multiple Polynesian traditions include a great flood.
- Hawaiian tradition: Nu'u (note the name) survived the flood on a large vessel and landed on Mauna Kea. He made offerings, and the rainbow appeared. Nu'u. Noah. The names.

### F. Africa (beyond the Dogon)

**The Supreme God:**
- **Nyame / Onyame** (Akan/Ashanti) — The supreme sky god. Remote. Withdrew from humanity (because an old woman kept hitting him with her pestle). The god who withdraws.
- **Olodumare / Olorun** (Yoruba) — The supreme god. Creator. Delegates to the orishas. Remote but ultimate authority.
- **Ngai** (Kikuyu/Maasai) — The one God. Lives on Mount Kenya.
- **Nzambi** (Kongo) — Supreme creator god.
- **Mulungu** (multiple East African) — The supreme god who went up to the sky.

The pattern across sub-Saharan Africa: a supreme creator god who is real, who made everything, but who withdrew and is now remote. Active spiritual life runs through lesser spirits/ancestors/orishas. This is the Deuteronomy 32:8 structure — the supreme god above, the overseers/spirits below.

**The Trickster:**
- **Anansi** (Akan/Ashanti) — Spider trickster. Steals all the stories/knowledge from the sky god Nyame. Brings them to earth. The disclosure pattern — knowledge taken from heaven and given to humanity.
- **Eshu / Elegba** (Yoruba) — Messenger between gods and humans. Stands at crossroads. Neither good nor evil. Maintains cosmic balance. Tests people. The closest African parallel to the authorized השטן. Eshu does not lie — he creates situations where your true nature is revealed. He is the prosecution.
- **Hare** (multiple East/Southern African) — Trickster. Brings fire. Connected to the moon and death. The hare was supposed to deliver the message of immortality and got it wrong — death entered through a messaging error. Cf. the serpent's reframing in the garden.

**The Flood:**
- **Yoruba**: Olokun (sea goddess) floods the world in anger. Obatala survives.
- **Mandé** (West Africa): A great flood in the deep past. A man warned by spirits.
- **Maasai**: Tumbainot and his family survive a great flood.
- Multiple traditions across the continent, though less prominent than in Mesopotamian/American/Asian traditions.

**The Dying God:**
- **Osiris** (Egyptian, already catalogued) — the African dying-and-rising god par excellence.
- **Leza** (multiple Bantu traditions) — A deity associated with death and resurrection cycles, rain, and agricultural renewal.

### G. Japan

**The Creator Pair:**
- **Izanagi and Izanami** — Create the islands by stirring the sea with a jeweled spear. Izanami dies giving birth to fire (Kagutsuchi). Izanagi descends to Yomi (underworld) to retrieve her. Sees her decaying. Flees. Seals the entrance.
  - The descent to retrieve the beloved dead: Orpheus/Eurydice (Greek), Ishtar's descent (Mesopotamian), Izanagi/Izanami (Japanese). Universal.

**The Banished Storm God:**
- **Susanoo** — Storm god, brother of Amaterasu (sun). Causes havoc in heaven. Banished.
  - In exile, defeats **Yamata no Orochi** — an eight-headed serpent. Finds a divine sword (Kusanagi) in its tail.
  - Banished from heaven (the departure). Defeats the multi-headed serpent (storm god vs chaos serpent). Finds the sword in the serpent (the weapon inside the enemy).
  - Susanoo is both the exile and the hero. The one cast out becomes the one who slays the dragon. Cf. the bronze serpent — the thing lifted up has the same form as the thing that bit you.

**The Sun Goddess Hides:**
- **Amaterasu** retreats into a cave after Susanoo's violence. The world goes dark. The gods must lure her out with a mirror and laughter.
  - The sun disappearing into a cave. Darkness over the earth. Drawn out by seeing her own reflection.
  - Light departing and returning. The world in darkness until the light is drawn back.

### H. Celtic / Irish

**The Divine Council:**
- **Tuatha Dé Danann** — "Peoples of the goddess Danu." A race of divine beings who ruled Ireland before the Milesians (humans). Skilled in magic, craft, and warfare. Eventually driven underground — they become the **Sídhe** (fairy folk), dwelling in mounds.
  - Divine beings who ruled, then lost their place, then went underground. The Watchers bound in the earth. The Titans in Tartarus. The Tuatha Dé in the fairy mounds.

**The Trickster/Knowledge-Bringer:**
- **Lugh** — "The Shining One" (Lugh Lámhfhada, "Lugh of the Long Arm"). Master of all arts. Arrives at the court of the Tuatha Dé and demonstrates every skill. Leads them in battle.
  - "The Shining One" — cf. הילל (Helel), Lucifer. But Lugh is the HERO, not the fallen one.
  - In the Celtic version, the light-bearer is good. He is the one who saves. The same title, opposite valence.

**The Flood:**
- **Cessair** — granddaughter of Noah in Irish mythology. Led a group of women and a few men to Ireland before the flood. All died except Fintan mac Bóchra, who survived by transforming into a salmon, then an eagle, then a hawk. He lives for thousands of years and remembers everything.
  - The flood survivor who becomes the living memory. The witness.

**The Otherworld:**
- **Tír na nÓg** — "Land of the Young." An otherworldly paradise across the sea. No death, no aging.
  - Eden. The garden. The paradise you can see but can't stay in.

## IX. The Iconographic Evidence — The Bucket and the Tree

Separate from textual traditions, the visual record:

| Location | Date | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **Göbekli Tepe** (Turkey) | ~9600 BCE | Pillar 43: bucket/handbag shapes. Predates Sumer by 5,000+ years. |
| **Sumerian cylinder seals** | ~3000-2000 BCE | Fish-cloaked Apkallu at the sacred tree. Containers in hand. |
| **Nimrud reliefs** (Assyria) | ~880 BCE | Winged Apkallu (human, eagle-headed, fish-cloaked) flanking the sacred tree. Bucket (banduddu) + cone (mullilu). |
| **La Venta** (Olmec, Mexico) | ~900 BCE | Stone figures holding bucket-shaped objects. |
| **Tula** (Toltec, Mexico) | ~900 CE | Atlantean columns holding objects resembling the banduddu. |
| **Sanchi** (India) | ~200 BCE | Tree spirits (yaksha/yakshi) flanking sacred trees, holding objects. |
| **Angkor Wat** (Cambodia) | ~1100 CE | Naga (serpent) guardians flanking the entrance. Churning of the ocean scene. |

The pattern: **beings flanking a sacred tree, holding something, often with serpent imagery nearby.** This is Genesis 2-3 as a universal pictogram.

The bucket may be purification water. The cone may be a date palm flower (pollination). The act depicted: tending the tree of life. The Apkallu as gardeners. Before the expulsion.
