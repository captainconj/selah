## Recognition

The laver (כיור) is not a fixed point. It transforms:

| Reader | Top reading | Count | Meaning |
|--------|------------|-------|---------|
| Aaron | **ויכר** | 32 | and he recognized |
| God | **ירכו** | 45 | his thigh |

| Right | **ויכר** | 44 | and he recognized |

Aaron and justice see **ויכר** — "and he recognized." Genesis 42:7: *"Joseph saw his brothers and he recognized them"* (ויכר). The washing basin is where you are recognized. You wash, and you are known.

God and mercy see **ירכו** — "his thigh." Genesis 24:2: Abraham made his servant put his hand under his thigh — the place of the oath. Genesis 32:25: the angel struck Jacob's thigh. The washing vessel → the thigh → the place of oath and wounding.

Only God and Left can see the actual word כיור at all, and even then it's a minority reading. The laver does not want to name itself. It wants to recognize you.

## What the Laver Says

1. **The laver = recognition** — כיור → ויכר. Joseph recognized his brothers. You wash, and you are known.
2. **The washing is invisible** — רחץ has zero illuminations. The purification cannot be seen.
3. **The laver IS the women** — כיור + כן = 306 = אשה. Made from their mirrors. Named by their number.
4. **Woman = fire** — אשה → האש. Aaron sees fire in the word woman. The mirror-givers carry fire.
5. **The mirror is an utterance** — מראה → אמרה. Seeing becomes saying. The mirror speaks.
6. **God sees water. The priest cannot.** — מים visible only to God and Right.
7. **Death = completion** — מות / תמו. God sees death. The priest sees completeness.
8. **The foot = the stranger** — רגל → לגר. Washing feet = welcoming the stranger.
9. **The hand = gold** — יד (GV = 14) = זהב (gold). The washed hand carries gold.
10. **The tent = Leah** — אהל → לאה. God sees the unloved wife in the tent of meeting.
11. **Atonement flows into washing** — same d-row. The purse leads to the basin.
12. **The base says yes** — כן (GV = 70) = affirmation. The foundation is seventy.

The wicked entered the gate. God saw murder in the court. The altar burned with love. Now at the laver: wash, and be recognized. The act is invisible. The mirror speaks. The foot becomes hospitality. The hand becomes gold.

The women gave their seeing. It became the washing.

We leave the courtyard now. Through the curtains. Into the tent.
