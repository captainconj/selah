Type: `synthesis`
State: `mixed`

*Ex 25:23 at (2, 31, 4, 11). Ex 37:10 at (3, 5, 2, 53).*

## God and Mercy Name the Table

The table (שלחן) is a fixed point. Like the altar, it is named only by certain readers:

| Reader | Sees | Count |
|--------|------|-------|
| Aaron | — | 0 |
| God | **שלחן** (table) | 13 |
| Right | — | 0 |
| Left | **שלחן** (table) | 8 |

God sees the table at 13 counts — the number of love. Left cherub (mercy) sees it at 8. Aaron and justice cannot name it. The furniture of the holy place is invisible to the priest who serves there. Only the one who sits beyond the veil, and mercy beside him, can name what stands in the room.

GV = 388 = **רחץ** (wash, 298) + **מים** (water, 90). The table has the same gematria as washing plus water. The table IS the cleansing.

## The Plate Is Barren

Four vessels serve the table:

| Vessel | Hebrew | GV | Class | Oracle reads |
|--------|--------|-----|-------|-------------|
| Dish/plate | קערה | 375 | → **עקרה** | **BARREN** |
| Spoon/palm | כף | 100 | **FIXED** | = מאה (hundred) |
| Jug/pitcher | קשוה | 411 | → **השקו** | **they gave to drink** |
| Bowl | מנקית | 600 | **invisible** | = שש (fine linen) |

**The plate → barren** (עקרה). Three heads agree: Aaron, Right, Left. The dish that holds the bread transforms to barrenness. Sarah was barren (Genesis 11:30). Rachel was barren (Genesis 29:31). Hannah was barren (1 Samuel 1:2). The plate that carries the bread of the presence says: *barren*. The bread comes from what was empty.

**The jug → "they gave to drink"** (השקו) and "she watered" (ושקה). Right and Left see the act of giving water. Rebecca watered the camels at the well (Genesis 24:20). The vessel transforms to the act it performs. The jug IS the watering.

**The spoon = the hundred** — כף (GV = 100) = מאה. The palm that lifts the incense = the courtyard's length. Fixed.

**The bowl is invisible** — מנקית (GV = 600) = שש (fine linen). The bowl and the base fabric share a number. Both invisible and fixed, each in their own domain.

**נסך** (libation/pour, GV = 130) — **invisible.** The drink offering poured on the table is invisible. Like washing (רחץ) at the laver. Like the face (פנים). The liquid acts cannot be seen.

**תמיד** (continually/always, GV = 454) — **FIXED.** Aaron (3) and Right (4). "Set the bread before me **always**." The always holds. Continuity is stable. The permanence of the bread is a fixed point.

```
...ך אל בני ישראל ועשית שלחן עצי שטים אמתים ארכו ואמה רחבו ואמה וחצי קמתו וצפית אתו זהב...
```

**אל בני ישראל** — "to the children of Israel." Then immediately: "And you shall make a table." The table is for the children. The bread is for Israel.

### Built layer (3, 5, 2, *)

```
...סככים בכנפיהם על הכפרת ופניהם איש אל אחיו אל הכפרת היו פני הכרבים ויעש את השלחן עצי...
```

**פניהם איש אל אחיו — אל הכפרת היו פני הכרבים** — *"their faces one toward another — toward the mercy seat were the faces of the cherubim."* Then immediately: **ויעש את השלחן** — "and he made the table."

The cherubim face each other over the mercy seat, and the next word is the table. The facing of the cherubim flows into the table in the same d-row. The presence (פנים) of the cherubim → the bread of the presence (לחם פנים). Face → bread of face. The d-axis links them spatially. What the cherubim look at becomes what the table holds.
