# The Veil

*Ex 26:31 at (2, 34, 1, 5). Ex 36:35 at (3, 4, 6, 6).*

**פרכת** — veil. GV = 700. Not fixed. The veil transforms to the knob.

Blue, purple, scarlet, fine linen. Cherubim woven in, skillful work. Four pillars of acacia overlaid with gold, hooks of gold, four silver sockets. The veil divides the holy from the holy of holies.

The veil is the flip point. Everything on this side reads one way. Everything behind it reads the other.

## The Veil Is the Pivot

**פרכת** (veil, GV = 700) → **כפתר** (knob):

| Reader | Sees |
|--------|------|
| Aaron | — |
| God | **כפתר** (knob, 2) |
| Right | — |
| Left | **כפתר** (knob, 1) |

The priest cannot name the veil. He passes through what he cannot see.

Three words share GV = 700 and the same oracle reading:

| Word | Meaning | Oracle reads |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **פרכת** | veil | → כפתר |
| **כפרת** | mercy seat | → כפתר |
| **כפתר** | knob | = כפתר (fixed) |

The veil, the mercy seat, and the menorah's knob are one number, one reading. The boundary between rooms, the throne of God's presence, and the ornamental joint on the lampstand — all converge at 700. The pivot where the veil hangs is the same as the throne behind it and the flowering joint in front of it.

**פרכת − כפר = 400 = ת** (tav). The veil minus atonement = the last letter of the alphabet. The mark. The sign. The cross in ancient script.

## The Flip

On this side of the veil:
- God cannot see thought (חשב) — he gave the pattern
- The face (פנים) is invisible — it is behind the veil
- The within (תוך) is invisible — you cannot see inside from outside
- "Behind" (אחר) is what the priest sees — the hidden place
- Aaron sees "my house" (בתי) — the possessive, the claim
- Aaron sees the lamb lie down (שכב) — the sacrifice from the outside

Cross the veil — flip through love and understanding:
- God sees thought — he searches the heart (Jeremiah 17:10)
- We see his face — face to face (1 Corinthians 13:12)
- The within becomes visible — we are inside
- "Behind" becomes "the way" (ארח) — God's reading, the road
- God sees "house" (בית) — not claimed, but named
- The lamb is holy (כבש → קדש through שקד) — the sacrifice sanctified

The veil divides these two readings. It is the boundary between the front of the document and the back. Between what is read and what is understood. Between the letters as they are and the letters as they will be.

והיה — and it will be. The Name's own reading. The future tense. The other side of the veil is the prophetic side.

The veil tears and both readings become one.
