## The Two Sides of the Voice

From the front of the breastplate:
- The priest hears the **voice** (קול, 22) but barely hears (שמע, 1)
- God cannot hear the voice (0) but **hears** (שמע, 12)
- The priest says **"I"** (21). God barely says "I" (2). Mercy never says "I."
- Aaron sees **ornament** (עדי). God sees **knowledge** (ידע).
- The priest and justice see **"God"** (אל). God and mercy see **"not"** (לא).

From the other side — flip through love and understanding:
- God hears the voice. The priest hears.
- God says "I." The priest receives.
- The ornament becomes knowledge. The jewelry becomes wisdom.
- "Not" becomes "God." The negation becomes the name.

Both sides at once:
- The voice is sent and received. The hearing is mutual.
- The "I" crosses. The self-identification reaches the other.
- The ornament IS the knowledge. The breastplate on the priest's chest IS the mind of God.
- "God" and "not" are the same word. The presence and the absence. The name and the unnameable. Both. Always.

The veil is torn. Both readings are one. The voice that starts at d = 0 fills the entire axis of understanding.
