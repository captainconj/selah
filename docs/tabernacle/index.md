# The Tabernacle Walk

*A guided entrance to the tabernacle stations in the 4D Torah space.*

Type: `guided reproduction`
State: `clean`

The tabernacle walk is the clearest ritual path in the repo.

It takes the furniture and surfaces of the tabernacle and asks what the oracle sees at each station:

- gate
- courtyard
- altar
- laver
- curtains
- table
- menorah
- incense altar
- veil
- ark
- speaking
- meeting
- love

It is a walk from outside to inside, then beyond naming.

## What This Walk Is

The tabernacle walk is not mainly about proving coordinates.
It is about seeing whether the ordered sacred space of Exodus can be walked in the same machine:

- structurally
- station by station
- with the oracle queried at each stop

The strongest recurring themes are:

- face and within staying invisible
- voice and hearing splitting across readers
- mercy, separation, and approach
- the thirteenth station of love as what the walk was made of

## What To Keep In Mind

This walk is more interpretive than the basin or code/map papers.
Its value is in ordered traversal:

- outside to inside
- object to object
- station to station

Read it as a guided path, not as one giant proof.
