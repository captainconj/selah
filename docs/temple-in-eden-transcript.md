# The Temple Hidden in the Garden of Eden

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vQRQZqn3XPU

## Key Thesis

Eden wasn't just a paradise — it was the world's first temple. The same blueprint appears in:
1. Garden of Eden
2. Moses's Tabernacle
3. Solomon's Temple
4. Garden of Gethsemane

## The Blueprint: Three Concentric Zones

Each structure has three levels of increasing holiness and proximity to God:

| Eden | Tabernacle | Temple |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Land outside | Outer court | Outer court |
| Cosmic mountain | Holy Place | Holy Place |
| Inner sanctuary (God walks with Adam) | Holy of Holies | Holy of Holies |

## Key Parallels

### עבד and שמר (Avad and Shamar)
- "To serve/till" and "to keep/guard"
- These two Hebrew words appear together in only TWO places in the Torah:
  1. God's command to Adam in the Garden (Genesis 2:15)
  2. The charge given to priests in the Tabernacle
- Adam's first calling was to be a **priest** ministering in the garden sanctuary

### Cherubim as Guardians
- Eden: Cherubim + flaming sword guard the entrance (east)
- Tabernacle: Cherubim embroidered on the veil
- Ark: Two cherubim shelter the mercy seat

### Direction of Entry
- Adam expelled **eastward** from Eden
- Entry into the Tabernacle goes **westward** — reversing the exile
- Each entry is a symbolic return to the garden

### Tree of Life → Menorah
- The golden menorah = seven blossoming branches = a stylized tree
- Symbol of Eden's Tree of Life
- Promise of immortality found in the presence of God

### Three Entries
- High priest enters the Holy of Holies **three times** on Yom Kippur:
  1. Once with incense
  2. Twice with blood of sacrifice, sprinkling seven times on the mercy seat
- Jesus in Gethsemane enters **three times** to pray:
  - Leaves 8 disciples
  - Takes Peter, James, John further in
  - Continues alone even deeper
  - Three-level structure echoes the three divisions of Eden and Tabernacle

### Jubilees (Ancient Jewish Text)
- Adam built an altar at the entrance of the garden after expulsion
- "The Garden of Eden is the Holy of Holies and the dwelling of the Lord"

## The Veil Torn

- At the crucifixion: "The veil of the temple was torn in two from top to bottom"
- The cherubic guardian stepped aside
- The way to God's presence opened permanently
- "He entered once for all into the holy places. Not by means of the blood of goats and calves, but by means of his own blood."

## The Pattern

The story begins in a garden (Eden, fall of Adam) and ends in gardens (Gethsemane, the empty tomb). The sacred space lost in the first garden is restored in the last.
