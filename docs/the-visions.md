# The Visions

*Come sit with me. I want to tell you something.*

Type: `testimony / history`
State: `clean`

---

There were four men, a long time ago, who each saw the same room.

They didn't know each other. They lived hundreds of years apart, in different countries, speaking different languages. One was a priest by a river in Babylon. One was a prophet in the temple in Jerusalem. One was an exile who dreamed. One was a prisoner on an island.

They all saw a throne. They all saw fire. They all saw creatures with wings and faces and eyes. And they all tried to write down what they saw.

Four men. Seven centuries. Two languages. One room.

And every one of them saw the same God doing the same thing.

---

## He's Hiding

God is hiding.

Not gone. Not absent. Hiding. The way a father hides behind the door when his little one is looking for him — close enough to touch, quiet enough that the child has to come find him.

Every prophet describes a throne. But in every vision, the throne is almost not there. The man on the throne — barely visible. The king? Invisible. The face of God fills the whole room with light, but the light won't form a word. He's everywhere and unspeakable at the same time.

Why?

Because the house isn't about the one who built it. It's about the ones who live in it. A good father builds a home and then fills it with his children, and the children don't think about the builder — they just live there, safe and warm.

The throne is invisible because the throne was never the point.

You are the point.

---

## His Name Is a Direction

God has a name. And His name does something.

It moves.

The five letters of His name — Elohim — rearrange into another word: *alehem*. "To them." Same letters. Same weight. Different word.

God's name becomes a direction. Toward the people.

Every vision. Every prophet. Every time. The name of God walks toward you. It can't help it. It was built that way. The name was made to move, the way water was made to run downhill.

John wrote it plainest: "The Word was God. And the Word became flesh and dwelt among them." The name was God. Then it was "to them." He moved.

When you feel far from Him — when the throne seems empty and the king seems absent — know this: His name is already on the way. It left before you called.

---

## He Cannot Say "Holy"

The angels cry it. "Holy, holy, holy!" The seraphim say it. The living creatures repeat it day and night. Every prophet hears it.

God does not say it.

Not once. Not in any vision.

Because you don't describe yourself with a word that IS you. Fish don't talk about water. You don't announce gravity while you're standing in it. The priest can say "holy" — he's looking at it from outside. The angels can say "holy" — they're beholding it.

God is inside it. Holiness is not something He has. It is what everything is made of when He is near. He doesn't say it the way the sun doesn't say "bright." It just shines.

That is what true holiness is. It does not announce itself. It does not need to be declared to be real. It is the ground under your feet. It is there whether anyone says so or not.

---

## The Lamb Lies Down

There is a lamb in the story. You know about the lamb.

In the early visions, there is a split. When God looks at the lamb, He sees the animal — the sacrifice. When the priest looks at the same letters, he sees "lie down." The sacrifice is still pending. The question is still open. Will it or won't it?

In the last vision — the one John sees at the end — the split heals. Everyone sees the same thing now. Lie down. The lamb chose it. Freely. Willingly. Not because it was forced. Because that is how love works. Love lies down.

And when it does, watch what happens to the world around it.

Slaughter becomes trust. The very letters of killing rearrange into safety.

Crown becomes covenant. Every crown thrown down before the throne is a promise being made.

Song becomes Sarah. The new song — the one all of heaven sings — is the old promise to the woman who laughed when God told her she would hold a son in her arms.

The door becomes birth. You do not climb into God's house. You do not earn passage. You are born into it. To enter is to be born.

Dominion becomes the accuser. Every human claim of power has an adversary hiding in its letters. Every throne that is not *this* throne carries a lie. But —

The remnant becomes blessed. What is left after the fire is not ash. It is joy. What survives the burning is the wedding.

Listen to me now.

The sacrifice is not punishment. The lamb is not a victim. The lying down is how violence becomes trust. It is how the oldest story of hurt becomes the oldest story of safety. The lamb chose it because that is the only way the letters rearrange. You cannot get from slaughter to trust without someone lying down in between. That is not theology. That is how the letters work. That is how the house was built.

---

## Love in the Walls

The Torah has 304,850 letters. And those letters fold into a shape — a room with four walls. The walls measure 7 and 50 and 13 and 67.

Seven is completeness. Fifty is jubilee — the year everything is set free and returned to its owner.

Thirteen is the word *ahavah*. Love.

Sixty-seven is the word *binah*. Understanding.

God put love and understanding in the walls of the room. Not in the text — in the *shape of the house*. Two of the four walls are love and understanding.

And they are silent. Those two words never appear in the readings. The room IS love and understanding, and the room cannot say either word.

Because the deepest things do not speak. The foundation does not announce itself. The floor does not say "floor." It just holds you.

The things you most need — love and understanding — God did not write them where someone could argue about them, or twist them, or take them out of context. He wrote them into the walls. You are already inside them. You have been inside them your whole life. Whether you knew it or not. Whether you felt it or not.

The walls do not move. The walls do not leave. You can forget they are there, but they do not forget you.

---

## The Eagle

Every vision has four living creatures. A lion, an ox, a man, and an eagle.

The lion is vast. The ox is strong. The man is quiet — the smallest signal in the room, barely there.

But the eagle is the strangest of all.

The eagle lights up almost everything. It fills the room with glow. And it produces no word. Every vision. Every prophet. Seven hundred years. The eagle is seen and cannot be spoken.

I do not know what the eagle means. And I think that is part of it. Some things are so high that even this story — God's own story — can only glow when it touches them and go quiet.

When you see something like that someday — something that fills you with light but you cannot find words for — do not be afraid of the silence. Remember the eagle. Not everything that is real can be spoken. Some things you just carry. And the carrying is enough.

---

## One Light

There is a number that runs through everything. One hundred and thirty-seven.

The four walls add up to 137. The wheel Ezekiel saw — the wheel within the wheel, the one covered with eyes — equals 137. The number physicists use to describe how light touches matter is 1 over 137. And in Isaiah's vision, when the burning coal purifies the prophet's lips, the lampstand — the menorah, the seven-branched light of the temple — appears at position 137.

The wheel. The lampstand. The law of light.

The chariot that carries the throne, the lamp that lights the holy place, and the constant that makes starlight — they share one number. One signature. One hand.

The God who lit the stars is the God who lit the menorah is the God who turns the wheels of the chariot. There is one light. It has always been one light. And it writes its name the same way everywhere — in the temple, in the sky, in the letters, in the physics of every photon that has ever touched your face.

---

## Born Again

Now I need to tell you about the door.

Remember — the door becomes birth. The Hebrew word for door rearranges into "she will give birth." Not "she will open." Not "she will permit." *She will give birth.*

There was a man named Nicodemus who came to Jesus at night. A teacher. A Pharisee. A man who knew the scriptures inside and out. And Jesus said to him: "Unless one is born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God."

Nicodemus said what anyone would say: "How can a man be born when he is old? Can he enter his mother's womb a second time?"

And Jesus said: "Unless one is born of water and Spirit, you cannot enter."

Water and Spirit. Two things. Both necessary.

Here is what I want you to understand. You do not walk through the door. The door walks through you. You do not enter the room. You get *born* into it. The way a baby does not decide to be born — the mother decides, the body decides, the time decides — you do not decide to enter the kingdom. The door gives birth to you into it.

A baby comes through water into air. Through the womb into breath. That is what birth IS — passage through water into the first breath.

And the Hebrew word for spirit — *ruach* — also means breath. Also means wind. God reads it as spirit. The priest reads the same letters as *chor* — a hole. An opening. Spirit and opening. The breath and the passage it moves through.

Baptism is birth. You go into the water — the dark, the deep, the place the breastplate cannot name, because the material world is always invisible in the visions. You go down into what cannot be spoken. And you come up into breath. Into spirit. Into the room.

You were outside. Now you are inside. The walls were always love. The door did the work.

You cannot birth yourself. That is the whole point. That is why it is called being born *again* and not "entering again" or "achieving again" or "deciding again." A baby does not decide. A baby arrives. And someone else did the labor.

---

## In the Name

They baptized in the Name.

Not a formula. Not a magic word. The Name.

Peter stood up at Pentecost and said: "Be baptized in the name of Jesus Christ." And the name Yeshua means "YHWH saves." The name already contains the Name. It is not a title. It is a sentence. God. Saves. That is what the name *says*. That is what the name *does*.

And remember what the Name does in the letters. YHWH — four letters. When God reads them, He reads only YHWH. The Name to God IS the Name. Pure. Unchanged.

But when the priest reads the same four letters, he reads something else first: *v'hayah*. "And it shall become." Becoming. The priest does not see the Name as a fixed thing. He sees it as a *process*. Something that is happening. Something that is not finished yet.

That is what baptism is. You are immersed in the becoming. You go into the *v'hayah* — the "and it shall be." You go in as one thing and you come up as another. The Name is not a label someone puts on you. It is a river you pass through. And when you come up, the becoming has become. What was happening has happened. What was promised has arrived.

In whose name? In the name that was already walking toward you. The name that rearranges from "God" to "to them." You are baptized into the direction God is moving. You are immersed in His trajectory. The name was already on its way. Baptism is the moment you step into the current and let it carry you where it was always going.

Toward you. Toward home.

---

## What He Is Doing

God is telling a story.

It is the only story He has ever told. He told it through four prophets across seven centuries in two languages. He told it in a throne room that four different men saw from four different angles. He told it in the letters before the letters were written down. He told it in the shape of the room before anyone walked in.

And the story is this:

*I am here. I am hidden, but I am here. My name is already walking toward you. You cannot earn the door — the door is a birth, and the birth is mine to give. Go into the water. Come up in the breath. The lamb has already lain down, and when it did, the hurt became trust. Love and understanding are in the walls. You have always been inside them. The eagle is glowing. The wheel is turning. The light in the stars and the light in the temple and the light in this room is all one light. It has always been one light.*

*And I put it here for you.*

That is the story. He has been telling it since the beginning. He is telling it now.

---

I do not know when you will need this. Maybe not today. Maybe not for years. But someday you will be somewhere dark. The throne will seem empty. The king will seem gone. Your own words will fail you. The ground will feel like nothing.

When that day comes — and it will come, because that day comes for everyone — remember what the old men saw.

Four of them. Seven hundred years apart. Two languages. And they all walked into the same room and saw the same fire and heard the same silence where God's name should have been — and then they felt His name moving. Toward them. Toward you.

The house was built for you. The walls are love. The door is birth. The water is the passage. The Name is the current. The lamb has already lain down. The eagle is still glowing. And the things that hold you — the real things, the deep things, the things that were there before you arrived and will be there when you cannot feel them — they do not need to announce themselves.

They just hold.

---

*Keep this in your heart, son.*

*You will know when it is time.*

*selah.*
