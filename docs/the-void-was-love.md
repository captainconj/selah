# The Void Was Love

*Before creation, there was void. The void's gematria is 13. Love's gematria is 13. We built a machine that reads the Torah in every direction the geometry defines. The void's fibers find love everywhere.*

---

## The Number

The second verse of the Torah: "The earth was formless and **void**."

**בהו** — void, chaos, emptiness. Three Hebrew letters: bet, he, vav. Their gematria sum: 2 + 5 + 6 = **13**.

**אהבה** — love. Aleph, he, bet, he. Their sum: 1 + 5 + 2 + 5 = **13**.

**אחד** — one. Aleph, chet, dalet. Their sum: 1 + 8 + 4 = **13**.

The void, love, and oneness are the same number in Hebrew. This is not something we chose. It is something the letters say.

---

## The Machine

The Torah has 304,850 Hebrew letters. This number factors as 2 × 5 × 5 × 7 × 13 × 67. When you fold the letter stream into a four-dimensional box of dimensions 7 × 50 × 13 × 67, each letter gets a coordinate in four-dimensional space.

In this box, there are 40 unique directions — axes, diagonals, and body diagonals. Each direction is a straight line through the text. A different reading. A different path through the same letters.

We built an engine that searches for any Hebrew word along every direction. When it finds the word, it tells us which Torah word each letter lands inside. If the ב of בהו lands at position 3,317, and that position is inside the word בגן ("in the garden"), then the void's first letter is hosted by the garden.

We call these paths **fibers**. Each fiber carries two messages: the word it spells and the words it passes through.

## Reproduction

These findings are fully reproducible. The code:

```clojure
(require '[selah.search :as s] '[selah.fiber :as f])
(s/build!)  ;; indexes 304,850 letters, 80,474 words

;; Garden → expulsion → exile
(let [hits (s/find-word [7 50 13 67] "בהו")]
  (f/print-fiber (first (filter #(some (fn [tw] (and tw (= (:word tw) "בגן")))
                                        (:torah-words %))
                                (f/non-surface hits)))))

;; Flesh → loved → firstborn
(let [hits (s/find-word [7 50 13 67] "בהו")]
  (f/print-fiber (first (filter #(some (fn [tw] (and tw (= (:word tw) "אהב")))
                                        (:torah-words %))
                                (f/non-surface hits)))))

;; The love command
(let [hits (s/find-word [7 50 13 67] "בהו")]
  (f/print-fiber (first (filter #(some (fn [tw] (and tw (= (:word tw) "ואהבת")))
                                        (:torah-words %))
                                (f/non-surface hits)))))
```

The search engine indexes the Torah once, then any word can be searched in any decomposition. Every fiber's coordinates, host words, and verses are reported. The machine counts. The reader interprets.
