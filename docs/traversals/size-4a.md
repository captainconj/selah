## Basin of תשמעו — "you-shall-hear"

**GV = 816.** Letters: ת ש מ ע ו.

| Word | Meaning | → |
|------|---------|---|
| **תשמעו** | **you-shall-hear / you-shall-obey** | **FIXED** |
| ושמעת | and-you-heard / and-you-obeyed | → תשמעו |
| מעשות | from-doing / deeds | → תשמעו |
| ותשמע | and-she-heard / and-you-heard | → תשמעו |

**Interpretation.**

Deeds (מעשות — from עשה, "to do") flow to hearing. What you do walks to what you hear. The Torah's great command is שמע — hear, obey, do. The basin reverses the expected sequence: doing comes first, and it resolves to hearing. You arrive at obedience through action, not the other way around. Na'aseh v'nishma — "we will do and we will hear" (Ex 24:7). Israel got the order right.

Every form of hearing collapses into the plural command: you (all) shall hear. The personal "and you heard" becomes the communal imperative. Hearing is never solitary.

**Interpretation.**

Nine (תשעה — tish'ah) flows to doing. The number before completion walks to action. Nine cannot rest — it must DO something to become ten. The incompleteness of nine IS the urgency of making.

This basin is the singular sibling of the size-5 basin תעשו ("you shall do," pl., GV=776). One letter apart, one value apart: the individual command and the communal command, parallel basins.

His horsemen (פרשיו — "his riders," from פרש, "horseman") flow to burning. The cavalry walks to the fire. Pharaoh's horsemen pursued Israel into the sea (Ex 14:23) and were consumed. Elijah was taken up by chariots of fire (2 Kings 2:11). The horsemen always burn.

And interpretation (also פרש — "to explain, to make distinct," as in Neh 8:8, "they read in the book distinctly and gave the sense") flows to burning. To explain IS to burn. Clarity is combustion.
