## Basin of סכתה — "toward-Succoth"

**GV = 485.** Letters: ס כ ת ה.

| Word | Meaning | → |
|------|---------|---|
| **סכתה** | **toward-Succoth (Gen 33:17)** | **FIXED** |
| כסתה | she-covered / her-covering | → סכתה |
| תכסה | it-shall-cover | → סכתה |
| הסכת | be-silent / listen (Deut 27:9) | → סכתה |

**Interpretation.**

Covering (כסתה, תכסה — "she covered," "it shall cover," from כסה) flows to Succoth — the place of booths, the temporary shelters. The sukkah IS a covering. The festival of Sukkot commands you to dwell under a covering that does not fully cover. Rain gets in. Stars show through. The basin gathers all covering into the booth.

"Be silent" (הסכת — hasket, Deut 27:9, "Be silent and hear, O Israel") flows to Succoth. Silence walks to the booth. The sukkah is the place where you stop talking and listen. Moses commands silence, and it resolves to the fragile shelter where you hear the wind.

Building (ובנית — "and you built / and you shall build," from בנה) flows to daughters. To build IS to produce daughters. The Hebrew pun is ancient: בנה (to build) and בן/בת (son/daughter) share a root. God built (ויבן) the rib into a woman (Gen 2:22). Every construction is a birth.

"Our houses" (בתינו — "our houses/our daughters") flows to his daughters. The house IS the daughter. The bayit (house) contains the bat (daughter). The dwelling and the offspring share a basin because they share a truth: your house is your children.

Nebaioth (נביות — Ishmael's firstborn son, Gen 25:13, the ancestor of an Arabian tribe) flows to daughters. The son of the exile walks to the daughters of the father. Ishmael's line converges with the building of the household.

Raamah (ורעמה — a descendant of Cush, Gen 10:7, a trading people) flows here. The merchant walks to the neighbor. Commerce creates community.

Overtaking (וישג — "and he reached / and he overtook," from נשג/השג, "to attain, to overtake") flows to approaching. The pursuit resolves to arrival. To chase IS to draw near. Reaching and approaching share a basin because they share a destination.

Erring (ישגו — "they shall go astray," from שגה) also flows here — the same pattern as the size-4 basin תשגו (GV=709). Approaching and erring share letters again. The convergence is structural.

"And she drew near" (וקרבה) flows here too. The feminine approach converges on the masculine hiphil command. She came close, and it became: bring near. The basin enacts the sacrifice.

**Interpretation.**

The uncircumcised (הערל — ha-arel, "the uncircumcised one," as in 1 Sam 17:26, David's taunt: "who is this uncircumcised Philistine?") flows to evil. The foreskin walks to harm. ערלה (foreskin/uncircumcision, Gen 17:11, "you shall circumcise the flesh of your foreskin") flows to "for evil." The covenant sign and its absence share a basin with harm. What is not cut off becomes what harms.

But the attractor is לרעה — "for evil." Joseph's brothers meant it for evil (Gen 50:20). And God meant it for good. The basin converges on the evil, not the good. The foreskin, the harming, the intent to do evil — they all arrive at "for evil." The good is not in this basin. The good is elsewhere. "You meant it for evil — God meant it for good" — two different words, two different basins. The evil gathers itself here. The good is a different attractor entirely.
