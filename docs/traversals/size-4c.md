## Basin of בקר — "cattle"

**GV = 302.** Letters: ב ק ר.

| Word | Meaning | → |
|------|---------|---|
| **בקר** | **cattle / herd / morning** | **FIXED** |
| קרב | battle / drawing-near / innards | → בקר |
| קבר | grave / burial | → בקר |
| ברק | lightning | → בקר |

**Interpretation.**

Three letters. Cattle, battle, grave, lightning. The grave (קבר) flows to the herd. Burial walks to the morning — for בקר is both "cattle" and "morning" (from the same root, the breaking open of day). The grave walks to dawn. Death walks to morning. This is the resurrection basin.

Lightning (ברק — Barak, the general who fought with Deborah, Judg 4:6) flows to cattle. The flash walks to the herd. And battle (קרב — qerav, also "drawing near," the same root as קרבן, "offering") flows here. To draw near, to fight, to die, to flash — all converge on the morning herd. The offering IS the morning.

The well (ביאר — be'er, also "he explained," from באר, "to make clear," Deut 1:5) flows to the Mighty One. Explanation walks to strength. Clarity IS power. And Beeri (בארי — "my well," Esau's Hittite father-in-law) flows here. The foreign well walks to Jacob's Mighty One. The alien father walks to the God of Israel.
