## Basin of עמודיו — "his-pillars"

**GV = 136.** Letters: ע מ ו ד י.

| Word | Meaning | → |
|------|---------|---|
| **עמודיו** | **his-pillars** | **FIXED** |
| ועמדיו | and-his-standings / and-his-positions | → עמודיו |
| ועמודי | and-pillars-of | → עמודיו |
| ויעמדו | and-they-stood | → עמודיו |

**Interpretation.**

Standing (ויעמדו — "and they stood," as Israel stood at Sinai, as the Levites stood to minister) flows to pillars. The act of standing resolves to architecture. What people do with their bodies becomes what Solomon builds for the Temple. Jachin and Boaz, the two pillars (1 Kings 7:21), are standing made permanent.

Every form converges on "his pillars" — the possessive. The pillars belong to someone. The tabernacle's pillars held up the curtains. Solomon's pillars held up the porch. The basin gathers every act of standing into one man's columns.

## Basin of והוציא — "and-he-brought-out"

**GV = 118.** Letters: ו ה צ י א.

| Word | Meaning | → |
|------|---------|---|
| **והוציא** | **and-he-brought-out (hiphil)** | **FIXED** |
| ויוצאה | and-she-was-brought-out | → והוציא |
| הוציאו | bring-him-out! / they-brought-out | → והוציאו |
| ויצאהו | and-he-brought-him-out | → והוציא |

**Interpretation.**

This basin is pure exodus in the causative — every member is a form of יצא in the hiphil, "to cause to go out, to bring forth." The size-4a basin ותצא (GV=497) gathered the simple going-out; this basin gathers the making-go-out. Someone is always being brought out by someone else. The exodus requires an agent.

The passive feminine (ויוצאה — "and she was brought out," as Tamar was brought out to be burned, Gen 38:25) flows to the active masculine "and he brought out." She was brought out, and it resolves to he who brought. The one extracted and the extractor share a basin. Exodus is never solitary — the size-4a basin said "she went out"; this one says "he brought her out."

Love's destination is the tent — the chuppah, the bridal canopy, the tent of meeting. אהב (love) walks to אהל (tent) because the letters are nearly the same. One letter apart: to love and to dwell.

"To her father" (לאביה — as in Judg 19:3, "her father's house") flows to the tents. The daughter returns to the tents. And "to bring in" (להביא — the causative infinitive, as in Ex 36:6, "the people were restrained from bringing") flows here. Every bringing-in walks to the tent. The tent IS the destination of every importation.

And at GV = 8, the lowest attractor in this set, everything that enters arrives at love.
