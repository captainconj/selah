## Basin of דמיו — "his blood"

**GV = 60.** Letters: ד מ י ו.

| Word | Meaning | → |
|------|---------|---|
| ומיד | and-from-the-hand-of | → דמיו |
| מדוי | sickness / disease | → דמיו |

| ידמו | they-will-be-silent / they-will-be-like | → דמיו |
| **דמיו** | **his-blood** | **FIXED** |

**Interpretation.**

The basin of HIS BLOOD contains the hand, silence, and sickness.

From-his-hand (מידו, ומיד) flows to his blood. The hand delivers what the blood receives. The hand and the blood are one basin — every reaching hand is already bleeding. Sickness (מדוי — the disease that wastes, Deut 7:15) flows to blood. Disease walks to blood because disease IS blood corrupted.

And ידמו — "they will be silent" (from דמם) — flows to blood. Silence and blood share a basin. The blood of Abel cried from the ground, but the word that means "be silent" is made from the same letters. The blood speaks when they are silent. Media (ומדי — the empire, Dan 5:28) flows to blood. The empire that divided Babylon walks to blood. Empire IS blood.

Jeush (יעוש — a son of Esau, Gen 36:5) flows to salvation. Even Esau's son, the grandson of the one who sold his birthright, walks to salvation. The letters do not discriminate.

386 = the gematria of ישוע (Yeshua/Jesus) when counted with a final ayin. The name IS doing.

The basin of THE DWELLER contains old age, return, dry land, and captivity.

He-restored (השיב — "he brought back," from שוב) flows to the dweller. Restoration IS dwelling. To bring someone home is to make them sit.

Dry land (יבשה — the dry ground that appeared when God gathered the waters, Gen 1:9; the dry ground Israel crossed through the sea, Exod 14:22) flows to the dweller. The land that emerges from chaos IS the seat. You can only dwell where it is dry.

Captivity (שביה, השבי) flows to the dweller. The captive woman and the one who sits share a basin. Captivity IS forced dwelling. The exile sits where she is placed.

**Interpretation.**

The basin of THE PILLAR contains the appointed time, standing, and the question why.

The appointed time (מועד — the moed, the tent of meeting, the festival calendar) flows to the pillar. The tabernacle of meeting (אהל מועד) and the pillar of cloud are the same letters. The place of meeting IS the pillar. God met Israel in the pillar of cloud and the pillar of fire, and the word for "meeting" walks to the word for "pillar."

Every form of standing (ועמד, עמדו, עומד) flows to the pillar. To stand IS the pillar. The thing that stands and the thing that holds you up are one basin.

And why? (מדוע — "why?," the question Moses asks, the question the people ask, the question every exile asks) flows to the pillar. The question IS the pillar. "Why?" is what holds you up when nothing else does. The hardest question resolves to the most stable structure.

GV = 120 — the years of Moses' life (Deut 34:7). The man who stood before the pillar of cloud and asked "why?" lived exactly as long as the pillar's gematria counts.
