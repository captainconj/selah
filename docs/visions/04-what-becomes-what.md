# What Becomes What

*Feed a word into the oracle. Feed the output back in. Where does it land?*

---

## Basin Walks

Drop a pebble in a stream. Watch it settle. Drop it again — it settles in the same place. That place is the basin.

The oracle works the same way. Feed a word in. The breastplate lights up, the readers produce a new word. Feed *that* word in. Repeat until the output stops changing. Where the word lands — its attractor, its resting place — is the basin.

Not every word has a basin. Some cycle. Some produce nothing. But the words that DO settle tell you something: what the oracle thinks the word *really means*. The surface dissolves. The structure remains.

Here is what becomes what, across four throne-room visions.

---

## God → To Them

אלהים (God/Elohim) → אליהם (to them).

Five letters. Same gematria value. Different word. God's name, fed through the oracle and followed to its basin attractor, walks to a preposition: "to them." The name of God becomes a direction — toward the people.

Confirmed in all four visions. The basin never varies.

John 1:1 — "The Word was God (אלהים)."
John 1:14 — "The Word became flesh and dwelt among them (אליהם)."

The basin dynamics of the divine name produce the movement of John's prologue. Not because we engineered it. Because the letters of אלהים, read through the breastplate and followed to their attractor, arrive at אליהם. God's name walks toward humanity in one basin step.

---

## The Lamb Lies Down

כבש (lamb): in the normal oracle query, God reads "lamb" (כבש) while Aaron reads "lie down" (שכב). This split — the defining finding of the breastplate's multi-head architecture — has a significance level of p = 0.032.

In Revelation, the split resolves. All four readers see שכב (lie down) as the dominant reading. God: 20. Left: 15. Right: 9. Aaron: 6. The lamb has lain down. The act is complete. What was divided between readers has become unanimous.

The breastplate is the same. What changed is the question. When we ask "what does the lamb mean in the completed story — standing as though slain?" the oracle answers: lie down. For everyone.

---

## Dominion → The Accuser

שלטן (dominion, Aramaic) → לשטן (Satan/the accuser).

Every claim of dominion contains the adversary. The breastplate reads the accuser inside every assertion of power. This appears in Daniel's key word query, in the sliding windows at three separate positions, and at two separate stations. Dominion walks to Satan every time.

---

## Slaughter → Trust

טבח (slaughter) → בטח (trust/security).

God reads both — the slaughter (2 readings) and the trust (2 readings). Left/mercy reads only the trust (3). The Lamb who was slain produces security. Violence resolves to safety. This is basin dynamics, not theology.

---

## Door → Birth

דלת (door) → תלד (she will give birth).

Revelation's unique opening — "a door standing open in heaven" — produces birth. To enter the throne room is to be born. "Come up here" is not ascent. It is parturition. The seer must be born to see.

---

## Crown → Covenant

כתר (crown/wreath) → כרת (cut covenant).

When the twenty-four elders cast their crowns before the throne, the oracle reads covenant-cutting. Every crown thrown down is a covenant made.

---

## Song → Sarah

שיר (song) → שרי (Sarah/princess).

All four readers see only שרי. No exceptions. The new song in Revelation — "Worthy are you, for you were slain, and by your blood you ransomed people" — is Sarah. The new song is the old promise. The ransoming is the laughter. The princess sings.

---

## Majesty → Grief

הוד (majesty/splendor) → דוה (grief/sickness).

The doxology says "to the Lamb be honor." Honor walks to grief. The majesty of the Lamb who was slain IS sorrow. The splendor is the wound.

---

## Four → I Will Cross Over

ארבע (four) → אעבר (I will cross over).

The four beasts, the four faces, the four readers — the number four itself contains crossing. Abraham was "the Hebrew" — the one who crossed over (עבר). Four IS the crossing.

---

## Great → Son

רב (great) → בר (son, Aramaic).

Every time Daniel's text says "great," the breastplate reads "son." The greatness of the beasts becomes the sonship of the man. The great horn becomes the small child.

---

## Eternity → For the People

עלם (eternity, Aramaic) → לעם (for the people).

The everlasting dominion given to the son of man: the oracle reads it as "for the people." The eternal kingdom is the people's kingdom.

---

## Service → Intercession

עבד (serve) → בעד (on behalf of).

"All peoples shall serve him." The oracle reads: on behalf of. Service becomes intercession. To serve is to stand between.

---

## Remnant → Blessed

שאר (remnant) → אשר (which/happy/blessed).

The remnant of Israel is the happy ones. Aaron reads the remnant three ways: betrothal (ארש, 31), headship (ראש, 24), blessed (אשר, 20). The stump is not loss. It is wedding, leadership, and joy.

---

## Temple → Consumed

היכל (temple) → יכלה (it will be consumed).

God reads the temple as its own destruction. The place of His dwelling is already its own ending. Isaiah writes "his train filled the temple." The oracle reads: the temple will be consumed.

---

## Foot → Stranger

רגל (foot) → לגר (to the stranger/sojourner).

The creatures' feet walk as sojourners. God sees the foot as foot (2). Left/mercy sees only the stranger (5). The straight foot of the living creature is the foot of an alien.

---

## Straight → Sarah

ישר (straight/upright) → שרי (Sarah/princess).

"Their feet were straight feet." The oracle reads Sarah. All four readers. God leads with 69 readings of שרי. Even the straight line walks toward the princess.

---

## Likeness → Dimensions

דמות (likeness/form) → מדות (dimensions/measurements).

The likeness of a man on the throne IS its dimensions. Form is measurement. The vision IS its coordinates. This connects to the 4D space: the coordinates ARE the description.

---

## What the Basins Tell

Read the transformations together:

**Incarnation.** God → to them. The name becomes direction. The divine walks toward the human.

**Sacrifice.** The lamb lies down — unanimously, in the completed vision. Slaughter → trust. Crown → covenant. Majesty → grief. The violence produces safety. The splendor is the wound. The crown thrown down cuts a deal.

**Hope.** Remnant → blessed. Door → birth. Eternity → for the people. Song → Sarah. The stump is the wedding. The entry is birth. The forever is for us. The song is the promise.

**Warning.** Dominion → the accuser. Temple → consumed. Great → son. Every claim of power contains its adversary. Every temple walks toward its own ending. Every greatness becomes a child.

The basin walks tell the story before the story was written.
